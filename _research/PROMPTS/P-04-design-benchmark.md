PASTE FROM HERE:

I'm designing a dark-themed, research-grade public markets-intelligence website (think a public Bloomberg/Koyfin desk with FT/Economist editorial voice). I want a benchmark study of how the best financial/editorial products handle dark-UI design, to pressure-test my system. With screenshots/links where possible:

1. **Dark-theme data UIs done well:** Koyfin, TradingView, Bloomberg terminal/web, Stratechery/paid newsletters dark modes, FT/Economist dark variants, Kalshi/Polymarket. For each: canvas value range, card separation approach (hairline vs tint vs shadow), accent discipline (how many accent hues), and how they keep dense tables readable on dark.
2. **Typography on dark:** documented best practices for serif body text on near-black (size/line-height/weight adjustments vs light mode), and who actually ships serif body on dark successfully.
3. **Dual-audience patterns:** products that publish the same intelligence at two depths (e.g., "simple/pro" toggles, explainer twins). What works, what fails, retention evidence if any.
4. **Skim/expand reading models:** progressive-disclosure patterns in long-form analysis (details/summary, "TL;DR + deep", collapsible sections) — accessibility and SEO side-effects.
5. **Status/confidence display:** how serious research products display confidence levels and data-freshness honestly (not fake-live). Examples of "honest placeholder" patterns for missing data.
6. **Chart thumbnails in editorial layouts:** who embeds small illustrative charts inline (FT? Axios? Chartr?) and at what sizes/aspect ratios.

Conclude with the 10 most actionable takeaways ranked by relevance to a one-accent-hue, hairline-separated, dark editorial system. Cite everything.
