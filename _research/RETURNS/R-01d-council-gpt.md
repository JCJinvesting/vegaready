# GPT-5.5 — Equities Desk Part F: Extensibility & Equities-Specific Framework Edges

## Framing: durability across regimes

An equities desk is durable across regimes when it treats equity index price as the last expression of a cross-asset balance-sheet system, not as a self-contained earnings-discounting machine; this framing is especially useful in funding, energy, and geopolitical shocks because liquidity, margin, dealer capacity, and factor crowding can reprice before bottom-up EPS models are changed ([FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The working hierarchy I would use is: first, identify whether the catalyst impairs funding or collateral; second, map the first-round equity expression through size, liquidity, quality, beta, and sector input-cost exposure; third, wait for earnings revisions only after the commodity, freight, claims, or appropriations channel is measurable in company guidance or consensus systems ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: well-established]. The practical edge is to separate “shock-to-price” from “shock-to-EPS,” because the March 2020 episode had forced selling, margin calls, MMF outflows, Treasury-market depth collapse, and S&P 500 volatility extremes before the earnings path was knowable with precision ([FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

## 1. Factor/style rotation under a funding-stress catalyst

The most repeatable first repricing in funding-stress episodes is small/illiquid underperformance versus large/liquid stocks, because funding stress raises the shadow price of liquidity and balance-sheet capacity before it proves an earnings recession; the Fama-French daily SMB factor was -7.14% in Aug.–Sep. 1998, -9.18% in Sep.–Nov. 2008, -9.29% in Jul.–Oct. 2011, -7.53% in Feb.–Mar. 2020, and -5.54% in the Mar. 2023 SVB window in my calculations from the public Fama-French daily five-factor file ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: well-established]. Quality/profitability generally reprices early when the shock is funding-led, because balance-sheet durability and operating profitability become scarce; Fama-French RMW was +13.18% in Sep.–Nov. 2008, +9.19% in Jul.–Oct. 2011, +4.19% during the UK LDI/gilt stress window, and +3.56% during the SVB stress window, while the COVID dash-for-cash was the exception at -2.90% because the initial liquidation was broad and indiscriminate ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html), [FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. Defensives versus cyclicals usually acts faster than EPS revisions in macro/funding shocks; using ETF proxies, defensive sectors XLP/XLU/XLV outperformed cyclical XLY/XLI/XLF/XLE by +21.25 percentage points in Sep.–Nov. 2008, +16.14 points in Jul.–Oct. 2011, +14.33 points in Feb.–Mar. 2020, and +7.00 points during the SVB window, based on Yahoo Finance adjusted prices for sector ETFs ([Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history)) [confidence: well-established]. Value versus growth is less stable as a first-round stress signal: Fama-French HML was +0.51% in Aug.–Sep. 1998, -7.47% in Sep.–Nov. 2008, -5.36% in Jul.–Oct. 2011, -17.92% in Feb.–Mar. 2020, +5.20% in the LDI/gilt window, and -7.68% in Mar. 2023, so the sign depends on whether financials/energy are the epicenter or the beneficiary ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: contested]. Low-volatility usually helps in classic equity drawdowns but can fail when rate duration is the shock; SPLV beat SPY by +10.07 points in Jul.–Oct. 2011, lagged SPY by -2.11 points in Feb.–Mar. 2020, lagged by -1.84 points during the LDI/gilt window, and was roughly flat versus SPY in the SVB window using ETF price histories ([Yahoo Finance SPLV history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPLV/history)) [confidence: contested].

Three analogues are most useful for desk playbooks. In the 1998 Russia/LTCM shock, Russia’s devaluation/default triggered a flight to safer and more liquid assets, LTCM lost 44% in August, and a 14-firm consortium injected $3.6 billion on 23 September 1998 to prevent disorderly liquidation; in that same Aug.–Sep. window, market beta was -10.86%, SMB was -7.14%, RMW was +2.28%, and CMA was +3.82% in the Fama-French daily data ([Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/ltcm-near-failure), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: well-established]. In the 2008 funding panic, Lehman filed on 15 September 2008, AIG received support the next day, and the Fed created liquidity programs for money-market funds and commercial paper; in Sep.–Nov. 2008, SPY fell -40.71%, IWM underperformed SPY by -7.01 points, defensives beat cyclicals by +21.25 points, and Fama-French RMW rose +13.18% ([Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/great-recession-and-its-aftermath), [Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: well-established]. In March 2020, the dash-for-cash included US prime MMF outflows of $125 billion, government/Treasury MMF inflows above $800 billion, record mid-March bond-fund outflows of $109 billion, a 12% S&P 500 one-day loss on 16 March, and a VIX peak of 83; in the 19 Feb.–23 Mar. window, Fama-French market beta fell -33.98%, SMB was -7.53%, HML was -17.92%, and defensive sectors beat cyclicals by +14.33 points ([FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html), [Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history)) [confidence: well-established].

## 2. Earnings-revision transmission

Consensus EPS revisions usually lag market-price factor rotation because sell-side estimates respond most strongly when public macro releases, commodity decks, company guidance, or industry-specific earnings surprises can be mapped into model line items; Agarwal and Hess find that analysts’ forecasts respond to macro announcements after controlling for firm and industry earnings surprises, with stronger reliance on macro news for cyclical industries and stronger effects in medium-term than current-year forecasts ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: well-established]. A realistic desk convention is “prices within hours/days, consensus within one to six weeks, annual EPS after guidance,” because analyst forecast revisions display momentum and underreaction, and post-forecast-revision drift is linked to serial correlation in individual analysts’ revisions ([Chen, Narayanamoorthy, Sougiannis, and Zhou SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2578757)) [confidence: well-established]. The Federal Reserve’s study of S&P 500 analyst forecast errors finds bottom-up analyst forecasts are largely uncorrelated with macro-based growth forecasts and that the macro-analyst gap predicts about 50% of current-quarter forecast errors and about 40% of quarter-ahead forecast errors, which is direct evidence that macro information is not fully embedded in consensus immediately ([Federal Reserve FEDS paper](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/files/2024049pap.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

By sector, energy EPS revisions have the shortest mechanical lag after an oil shock because spot and futures curves flow quickly into realized price decks, but consensus often waits for company hedging, production, and capex commentary before changing full-year EPS; this is why the desk should monitor the oil curve first and weekly/monthly earnings-revision breadth second ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: well-established]. Transports have a faster price reaction than EPS revision because fuel surcharges can be based on prior-week fuel prices and a rapid diesel/jet-fuel move creates a temporary mismatch before carriers can reprice contracts or fares; one logistics explainer describes weekly fuel-surcharge adjustment and a lag when fuel prices rise rapidly ([Direct Drive Logistics](https://www.directdrivelogistics.com/logisticsNews/LogisticsFuelCosts)) [confidence: anecdotal]. Airlines have a 30–90 day earnings-risk window after a jet-fuel spike because near-term tickets may have been sold before the fuel shock and fares cannot be retroactively repriced, according to public reporting of airline-analyst commentary ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/airfare-prices-jet-fuel-iran-war.html)) [confidence: anecdotal]. Insurers revise in two stages: short-tail catastrophe and auto/property claim costs can affect current-quarter EPS quickly, while long-tail lines take longer because claims may settle years later and inflation assumptions require reserve/pricing model changes ([BDO insurance inflation analysis](https://www.bdo.com/insights/industries/insurance/anticipating-future-claims-costs-the-impact-of-inflation-on-property-casualty-insurance)) [confidence: well-established]. Defense primes are slowest on EPS despite fast stock moves, because military-spending news first reduces uncertainty around future procurement cash flows and forecast dispersion, but recognized revenue depends on contracts, funding, backlog conversion, and ASC 606 performance obligations ([NBER war puzzle paper](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w29837/w29837.pdf), [Deloitte DART aerospace and defense revenue recognition](https://dart.deloitte.com/USDART/home/publications/deloitte/industry/aerospace-defense/revenue-recognition-asc-606-step-1)) [confidence: well-established]. Consumer EPS revisions after oil/food/shipping shocks are usually the most model-dependent, because analysts need evidence on pass-through, volumes, mix, and margin absorption, and the academic literature says analysts put more weight on macro information when the firms are more cyclical ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: contested].

## 3. Index plumbing: what is free versus vendor-only

Buyback blackout calendars are partially observable from free data because earnings dates are public and customary blackout periods are tied to the weeks before quarterly results, but the exact percentage of market capitalization in blackout and the live discretionary buyback bid are generally prime-broker or issuer-desk datasets; Skadden notes companies customarily institute blackout periods late in each quarter, and Harvard Law School Forum notes Rule 10b5-1 plans can permit repurchases during a closed window if pre-established ([Skadden](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2022/03/revisiting-share-repurchases-in-volatile-times), [Harvard Law School Forum](https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2025/08/14/structuring-share-repurchases-under-rules-10b-18-and-10b5-1/)) [confidence: well-established]. The free implementation is a qualitative blackout heat map built from public earnings calendars and SEC repurchase disclosures, while the vendor-only implementation is a daily estimate of authorized, active, blackout-adjusted corporate demand by index constituent and broker execution channel ([Yahoo Finance earnings calendar](https://finance.yahoo.com/calendar/earnings/), [Nasdaq earnings calendar](https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/earnings)) [confidence: contested].

CTA and systematic positioning are partially observable from free public data through CFTC COT/TFF and OFR aggregations, because the TFF report includes Dealer/Intermediary, Asset Manager/Institutional, Leveraged Funds, and Other Reportables categories and is generally released Friday at 3:30 p.m. ET for the prior Tuesday’s positions ([CFTC COT](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm)) [confidence: well-established]. The OFR Hedge Fund Monitor publishes leveraged-fund net notional exposure to major equity-index futures and identifies leveraged funds as including hedge funds, CTAs, CPOs, and certain unregistered funds, but it is still a weekly futures proxy rather than a complete real-time CTA model ([OFR Hedge Fund Monitor](https://www.financialresearch.gov/hedge-fund-monitor/categories/size/chart-90/)) [confidence: well-established]. Real-time CTA rebalancing thresholds, vol-control AUM, target-vol allocations, and dealer-client flow decomposition are vendor-only or desk-estimated because they require fund universes, AUM, leverage assumptions, intraday volatility inputs, and proprietary model calibration ([J.P. Morgan market data intelligence page](https://www.jpmorgan.com/markets/market-data-intelligence)) [confidence: well-established].

0DTE activity is partly observable from free sources because Cboe publishes SPX/XSP 0DTE education and market resources, Cboe has public market-statistics pages, and OCC provides volume, open interest, series, new listings, and put/call ratio resources ([Cboe 0DTE resources](https://www.cboe.com/en/tradable-products/0dte/), [OCC options data](https://www.optionseducation.org/referencelibrary/market-data), [Cboe options statistics](https://www.cboe.com/en/markets/us/options/market-statistics/)) [confidence: well-established]. Aggregate gamma exposure is less cleanly public: Barchart and SpotGamma offer public gamma-exposure pages or snapshots, but robust dealer gamma by strike, 0DTE customer type, and intraday hedging pressure require OPRA-level options data, trade classification, implied-vol surfaces, and dealer-position assumptions ([Barchart SPX gamma exposure](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$SPX/gamma-exposure), [SpotGamma SPX gamma exposure](https://spotgamma.com/free-tools/spx-gamma-exposure/)) [confidence: contested].

## 4. Breadth and internals: three diagnostic free metrics

The first diagnostic breadth metric is the percent of S&P 500 stocks above the 200-day moving average, because it distinguishes a durable index advance from a narrow mega-cap rally; Barchart publishes “S&P 500 Stocks Above 200-Day Average ($S5TH),” and StockCharts lists $SPXA200 and $SPXA200R breadth symbols for S&P 500 stocks above the 200-day average ([Barchart $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH), [StockCharts symbol catalog](https://stockcharts.com/freecharts/catalog/?sl=sa)) [confidence: well-established]. The update cadence is effectively daily/end-of-session for the signal even when quote display may be delayed, because the 200-day moving-average condition is based on daily closing prices, and Barchart states stocks data can be delayed by 15 minutes in its data-delay help page ([Barchart real-time vs delayed data](https://www.barchart.com/trader/help/layout/realtime_delayed.php)) [confidence: well-established].

The second diagnostic breadth metric is the equal-weight versus cap-weight S&P 500 ratio, because it measures whether average constituents are confirming index leadership; S&P describes the S&P 500 Equal Weight Index as the equal-weight version of the widely used S&P 500, while S&P describes the S&P 500 as 500 leading companies covering about 80% of available U.S. market capitalization ([S&P 500 Equal Weight Index](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500-equal-weight-index/), [S&P 500](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500/)) [confidence: well-established]. The free implementation is to chart RSP/SPY or SPXEW/SPX daily, because RSP is an ETF linked to the equal-weight index and SPY is an ETF linked to the cap-weighted S&P 500 ([Yahoo Finance RSP history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/RSP/history), [Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history)) [confidence: well-established].

The third diagnostic breadth metric is 52-week new highs minus new lows, because it detects whether stress is producing broad downside expansion or merely rotating leadership; Barchart publishes 52-week new-high and new-low pages for U.S. exchanges, and MarketInOut defines the New Highs-New Lows indicator as the daily difference between stocks reaching new 52-week highs and stocks reaching new 52-week lows ([Barchart highs-lows](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/highs-lows), [MarketInOut new highs-new lows](https://www.marketinout.com/technical_analysis.php?t=New_Highs-New_Lows&id=70)) [confidence: well-established]. The update cadence should be treated as intraday-to-daily for observation and end-of-day for signal confirmation, because new highs/lows are session extrema and delayed public quote feeds can make intraday readings less reliable ([Barchart real-time vs delayed data](https://www.barchart.com/trader/help/layout/realtime_delayed.php)) [confidence: well-established].

## 5. Cross-desk hooks: credit-to-equities lead/lag relationships

The strongest credit-to-equity hook is HY OAS as a risk-appetite and forward-return state variable, because FRED publishes the ICE BofA US High Yield OAS daily at close, and the series measures spreads between a below-investment-grade bond index and a spot Treasury curve ([FRED HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)) [confidence: well-established]. LSEG/FTSE Russell finds U.S. high-yield spreads have forecasted future HY returns well over the last two decades, with spread/future-return correlations of 0.69 at one year, 0.87 at two years, 0.88 at three years, and 0.86 at four years, while HY and Russell 1000 monthly returns had an 84% correlation over the five years to February 2023 and 92% over the final year of that sample ([LSEG/FTSE Russell](https://www.lseg.com/content/dam/ftse-russell/en_us/documents/research/valuation-matters-us-high-yield-and-equities.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The equity use is not “HY OAS predicts next week’s SPX point target,” but “HY OAS widening without equity breadth damage is an early-warning divergence, and HY OAS tightening after a shock is a necessary condition for durable equity beta re-risking” ([FRED HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2), [LSEG/FTSE Russell](https://www.lseg.com/content/dam/ftse-russell/en_us/documents/research/valuation-matters-us-high-yield-and-equities.pdf)) [confidence: contested].

The second hook is CDX/iTraxx versus VIX, because the VIX-credit relationship captures whether equity-option stress is being confirmed by traded credit risk; Figuerola-Ferretti and Paraskevopoulos find cointegration between VIX and CDS/iTraxx, predominant VIX price leadership, and both VIX and iTraxx values rising by about 400% over 2008 ([EFMA paper](https://www.efmaefm.org/0efmameetings/EFMA%20ANNUAL%20MEETINGS/2011-Braga/papers/0229.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The direction of price discovery is contested for credit versus equities, because OFR’s 2024 CDS paper notes that Acharya and Johnson find CDS-to-stock information flow, while Norden and Weber and Hilscher et al. find information runs from equities to CDS ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: contested]. The practical desk rule is to use VIX-CDX divergence as a cross-check rather than a single trigger, because VIX often leads credit-vol adjustment in broad risk shocks while CDS can lead in firm-specific credit-information events ([EFMA paper](https://www.efmaefm.org/0efmameetings/EFMA%20ANNUAL%20MEETINGS/2011-Braga/papers/0229.pdf), [OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: contested].

The third hook is credit-index price discovery versus cash bonds as a funding-stress confirmation signal, because OFR finds single-name CDS spreads led corporate bond spreads before UMR with a 1% CDS-spread increase associated with a 0.20% next-day bond-spread increase, while post-UMR the same relationship weakened to 0.11%; OFR also finds CDX index changes led corporate bond index changes across the sample and remained stable after UMR ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The equity page should therefore reference the credit desk when CDX or HY OAS moves ahead of equity breadth, because the credit desk owns the spread-quality, default, and cash-bond liquidity diagnosis while equities should translate the signal into beta, sector, and factor exposure ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

## 6. What would falsify the equities framework

Falsifier 1: If a funding-stress catalyst is identified at date T but by T+5 trading days HY OAS has not widened by at least 50 bps, VIX has not risen above 25 or by at least 8 points, and the S&P 500 percent above 200dma has not fallen by at least 5 percentage points, then the desk’s “funding shock first” regime read should be downgraded to an earnings or idiosyncratic shock; the observables are FRED HY OAS, FRED/Cboe VIX, and Barchart/StockCharts breadth ([FRED HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2), [FRED VIX](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/VIXCLS), [Barchart $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH)) [confidence: contested]. Falsifier 2: If by T+10 trading days small caps are outperforming large caps, equal weight is outperforming cap weight, and Fama-French SMB is positive while HY OAS remains wider, then the assumed liquidity preference for large/liquid equities is wrong for that episode; the observables are IWM/SPY, RSP/SPY, and Fama-French SMB ([Yahoo Finance IWM history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/IWM/history), [Yahoo Finance RSP history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/RSP/history), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: contested]. Falsifier 3: If by T+15 trading days defensives have not outperformed cyclicals and new lows are not expanding, then the shock is not being transmitted through macro risk aversion or funding stress as expected; the observables are XLP/XLU/XLV versus XLY/XLI/XLF/XLE and 52-week new highs-lows ([Yahoo Finance sector ETF histories](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/XLP/history), [Barchart highs-lows](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/highs-lows)) [confidence: contested]. Falsifier 4: If by the next weekly CFTC release after T+7 leveraged-fund equity-index futures exposure has not fallen and OFR/CFTC proxies do not show de-risking, then the CTA/vol-control deleveraging leg should be removed from the narrative; the observables are CFTC TFF and OFR leveraged-fund equity-index futures exposure ([CFTC COT](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm), [OFR Hedge Fund Monitor](https://www.financialresearch.gov/hedge-fund-monitor/categories/size/chart-90/)) [confidence: well-established]. Falsifier 5: If by the first full earnings-revision cycle after T, defined as the next four weekly consensus snapshots or the next reporting-season update, sector EPS revisions do not move in the predicted direction for the directly exposed sectors, then the catalyst is price-only rather than earnings-transmissive; this falsifier is most important for energy, transports, insurers, defense primes, and consumer sectors because analyst revisions can lag and underreact to macro information ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161), [Federal Reserve FEDS paper](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/files/2024049pap.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].
