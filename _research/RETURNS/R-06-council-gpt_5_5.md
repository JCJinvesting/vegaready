# R-06 — Fetchable sources for four pending credit tiles

## Executive synthesis

The only one of the four tiles for which I would publish a daily or intraday figure without waiting for a fund-manager PDF is CDX.NA.IG/HY, because CFTC Part 43 requires transaction-level swap pricing data to be made publicly available in a machine-readable form and DTCC exposes a public PPD API plus ZIP/CSV slice files for credit trades ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf), [DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).  The CDX figure should be labeled as “SDR trade prints, on-the-run series, last eligible print,” not as the official Markit/S&P end-of-day index level, because the SDR feed contains executed trades and amendments rather than a contributed composite close ([DTCC OTC Direct Connect](https://www.dtcc.com/data-services/market-aggregates-and-analytics/otc-direct-connect), [S&P CDX.NA.HY index page](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/fixed-income/cdxna-hy-5y-short-total-return-index)).

For the US HY distress ratio, I found no free programmatic source that publishes the canonical share of the broad US HY universe trading with spread-to-worst above 1,000 bp, but I did find reliable delayed publications and a computable ETF proxy using BlackRock/iShares holding-level yield-to-worst and price fields ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110), [BlackRock product-data API for HYG holdings](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=holdings&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239565&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG)).  The honest live label is “HYG ETF-derived distress proxy,” while the delayed canonical-ish label is “J.P. Morgan distress ratio as republished by Eaton Vance/Morgan Stanley, quarter-end” ([Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor Q3 2025](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q32025.pdf)).

For EMBI Global Diversified spread, I found no free official J.P. Morgan programmatic feed for the canonical sovereign spread, but SSGA’s EM debt commentary reliably republishes “EMBI GD Spread” as a current level in basis points and BlackRock’s EMB ETF page/API provides a daily portfolio option-adjusted spread that is a usable but noncanonical ETF proxy ([SSGA Emerging Market Debt Commentary](https://www.ssga.com/us/en/institutional/insights/emerging-market-debt-commentary-feb-2026), [BlackRock product-data API for EMB fundamentals](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=fundamentalsAndRisk&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239572&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHEMX&ticker=EMB)).  The honest live label is “EMB ETF OAS proxy, not EMBI Global Diversified,” while the delayed canonical label is “EMBI GD spread as published by SSGA/TCW, month-end” ([TCW Emerging Markets Income Fund characteristics](https://www.tcw.com/-/media/Downloads/com/Products/US-Funds/TCW-Funds/Monthly-Characteristics/CHAR-EIF.pdf/1000)).

For the Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan index, Morningstar’s public index page exposes the Loan 100 index level but not the broader US LLI level or spread, LSTA’s latest daily Loan 100 file page is member-gated, and Morgan Stanley/Eaton Vance monthly PDFs provide the broad Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan Index spread-to-3-year, average price, defaults, and market outstandings with a monthly lag ([Morningstar LSTA Loan 100 page](https://indexes.morningstar.com/indexes/details/morningstar-lsta-us-leveraged-loan-100-FS0000HS44), [LSTA daily index file page](https://www.lsta.org/content/daily-index-file/), [Morgan Stanley Eaton Vance Floating-Rate ETF monthly review](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/etf/monthlyreview_etf_eatonvancefloatingrate_us.pdf)).  The honest live label is “Morningstar LSTA Loan 100 public index level,” while the delayed broad-market label is “Morningstar LSTA US LL Index spread-to-3-year as republished by Morgan Stanley/Eaton Vance, month-end” ([Morningstar leveraged-loan methodology](https://indexes.morningstar.com/api/docs/69bd3e9250ca695336d4045f)).

## 1) US HY distress ratio

### Canonical definition and direct/delayed feeds

The canonical metric the desk described is the percentage of the US high-yield bond market trading at spread-to-worst greater than 1,000 bp, and Eaton Vance states this definition verbatim while sourcing the data to J.P. Morgan at quarter-end ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110)).  Morgan Stanley’s Q3 2025 High Yield Market Monitor uses the same J.P. Morgan wording, stating that the distress ratio measures the percent of high-yield bonds trading with spread-to-worst greater than 1,000 bp ([Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor Q3 2025](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q32025.pdf)).  Fridson/S&P commentary describes the longer-running “distress ratio” as the percentage of issues in the ICE BofAML US High Yield Index quoted at option-adjusted spread of at least 1,000 bp, which is definitionally close but provider/universe-specific rather than automatically identical to the J.P. Morgan spread-to-worst series ([S&P Global Market Intelligence](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2021/11/fridson-monthly-definitizing-the-definition-of-distress-67760867)).  FRED publishes the aggregate ICE BofA US High Yield Index option-adjusted spread, but FRED’s notes define it as the capitalization-weighted OAS of the overall below-investment-grade index and not as a count or par share of constituents above 1,000 bp ([FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)).

I did not find a free API/CSV/JSON endpoint that directly publishes the current canonical US HY distress ratio as a standalone time series.  The closest true-number delayed feed is the recurring Eaton Vance/Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor PDF path, whose fetch format is PDF text extraction at URLs such as `https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?...` and `https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q32025.pdf` ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110), [Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor Q3 2025](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q32025.pdf)).  The update cadence is quarterly in the examples I found, with quarter-end as-of dates shown in the source note and publication lag dependent on the manager’s posting schedule ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110)).  The units are percent of bonds or market par, depending on the publisher and source, so the tile must display the source’s exact definition rather than shortening it to “US HY distress ratio” without a methodology note ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110), [S&P Global Market Intelligence](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2021/11/fridson-monthly-definitizing-the-definition-of-distress-67760867)).  Fragility is medium for the manager PDFs because the PDF names change by quarter, but it is low for a site search or landing-page poll for `article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q*.pdf` under Morgan Stanley/Eaton Vance’s publication directories ([Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor Q3 2025](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q32025.pdf)).

### Regulatory public-dissemination channels

I found no regulatory public-dissemination channel that carries a US HY bond distress ratio.  FINRA TRACE does not publish synthetic CDS products, and the search result FAQ states that synthetic indices and credit-default swaps are not TRACE-eligible securities and are not reported to FINRA ([FINRA TRACE structured-products FAQ](https://www.finra.org/finra-data/browse-catalog/structured-product-activity-reports-and-tables/structured-product-reports-faq)).  CFTC swap data can be used for CDX index-CDS prints, but it does not include the cash-bond constituent spread distribution needed for a high-yield bond distress ratio ([CFTC Weekly Swaps Report](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/SwapsReports/index.htm), [DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).  OFR’s public FSI is a daily stress index made from multiple market variables and not a constituent-level HY distress measure ([OFR Financial Stress Index](https://www.financialresearch.gov/financial-stress-index/)).

### Computable ETF proxies from public holdings files

The best computable proxy is BlackRock’s HYG product-data API, because the `holdings` component returns holding-level fields including `holdingPercent`, `marketValue`, `parValue`, `unitPrice`, `yieldToMaturity`, `yieldToWorst`, `maturityDate`, `duration`, `modifiedDuration`, `assetClass`, `isin`, and `cusip` for HYG ([BlackRock product-data API for HYG holdings](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=holdings&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239565&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG)).  The API fetch format is JSON, and the relevant request is `GET https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?...component=holdings...portfolioId=239565...aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG` ([BlackRock product-data API for HYG holdings](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=holdings&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239565&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG)).  The stronger proxy formula is a market-value-weighted share of fixed-income holdings where `yieldToWorst - matched Treasury yield >= 10.00 percentage points`, but this is not canonical because it substitutes ETF holdings for the broad index and approximates spread-to-worst without provider OAS curves or full call analytics ([BlackRock product-data API for HYG holdings](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=holdings&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239565&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG)).  A more robust but less canonical proxy is a market-value-weighted share of HYG holdings with `unitPrice < 70` or `unitPrice < 60`, because HYG provides `unitPrice` directly and Vontobel noted that Bloomberg has used a price-below-60 distress variant while J.P. Morgan used spread greater than 1,000 bp ([BlackRock product-data API for HYG holdings](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=holdings&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239565&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG), [Vontobel Asset Management](https://am.vontobel.com/en/insights/rising-hy-defaults-more-than-priced-in)).

SPDR’s JNK daily holdings file is a stable XLSX at `https://www.ssga.com/library-content/products/fund-data/etfs/us/holdings-daily-us-en-jnk.xlsx`, and it contains columns such as name, identifier, weight, coupon, par value, market value, local currency, and maturity ([SPDR JNK holdings XLSX](https://www.ssga.com/library-content/products/fund-data/etfs/us/holdings-daily-us-en-jnk.xlsx)).  JNK’s file is weaker for a distress-ratio proxy because it does not expose holding-level spread or yield-to-worst in the holdings file I inspected, although market value divided by par value can produce a rough price proxy ([SPDR JNK holdings XLSX](https://www.ssga.com/library-content/products/fund-data/etfs/us/holdings-daily-us-en-jnk.xlsx)).  BlackRock’s separate HYG early-holdings CSV at `https://www.ishares.com/us/literature/holdings/ishhyld-etf-early-holdings.csv` is stable and daily-looking, but it lacks the yield-to-worst field that makes the product-data JSON preferable for a distress proxy ([iShares HYG early holdings CSV](https://www.ishares.com/us/literature/holdings/ishhyld-etf-early-holdings.csv)).  The HYG API path is more fragile than an issuer CSV because it is an internal product-data endpoint, but it is currently used by the public product page and exposes the needed fields without authentication ([iShares HYG product page](https://www.ishares.com/us/products/239565/ishares-iboxx-high-yield-corporate-bond-etf)).

### Scheduled publications and recommendation

Scheduled publication polling should use the Eaton Vance/Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor as the fallback canonical-delayed source, because it explicitly states the J.P. Morgan definition and source date ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110)).  Additional scheduled commentary sources are useful for cross-checking but not for automation unless they consistently expose machine-readable text; State Street’s “Mind on the Market” example quotes a BofA HY distress ratio and defines it as the percentage of bonds trading at 1,000+ bp, but it is an article/PDF-style commentary rather than a dedicated data feed ([State Street Global Advisors](https://www.ssga.com/de/en_gb/institutional/insights/mind-on-the-market-01-august-2025)).  The PRIMARY recommendation is HYG JSON as a daily “ETF-derived HY distress proxy,” computed as either YTW-minus-Treasury ≥ 1,000 bp or price-below-threshold and displayed with the formula in the tile ([BlackRock product-data API for HYG holdings](https://www.blackrock.com/varnish-api/blk-one01-product-data/product-data/api/v2/get-product-data?slice=us&appSubType=ISHARES&appType=PRODUCT_PAGE&component=holdings&locale=en_US&portfolioId=239565&targetSite=us-ishares&userType=individual&excludeContent=true&includeConfig=true&portfolioType=ISHARES_FUND_DATA&currencyCode=USD&shareClass=%20&aladdinFundTicker=ISHHYLD&ticker=HYG)).  The FALLBACK recommendation is the latest Eaton Vance/Morgan Stanley PDF value labeled “J.P. Morgan HY distress ratio, as published, quarter-end date,” and the tile must suppress itself or show “awaiting latest publication” when the PDF as-of date is stale ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110), [Morgan Stanley High Yield Market Monitor Q3 2025](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/insights/articles/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q32025.pdf)).

## 2) CDX.NA.IG and CDX.NA.HY index levels

### Direct/delayed feeds and official-data boundary

S&P Global’s public CDX.NA.HY total-return index page identifies a CDX.NA.HY product family but does not expose the live on-the-run CDS spread or price level needed for the desk tile ([S&P CDX.NA.HY index page](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/fixed-income/cdxna-hy-5y-short-total-return-index)).  S&P’s page describes return indices that measure performance from a protection-buyer or protection-seller position, which is not the same thing as publishing the tradable CDX.NA.IG spread or CDX.NA.HY upfront/price quote ([S&P CDX.NA.HY index page](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/fixed-income/cdxna-hy-5y-short-total-return-index)).  I did not find a free official Markit/S&P API, CSV, or JSON endpoint that publishes the composite on-the-run CDX.NA.IG/HY levels.  The public direct route that exists is therefore not an official composite feed but CFTC Part 43 public dissemination of actual index-CDS trade prints through DTCC’s PPD infrastructure ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf), [DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).

The Tradeweb MAT filing confirms that CDX.NA.IG 5Y and CDX.NA.HY 5Y are traded as current on-the-run series and the next series that will replace the current series on a rolling basis ([Tradeweb MAT filing](https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/stellent/groups/public/@otherif/documents/ifdocs/tradewebmatdeter101813.pdf)).  The same filing states that the IG roll occurs on March 20 and September 20 or the next business day, while the HY roll occurs on March 27 and September 27 or the next business day ([Tradeweb MAT filing](https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/stellent/groups/public/@otherif/documents/ifdocs/tradewebmatdeter101813.pdf)).  The same filing also gives quote-convention clues by listing typical bid/offer spreads of 0.5 bp for CDX IG OTR and 0.125 points for CDX HY OTR, which matches the desk’s warning that IG is spread-quoted while HY is price/upfront-sensitive ([Tradeweb MAT filing](https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/stellent/groups/public/@otherif/documents/ifdocs/tradewebmatdeter101813.pdf)).  Because the SDR feed is print-based, the tile should compute the latest eligible on-the-run print after filtering for underlier name, effective/expiration date, execution timestamp, action type, and price/spread notation rather than treating every DTCC amendment as a fresh trade ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).

### Regulatory public-dissemination channels: exact DTCC paths

DTCC’s dashboard shell is at `https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard`, and the public Angular app constructs its API base as `https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api` for non-portal access ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard), [DTCC PPD main JavaScript](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/main.7b18a773152082f7.js)).  The live ticker endpoint for CFTC credit trades is `GET https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api/ticker/CFTC/CREDITS`, returns JSON, and includes fields such as `disseminationIdentifier`, `actionType`, `eventType`, `eventTimestamp`, `assetClass`, `cleared`, `executionTimestamp`, `effectiveDate`, `expirationDate`, `notionalAmountLeg1`, `price`, `priceNotation`, `spreadLeg1`, `spreadNotationLeg1`, `uniqueProductIdentifier`, `uniqueProductIdentifierShortName`, `uniqueProductIdentifierUnderlierName`, and `disseminationTimestamp` ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard), [DTCC PPD main JavaScript](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/main.7b18a773152082f7.js)).  The intraday slice-file list endpoint is `GET https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api/slice/CFTC/CR`, returns JSON file metadata, and provides S3 ZIP URLs such as `https://kgc0418-tdw-data-0.s3.amazonaws.com/cftc/slices/CFTC_SLICE_CREDITS_YYYY_MM_DD_N.zip` that contain CSV rows ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard), [DTCC PPD main JavaScript](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/main.7b18a773152082f7.js)).  The cumulative end-of-day file-list endpoint is `GET https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api/cumulative/CFTC/CR`, returns JSON file metadata, and provides S3 ZIP URLs such as `https://kgc0418-tdw-data-0.s3.amazonaws.com/cftc/eod/CFTC_CUMULATIVE_CREDITS_YYYY_MM_DD.zip` containing CSV records ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard), [DTCC PPD main JavaScript](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/main.7b18a773152082f7.js)).  The app code refreshes slice, ticker, and cumulative lists on a 120-second interval, so operational polling every two minutes aligns with the public UI behavior rather than hammering the endpoint ([DTCC PPD main JavaScript](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/main.7b18a773152082f7.js)).

Part 43 requires real-time public reporting of swap transaction price and volume as soon as technologically practicable after execution, subject to time delays for block trades and large notional off-facility swaps ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf)).  Part 43 requires SDRs to disseminate swap transaction and pricing data in a consistent, usable, machine-readable electronic format that can be downloaded, saved, and analyzed ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf)).  Part 43 also imposes anonymity, rounding, notional caps, and time-delay rules, including a USD 100 million cap for credit swaps in the older CFR table I reviewed ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf)).  DTCC’s OTC Direct Connect page confirms the same public-data flow: firms report OTC derivatives transaction and pricing data to DDR, DDR posts the reported data to the PPD Dashboard, and subscribers receive the same data immediately after public dissemination via AWS Data Exchange ([DTCC OTC Direct Connect](https://www.dtcc.com/data-services/market-aggregates-and-analytics/otc-direct-connect)).

### Computation details and gotchas

For CDX.NA.IG, filter the DTCC trade records where `assetClass = CR`, `uniqueProductIdentifierUnderlierName = CDX.NA.IG`, tenor is the current 5Y on-the-run maturity, and the record has a usable `spreadLeg1` or price/spread package field rather than only lifecycle metadata ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).  For CDX.NA.HY, filter where `uniqueProductIdentifierUnderlierName = CDX.NA.HY`, then prefer price/upfront notation fields when present because HY index markets conventionally quote in price points rather than simple running-spread terms ([Tradeweb MAT filing](https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/stellent/groups/public/@otherif/documents/ifdocs/tradewebmatdeter101813.pdf), [DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).  Exclude `MODI` amendments unless the amendment updates the economic price field for the original dissemination identifier, because the ticker can include lifecycle modifications with blank price fields and older execution timestamps ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).  Report the timestamp basis explicitly as “execution timestamp” or “dissemination timestamp,” because Part 43 and the PPD feed carry both concepts and block/large-notional delays mean dissemination time can lag execution time ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf)).

### Scheduled publications and recommendation

I would not use financial aggregator pages for this tile unless they are explicitly tied to the current CDX underlier, series, tenor, and quote convention, because the public S&P page I checked does not expose tradable levels and many “CDX” pages refer to ETFs or return indices rather than on-the-run index-CDS quotes ([S&P CDX.NA.HY index page](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/fixed-income/cdxna-hy-5y-short-total-return-index)).  The CFTC weekly swaps report is useful as a sanity check for activity and coverage, but it reports weekly notional and ticket aggregates rather than CDX.NA.IG/HY price or spread levels ([CFTC Weekly Swaps Report](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/SwapsReports/index.htm)).  The PRIMARY recommendation is DTCC PPD ticker/slice extraction, displayed as “latest SDR trade print, CDX.NA.IG/HY 5Y OTR, quote field as reported,” with a hover note that the number is not Markit/S&P composite close ([DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard), [GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf)).  The FALLBACK recommendation is the DTCC cumulative EOD ZIP/CSV file for the latest completed UTC date, using the latest eligible new-trade record and showing “EOD public SDR print-derived level” rather than “official close” ([DTCC PPD main JavaScript](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/main.7b18a773152082f7.js)).

## Implementation notes for tile integrity

Every tile should store `value`, `units`, `methodology_label`, `as_of_date`, `source_url`, `fetch_time_utc`, and `staleness_policy`, because all four metrics have at least one viable path whose data date can diverge from fetch date ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q42025_ev.pdf?1775520000110), [DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard), [SSGA Emerging Market Debt Commentary](https://www.ssga.com/us/en/institutional/insights/emerging-market-debt-commentary-feb-2026), [Morgan Stanley EVLN monthly review](https://www.morganstanley.com/im/publication/etf/monthlyreview_etf_eatonvancefloatingrate_us.pdf)).  For CDX, store both execution timestamp and dissemination timestamp, because Part 43 dissemination can be delayed for block or large-notional trades and because amended records can carry old execution times with new dissemination identifiers ([GovInfo 17 CFR Part 43](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CFR-2012-title17-vol1/pdf/CFR-2012-title17-vol1-part43.pdf), [DTCC PPD dashboard](https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/cftcdashboard)).
