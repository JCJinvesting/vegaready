## Executive summary — my analytical angle

My contribution to the council leans on **mechanism design and second-order reasoning**: I am less interested in cataloguing that "quality outperformed in 2020" than in explaining *why the plumbing made it so*, and — more usefully for a desk — in marking the exact conditions under which each relationship **stops being true**. Three structural ideas run through this report. First, **DTS (duration-times-spread) is the hidden organizing principle of credit risk**: because spread volatility scales with the *level* of spread, a portfolio's true risk unit is not spread duration but duration × spread, which means a "low-risk" book can become a high-beta book overnight simply because spreads widened — no trade required ([Robeco / Ben Dor-Dynkin](https://www.robeco.com/en-us/insights/2024/09/duration-times-spread-measuring-credit-risk)). Second, **most credit "factors" are partially the same trade wearing different clothes** — carry, value, and "reach-for-yield" all load on the same systematic spread beta, so a multi-factor book offers far less crisis diversification than its backtest implies, a point Robeco's own researchers concede about carry ([Houweling, van Zundert & Valerie, Robeco](https://assets.ctfassets.net/tl4x668xzide/4MujAXqGc3WPksku8CfQ9S/b1e08febc549d28bb2cee805e78e5530/201707-does-carry-add-value-to-existing-credit-factors.pdf)). Third, **the single most valuable skill on a credit desk is distinguishing technical (forced-seller/dealer-balance-sheet) widening from fundamental repricing** — they look identical on a spread chart but demand opposite trades, and the four post-2008 episodes give us a clean decoder for telling them apart.

I also foreground **epistemic humility** as a deliverable in itself. Several things this desk would *love* to have — real-time aggregate dealer corporate-bond inventory net of hedges, position-level CDX swaption skew, and clean free crowding gauges — **do not exist in public form**, and I flag each one explicitly rather than fabricate a proxy. The honest "no public figure exists" is treated here as a finding, not a gap. The report is built to drop directly into analyst tables, **decoder cards** (boxed, reusable interpretive heuristics), and a **scenario × cohort matrix**, with every empirical claim carrying a working URL, an as-of date, and a HARD/DIR tag.

A note on current levels for context (not a call): as of June 11, 2026, ICE BofA US **HY OAS sat at ~278 bps** and **IG (Corporate Master) OAS at ~75 bps** per FRED — both deep in the bottom decile of post-GFC readings, i.e. a regime where carry is cheap insurance is expensive, and the technical/fundamental distinction below matters most **[HARD]** ([FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2); [FRED BAMLC0A0CM](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLC0A0CM)).

# GAP 1 — Credit factors: carry, quality, spread-duration/DTS, momentum/value, crowding

## 1.1 Precise practitioner definitions

| Factor | Practitioner definition | Formula / construction | Key source | Tag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Carry** | The expected credit return assuming no default — the spread "pick-up" plus roll-down. Sophisticated desks net out *expected loss*: `carry ≈ OAS − (PD × LGD)`. | `carry = spread pick-up + roll-down`; roll-down = spread change as the bond ages down its curve. Robeco define carry as "the expected credit return... the additional yield on top of the Treasury yield." | [Houweling, van Zundert & Valerie, Robeco (2017)](https://assets.ctfassets.net/tl4x668xzide/4MujAXqGc3WPksku8CfQ9S/b1e08febc549d28bb2cee805e78e5530/201707-does-carry-add-value-to-existing-credit-factors.pdf) | [HARD def] |
| **Quality / up-in-quality** | Tilt toward stronger balance sheets: IG over HY, BB over CCC; metrics are leverage, interest coverage, profitability. In credit, Quality is largely a *natural extension of the Low-Risk factor*. | Rank issuers on leverage / coverage / profitability; "up-in-quality" = rotate down the spread-beta ladder (CCC→B→BB→BBB→A). | [Houweling & van Zundert, "Smart Credit Investing"](https://media.fssuper.com.au/prod/media/library/FS_Super/FS_Super_-_Smart_credit_investing.pdf); [BSIC systematic credit](https://bsic.it/a-systematic-approach-to-credit-investing/) | [HARD def] |
| **Spread duration** | Price sensitivity to a **1 bp** parallel move in the bond's *credit spread*, holding the risk-free rate fixed. | `ΔP/P ≈ −SpreadDuration × ΔSpread`. | [Convex glossary](https://convextrade.com/glossary/credit-spread-duration) | [HARD def] |
| **DTS (Duration × Spread)** | The **risk unit** of credit. Because spread changes are proportional to spread *level* (percentage spread vol is far more stable than absolute), the correct exposure measure is duration × spread, not duration alone. | `DTS = OASD × OAS`. Portfolio DTS = Σ(weightᵢ × OASDᵢ × OASᵢ). | [Ben Dor, Dynkin, Hyman et al. (2007), via Robeco](https://assets.ctfassets.net/tl4x668xzide/4Aapx0GUWLqc0ow8jlMAMb/0a213610fc9f13098d30308c88558723/201911-measuring-credit-risk-with-dts-hk-sg.pdf); [S&P Indexology](https://www.indexologyblog.com/2017/10/12/credit-risk-measure-in-the-sp-u-s-high-yield-low-volatility-corporate-bond-index/) | [HARD def] |
| **Value** | Spread *relative to fundamental risk* — bonds whose OAS is high vs a fair-value model (rating, maturity, equity vol, leverage). Distinct from carry because it references fundamentals. | Long top-decile "cheap" (high spread vs fair value), short rich. | [BSIC](https://bsic.it/a-systematic-approach-to-credit-investing/); [Robeco](https://media.fssuper.com.au/prod/media/library/FS_Super/FS_Super_-_Smart_credit_investing.pdf) | [HARD def] |
| **Momentum** | Past 6–12m credit excess return (bond-level) and/or equity momentum of the issuer translated into credit. | Long top-decile past-6m credit excess return (Jostova et al. convention). | [Robeco](https://media.fssuper.com.au/prod/media/library/FS_Super/FS_Super_-_Smart_credit_investing.pdf); [BSIC](https://bsic.it/a-systematic-approach-to-credit-investing/) | [HARD def] |
| **Crowding** | No single canonical public gauge. Proxies: fund-flow concentration, dealer/positioning surveys, factor-valuation spreads, and CDX cash-basis. | See §1.5 — flagged as a partial-data area. | (multiple) | [DIR] |

> **DECODER CARD — "DTS is destiny"**
> A book's credit risk = Σ DTS, *not* Σ spread duration. Two consequences the desk should internalize: **(1)** if spreads widen 50%, your risk rises ~50% with zero trading — you are mechanically *longer* risk in a selloff exactly when you'd want to be shorter (negative convexity of the risk measure). **(2)** A "barbell" of short-dated CCC + long-dated AAA can carry the *same DTS* as an intermediate BBB book but behaves nothing like it in a flight-to-quality. Always decompose DTS by rating bucket, not just report the total.
> *Source: [Ben Dor/Dynkin DTS methodology via Robeco](https://assets.ctfassets.net/tl4x668xzide/4Aapx0GUWLqc0ow8jlMAMb/0a213610fc9f13098d30308c88558723/201911-measuring-credit-risk-with-dts-hk-sg.pdf).* **[HARD def / DIR interpretation]**

## 1.2 The uncomfortable truth: carry ≈ value ≈ spread beta

Robeco's own researchers — among the most-cited proponents of credit factor investing — conclude that **carry is *not* a separate factor**: empirically the carry premium "is driven by taking higher risk, both higher credit market risk and higher default risk," its risk-adjusted outperformance vs the market is "statistically insignificant," and a carry portfolio "behaves very similarly to a value portfolio" ([Houweling, van Zundert & Valerie, Robeco 2017](https://assets.ctfassets.net/tl4x668xzide/4MujAXqGc3WPksku8CfQ9S/b1e08febc549d28bb2cee805e78e5530/201707-does-carry-add-value-to-existing-credit-factors.pdf)) **[HARD]**. The same body of work finds the **Quality premium for IG is weak** and is largely *subsumed by the Low-Risk factor*, while Quality "still adds value" mainly in HY ([Robeco / BSIC summary](https://bsic.it/a-systematic-approach-to-credit-investing/)) **[HARD]**. The second-order implication for a desk: a "diversified multi-factor credit sleeve" that is long carry + long value + reaching down in quality is, in a crisis, **mostly one position** — long systematic spread beta — and will not diversify the way the brochure suggests. The real diversifiers are **Momentum** (which can go *short* high-beta names) and genuine **up-in-quality / Low-Risk**, which is the only factor reliably *short* DTS.

## 1.3 Episode-by-episode: which factor led, protected, broke

**2008 GFC — quality protected, carry/value broke, DTS exploded.** HY OAS ran from ~280 bps (Jul 2007) to a record **2,023 bps on Nov 21, 2008** (peak ~2,182 bps on Dec 15 on intraday/close basis), while IG/BBB OAS peaked near **590–612 bps** — an HY/IG amplitude ratio of ~3.5× **[HARD]** ([Eco3min GFC anatomy, FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2/BAMLC0A4CBBB](https://eco3min.fr/en/hy-oas-2008-2009-financial-crisis-anatomy-credit-spread/); [Eco3min HY vs IG](https://eco3min.fr/en/hy-oas-vs-ig-oas-credit-spread-comparison/)). Mechanism: a *carry/reach-for-yield* book was disproportionately in lower-quality, higher-DTS paper that took the brunt; *up-in-quality* (the one factor short DTS) protected capital in relative terms; *value* "broke" because cheap bonds got cheaper as fundamentals deteriorated faster than spreads could reprice. The Moody's trailing-12m HY default rate rose toward its **14.7% cycle peak in Nov 2009**, validating ex-post that this was *fundamental*, not merely technical ([Eco3min](https://eco3min.fr/en/hy-oas-2008-2009-financial-crisis-anatomy-credit-spread/)) **[HARD]**.

**2011 euro-sovereign — flight-to-quality, mostly technical for US credit.** This was primarily a *funding/contagion* episode: euro-area peripheral spreads over Bunds hit a then-record **~459 bps** in mid-Nov 2011, and US 5y swap spreads/LIBOR-OIS blew out (LIBOR-OIS to ~38 bps, the widest since June 2009) ([Bundesbank Nov 2011](https://www.bundesbank.de/resource/blob/707228/6173a9d86b8b4f03ed77d991a2e2a7b2/472B63F073F071307366337C94F8C870/2011-11-financial-markets-data.pdf); [Bloomberg, Nov 2011](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2011-11-17/money-market-spreads-surge-to-two-year-high-on-concern-over-europe-crisis)) **[HARD]**. For US credit the dominant move was *up-in-quality and into liquidity* — flight-to-liquidity, not a domestic default cycle — which is why US HY recovered faster than 2008 once the ECB's LTRO/OMT backstop arrived. **Quality and Low-Risk led; carry/value were punished but not destroyed.**

**2015–16 energy/commodity — the cleanest "quality wins, carry/value/CCC break" case.** This was a *sector-concentrated fundamental* shock, ideal for illustrating dispersion. In 2015, **CCC bonds returned −15% vs BB −0.4%** — a ~15-point quality spread in a single year ([Angelo Gordon Q4 2015](https://cmp.angelogordon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/AG-Capital-Markets-Perspectives-2015-Q4_cmp-20.pdf)) **[HARD]**. In Q4 2015 alone, the broad HY index returned −2.17% (+33 bps spread widening) while the *higher-quality* sub-index returned −1.13% (−3 bps, i.e. spread *tightening*); within ratings, **BB −0.11% > B −2.40% > CCC −8.48%**, and Energy was −12.8% ([BMO/Monegy Q4 2015](https://www.bmo.com/monegy/pdf/Q4_2015_Monegy_High_Yield_Summary.pdf)) **[HARD]**. Forced-seller mechanics: HY funds saw **~$13bn outflows and loan funds ~$20bn**, with "broad illiquidity and retail outflows" creating a "pronounced bifurcation between the 'have' and 'have not' credits" ([Angelo Gordon](https://cmp.angelogordon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/AG-Capital-Markets-Perspectives-2015-Q4_cmp-20.pdf)) **[HARD]**. *Up-in-quality and Momentum (which would have been short energy) led and protected; carry/value broke catastrophically in the distressed sleeve.*

**2020 COVID dash-for-cash — quality led, but the rally inverted everything.** AAA IG OAS widened ~150 bps while HY widened **>500 bps** over the Feb–Mar dislocation ([Philadelphia Fed WP 20-43](https://www.philadelphiafed.org/-/media/frbp/assets/working-papers/2020/wp20-43.pdf)) **[HARD]**. J.P. Morgan AM's factor decomposition is the cleanest public study: in the drawdown (Period 2) **factor investing added value** with *Quality outperforming and Value underperforming*; in the stimulus-driven rally (Period 3) "factor strategies struggled... Value added value in the rebound... Momentum was negative during the rally given the pace of the turnaround" ([J.P. Morgan AM, Jun 2020](https://am.jpmorgan.com/se/en/asset-management/per/insights/etf-perspectives/factor-investing-in-volatile-credit-markets/)) **[HARD]**. The decisive second-order lesson: **the policy backstop (SMCCF) inverted the factor ranking within days** — the Fed's Mar 23 / Apr 9 announcements cut IG spreads ~100 bps and the effect was *concentrated in lower-rated IG / fallen angels*, i.e. it rewarded exactly the *lower-quality, higher-carry* names that quality had been protecting against ([Brookings/Liang](https://www.brookings.edu/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/wp69-liang_1.pdf); [Boston Fed SMCCF efficacy](https://www.bostonfed.org/publications/research-department-working-paper/2024/the-fed-takes-on-corporate-credit-risk-an-analysis-of-the-efficacy-of-the-smccf.aspx)) **[HARD]**.

**2022 rate-shock / UK-LDI — a factor episode about *duration*, not credit, with forced-selling tells.** Unusually, the 2022 drawdown was driven by the *risk-free rate*, not spreads, so the protective factor was **short duration**, and the LDI crisis was a *pure forced-seller* event: leveraged UK pension LDI funds (~£200bn) faced margin calls as gilt yields spiked, were forced to dump gilts into evaporating liquidity, and the BoE bought **£19.3bn** (£12.1bn conventional + £7.2bn linkers) over 13 working days to break the doom-loop ([BoE WP "Anatomy of the 2022 gilt crisis"](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/working-paper/2023/an-anatomy-of-the-2022-gilt-market-crisis.pdf); [BoE Quarterly Bulletin](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/quarterly-bulletin/2023/2023/financial-stability-buy-sell-tools-a-gilt-market-case-study)) **[HARD]**. For credit factors the lesson is that **DTS missed the risk** — the loss came through *rates duration* and *leverage/margin*, dimensions DTS does not measure — a clean example of model fragility (§1.4).

There is **no single universally-canonical credit-specific rotation table** in the public literature; the strongest publicly-readable framings are (a) the equity-factor-by-recession evidence from **Russell Investments**, which credit desks routinely borrow, and (b) the **J.P. > **DECODER CARD — the "momentum crash" trap**
> Momentum is the best pre-recession factor but suffers a violent reversal in the V-shaped recovery (the "momentum crash" of Daniel & Moskowitz): the short leg — the battered low-quality names — snaps back hardest, so a momentum book that was *short junk* gets run over. In credit this maps directly onto 2020: momentum was "flat through the drawdown, negative during the rally" ([JPM AM](https://am.jpmorgan.com/se/en/asset-management/per/insights/etf-perspectives/factor-investing-in-volatile-credit-markets/); equity mechanism: [Russell/QRG](https://www.investpmc.com/sites/default/files/documents/QRG-WP-FactorPerformance.pdf)).
> **Desk rule:** if a policy backstop is plausible, *cut momentum gross before the turn, not after.* **[HARD evidence / DIR rule]**

## 1.5 Crowding gauges — *epistemic-humility flag*

**There is no clean, free, real-time credit-crowding index.** What the desk can assemble from public data: (1) **fund-flow concentration / extremes** (ICI/EPFR, §2.3) as a proxy for consensus reaching-for-yield; (2) **factor-valuation spreads** (how rich the long leg trades vs short — Research Affiliates and Alpha Architect publish methodology but not live credit readings — [Research Affiliates](https://www.researchaffiliates.com/insights/publications/articles/828-factor-timing-keep-it-simple); [Alpha Architect](https://alphaarchitect.com/factor-investing-premiums-and-the-economic-cycle/)); (3) the **CDX cash-basis** (negative basis = cash bonds cheap to CDS = forced cash selling / crowded longs unwinding). Live, position-level crowding from dealer or hedge-fund positioning is **not public** — treat any "crowding score" sourced from a vendor as proprietary and uncheckable. State this to clients rather than imply precision.

## 1.6 Current factor readings — *honest answer: largely not free/public*

Live, methodology-consistent credit-factor returns (a Robeco-style Value/Momentum/Quality/Carry long-short series) are **proprietary** (Robeco, AQR, Bloomberg-Barclays). The only *free* anchors are the index OAS levels and rating-bucket OAS from FRED (§ exec summary; BBB via BAMLC0A4CBBB). **Recommendation:** report current *positioning posture* (tight spreads ⇒ carry cheap, up-in-quality expensive) qualitatively; do **not** publish a live factor-return number from memory.

# GAP 2 — Positioning & technicals (supply/demand and the forced-selling read)

## 2.1 Dealer inventory — NY Fed primary dealer positions

**Series & access.** The NY Fed publishes **primary dealer net positions** under the FR 2004 collection, weekly, as of close of business **Wednesday**, released the following **Thursday** ([NY Fed Primary Dealers](https://www.newyorkfed.org/markets/primarydealers); [Fed FR 2004 forms](https://www.federalreserve.gov/apps/reportingforms/Report/Index/FR_2004)). The machine-readable time series live at the NY Fed Markets API; the relevant corporate identifiers are **`PDPOSCS-TOT`** (Total Corporate Securities net position), with sub-series splitting **IG vs below-IG** by maturity bucket (e.g. `PDPOSCSBND-...`), under dataset code **SBN2024** ([NY Fed timeseries dictionary](https://markets.newyorkfed.org/api/pd/list/timeseries.xlsx); weekly statistical release [PDF](https://www.newyorkfed.org/medialibrary/media/banking/reportingforms/primarystats/dealpdf.pdf)). The data are also mirrored in the **OFR Short-Term Funding Monitor** ([OFR/financialresearch.gov](https://www.financialresearch.gov/short-term-funding-monitor/datasets/nypd/)). FRED carries a quarterly Flow-of-Funds dealer holdings series ("Security Brokers and Dealers; Corporate and Foreign Bonds (Net)") for longer history ([FRED dealers tag](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/tags/series?t=assets%3Bdealers)).

**Critical methodology caveats (epistemic-humility flags).** (1) The reported "net outright position" **commingles** corporates with a broader securities definition and is **net of dealer shorts/hedges**, so it understates gross intermediation capacity. (2) The FRBNY only began collecting **corporate bonds as a separate asset class on April 3, 2013**; pre-2013 corporate positions were **extrapolated** ([He, Kelly & Manela, NBER w26494](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w26494/w26494.pdf)). (3) It covers only **~20 primary dealers**, missing non-primary intermediaries. He et al. note bluntly: "data on dealers' exact holding amounts of corporate bonds [is not available]" — so even the academic gold standard reconstructs inventory from TRACE rather than observing it ([NBER w26494](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w26494/w26494.pdf)). **The desk should treat the level as indicative and the *change* as the signal.**

**How to read it.** Inventory **build** = dealers absorbing customer selling (a buffer, often a contrarian "they're catching the knife" signal in early stress); inventory **drawdown into a selloff** = dealers *refusing* to warehouse risk = the balance-sheet channel is binding = forced sellers must clear at fire-sale prices. He, Kelly & Manela quantify the inventory channel: **a 1-standard-deviation dealer-inventory shock is associated with quarterly credit-spread increases of ~3–40 bps, and a 1-SD intermediary-distress shock ~4–70 bps, with higher sensitivities for lower-rated bonds** ([NBER w26494](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w26494/w26494.pdf); [slides](https://cpb-us-w2.wpmucdn.com/voices.uchicago.edu/dist/6/2325/files/2021/11/dealer_inventory_slides_final.pdf)) **[HARD]**.

**Post-2008 balance-sheet (SLR) context.** The structural backdrop: dealers' capacity to warehouse bonds has collapsed since the crisis. Duffie (Jackson Hole 2023) finds **primary-dealer balance sheets relative to Treasuries outstanding have shrunk by a factor of ~4 since 2007**, and by 2017 dealer corporate-bond inventories were at an **all-time low relative to market size** ([GARP on SLR / Duffie](https://www.garp.org/risk-intelligence/credit/new-look-leverage-ratio-240503); [ECB WP 2589](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp2589~2fda18e5ca.en.html); [AEA paper](https://www.aeaweb.org/conference/2022/preliminary/paper/6DrkdfYy)) **[HARD]**. The **Supplementary Leverage Ratio (SLR)** taxes balance sheet regardless of asset risk, so in stress dealers shed *even safe* inventory — the mechanism behind 2020's dash-for-cash, when "dealers' balance sheets were full, everybody had sold to them, they couldn't buy any more" until the Fed offered ~$1tn/day repo and bought $40bn/day of Treasuries to *drain and refill* dealer balance sheets ([Bianco/MacroVoices, Mar 2020](https://www.macrovoices.com/podcast-transcripts/814-jim-bianco-covid-19-risk-parity-unwind-what-s-next-for-markets)) **[DIR/contemporaneous]**.

> **DECODER CARD — reading dealer inventory in stress**
> | Inventory move | Spread move | Interpretation |
> |---|---|---|
> | Build ↑ | Widening | Dealers still warehousing — buffer intact, often early stage |
> | **Drawdown ↓** | **Widening** | **Balance-sheet channel binding — forced sellers, fire-sale risk = TECHNICAL** |
> | Build ↑ | Tightening | Dealers positioning for recovery — bullish confirmation |
> | Flat | Widening | Pure fundamental repricing, dealers not the marginal player |
> *Read the **weekly change**, not the level; lower-rated buckets carry the most signal. Source: [NBER w26494](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w26494/w26494.pdf).* **[HARD/DIR]**

## 2.2 TRACE — what FINRA publicly discloses (and what it hides)

FINRA's **TRACE** disseminates post-trade corporate/agency/securitized transaction data; publicly free are the **daily "Report Card" and Monthly Volume Reports** (total + average-daily volume for corporate, agency, securitized products), the **daily aggregate volumes**, and the academic-research datasets ([FINRA Monthly Volume Reports](https://www.finra.org/finra-data/browse-catalog/trace-volume-reports/about-trace-monthly-volume-reports); [FINRA Corporate Bonds Daily Report Card](https://www.finra.org/compliance-tools/report-center/trace/corporate-bonds-daily); [FINRA academic studies](https://www.finra.org/filing-reporting/trace/trace-independent-academic-studies)) **[HARD]**. **What TRACE does NOT publicly show:** (1) **dealer identity** (counterparties anonymized); (2) **full size on large trades** — block trades above dissemination caps ($5mm IG / $1mm HY) are shown as "5MM+"/"1MM+", masking true size; (3) **dealer inventory** (it is *transactions*, not positions — academics must *reconstruct* inventory from signed trade flow, per He et al.). **Reading depth/stress:** rising average trade *count* with *falling* average trade *size*, widening realized bid-ask, and a jump in same-day round-trip dispersion ("noise") all signal thinning depth. During COVID, the corporate-bond pricing-"noise" measure **peaked at ~50 bps on Mar 23, 2020** even *after* the PDCF began — a quantified depth-collapse tell ([Fed FEDS Note, Jul 2021](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/dealer-inventory-constraints-in-the-corporate-bond-market-during-the-covid-crisis-20210715.html)) **[HARD]**.

|---|---|---|---|
| **ICI** | Estimated Long-Term Mutual Fund Flows + **Combined Estimated LT Flows & ETF Net Issuance** (bond detail), free on ici.org | **Weekly (Wed)** + monthly | Most authoritative free US flow series; bond-fund inflow/outflow extremes ([ICI flows](https://www.ici.org/research/stats/flows); [ICI combined](https://www.ici.org/research/stats/combined_flows); [ICI weekly ETF+LT](https://www.ici.org/research/statistics/etfs/weekly-combined-estimated-etf-and-longterm-flows)) **[HARD]** |
| **EPFR** | Headline weekly flow figures in free "Global Navigator" notes (full data paid) | Weekly | Global, granular IG/HY/EM splits; e.g. "$26bn into Bond Funds first week of Aug 2025" ([EPFR](https://epfr.com/insights/global-navigator/more-mixed-signals-early-august/)) **[HARD headline / DIR detail]** |
| **Lipper** (Refinitiv/LSEG) | Weekly US fund-flow report widely *quoted* in press; **underlying data paid** | Weekly (Thu) | The classic "HY fund outflow of $Xbn" headline; reconcile vs ICI **[DIR]** |

**How to read flow-driven spread moves.** Retail HY/loan funds are the *forced-seller transmission channel*: when redemptions hit open-end funds that hold illiquid bonds, managers sell the most-liquid names first, then are forced into the rest — converting a flow shock into price impact. The 2015–16 episode is the textbook case: **~$13bn HY + ~$20bn loan outflows** drove the bifurcation and "credible-bid" collapse ([Angelo Gordon Q4 2015](https://cmp.angelogordon.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/AG-Capital-Markets-Perspectives-2015-Q4_cmp-20.pdf)) **[HARD]**. **Tell:** flow-driven widening shows up as *uniform* widening (the fund sells a slice of everything) and reverses fast when flows turn; fundamental widening is *dispersed* (concentrated in deteriorating names). *Epistemic flag:* ICI is the only fully-free, methodology-documented US series; EPFR/Lipper headlines are free but the underlying data is paid — cite the headline figure, not an implied precision.

## 2.4 Credit-index options / CDX swaptions — *partial-data flag*

Conceptually, **CDX index options (payer/receiver swaptions) and their skew are the cleanest hedging-pressure gauge** in credit: heavy demand for **payer** swaptions (the right to *buy* protection / be short credit) richens payer vol and steepens the **payer-over-receiver skew**, signaling that real money is paying up to hedge downside. A documented example: in mid-2023, "continued hedging in USD HY [was] reflected in a wider cash-CDX basis and payer-receiver swaption skew" ([Φpost / Credit Today, Jul 2023](https://www.phipost.com/article/credit-today-2023-07-14/index.html)) **[HARD/contemporaneous]**. Academic work confirms the structural link: for CDX, changes in spread and option-implied **volatility are strongly positively correlated (~0.675)**, and spread-vs-skewness ~0.255 ([Collin-Dufresne et al., UZH](https://www.phd-finance.uzh.ch/dam/jcr:bc0971f7-a73f-475f-99d6-e73f9bc7f7f0/FS_spring21_paper_collin-dufresne.pdf)) **[HARD]**. Mechanics references: [Baruch IRC lecture on default swaptions](https://mfe.baruch.cuny.edu/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/IRC_Lecture7_2019.pdf). **Epistemic-humility flag:** *there is no free public feed of live CDX swaption skew or index-option positioning.* The pricing lives in dealer runs and Bloomberg/IHS-Markit terminals. A public-desk note can describe the *signal* and cite a contemporaneous commentary, but **cannot publish a live skew number from a free source** — say so.

## 2.5 Primary issuance as a supply technical (SIFMA)

**Source.** SIFMA publishes free **US Corporate Bond statistics** (issuance, trading ADV, outstanding) and the quarterly **Research Quarterly: Fixed Income — Issuance & Trading** ([SIFMA US Corporate](https://www.sifma.org/research/statistics/us-corporate-bonds-statistics); [SIFMA Research Quarterly](https://www.sifma.org/research/statistics/research-quarterly-fixed-income-issuance-and-trading)). Anchors: 2024 corporate issuance **$2.0tn (+30.6% Y/Y)**, outstanding ~**$11.5tn (4Q25)**; quarterly issuance has exceeded $600bn only a handful of times (1Q24 hit **$627.6B, +135.8% Q/Q**; 1Q26 corporates **$775.2B**, largest since 2Q20) ([SIFMA Fact Book](https://www.sifma.org/research/statistics/fact-book); [SIFMA 1Q24 RQ](https://www.sifma.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/SIFMA-Research-Quarterly-Fixed-Income-IT-1Q24.pdf); [SIFMA RQ Apr 2026](https://www.sifma.org/research/statistics/research-quarterly-fixed-income-issuance-and-trading)) **[HARD]**.

**How supply moves secondary spreads.** Heavy primary calendars widen secondary spreads *through concession alone*, even with stable default risk: a big calendar "raises the price of access through new-issue concession, and secondary bonds cheapen to compete with fresh supply" — the market "rationalizes the balance sheet" ([EBC, Jan 2026](https://www.ebc.com/forex/credit-vs-supply-the-2026-bond-market-pressure-point)) **[DIR/HARD-mechanism]**. Contemporaneous color from 2015: "sectors that have seen a lot of supply are seeing spread pressure... deals coming with large new-issue concessions but not tightening in secondary" ([Reuters, Goldman's Fine](https://jp.reuters.com/article/stunning-bounce-back-for-us-high-grade-idUSL1N11G1S1/)) **[HARD-quote]**. **Tell that supply (not fundamentals) is driving:** new issues require *rising* concessions to clear, secondary cheapens in *sympathy* with the calendar, dispersion rises within BBB, and the move *reverses* once the calendar clears — vs fundamental widening, which persists ([EBC](https://www.ebc.com/forex/credit-vs-supply-the-2026-bond-market-pressure-point)).

## 2.6 The master question — distinguishing TECHNICAL vs FUNDAMENTAL widening

> **DECODER CARD — Technical vs Fundamental spread-widening (the desk's most valuable read)**
> | Tell | TECHNICAL (forced-seller / dealer / supply) | FUNDAMENTAL (repricing default risk) |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Dispersion** | Low — *everything* widens together (fund sells a slice of all) | High — concentrated in deteriorating names/sectors |
> | **Dealer inventory** | Drawdown (refusing risk) | Roughly flat (dealers not the driver) |
> | **CDS–cash basis** | Cash cheap to CDS (negative basis) — cash forced sellers | Basis broadly neutral; moves with names' fundamentals |
> | **ETF NAV** | ETF trades at **discount** to NAV (liquidity premium) | ETF ≈ NAV |
> | **Speed & reversal** | Fast, violent, **mean-reverts** on backstop/flow turn | Grinding, **persists**; tracks rising default rate |
> | **Default-rate confirmation** | Default rate does *not* follow | Trailing-12m default rate *rises ex-post* |
> | **Cross-asset** | Funding stress (LIBOR-OIS / SOFR spikes), repo strain | Equity earnings revisions, credit downgrades |
> | **Episode archetype** | 2020 COVID, 2022 UK-LDI, 2011 funding leg | 2008 GFC (also technical), 2015–16 energy |
> **Killer single tell:** in 2020 the **ETF-to-NAV discount and IG-cash-cheap-to-CDS** were diagnostic of *technical* dysfunction — "IG corporate bonds traded at a discount to CDS; ETFs traded at a discount to NAV, more so for *safer* bonds" — the giveaway that this was plumbing, not credit ([Haddad, Moreira, Muir, RFS 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7928582/)). When the *safest* bonds are the cheapest, you are looking at a liquidity scramble, not a default scare. **[HARD anchor / DIR framework]**

# GAP 3 — Cross-asset & regime correlations

## 3.1 Key relationships and their regime-dependence

**HY OAS vs equity vol (VIX).** The tightest cross-asset pair in credit, with **weekly/monthly correlation ~0.7–0.85** ([Convex VIX-vs-HY](https://convextrade.com/compare/vix-vs-hy-spreads)) **[HARD]**. The mechanism is structural (Merton): equity is a call on firm assets, bondholders are short a put, so rising asset vol mechanically raises both implied equity vol and default probability — confirmed back to Collin-Dufresne, Goldstein & Martin (2001) ([JBES study](https://jbes.scholasticahq.com/article/146576-investigating-the-vix-index-relationship-with-high-yield-investment-grade-bond-spreads-exploring-structural-breaks-threshold-effects.pdf)) **[HARD]**. *Regime-dependence:* the link is **stronger for HY than IG**, and *strengthens at high VIX* (structural breaks / threshold effects) ([JBES](https://jbes.scholasticahq.com/article/146576-investigating-the-vix-index-relationship-with-high-yield-investment-grade-bond-spreads-exploring-structural-breaks-threshold-effects.pdf)). *When it breaks:* (a) **vol-suppression regimes** (post-2017 short-vol, post-2020 0DTE) push VIX *below* what credit implies — VIX/HY ratio depressed; (b) **idiosyncratic credit events** (a sector default wave) widen HY with calm VIX. Watch the VIX/HY-OAS ratio: long-run ~0.04–0.06; **>0.08 = equity stress running ahead of credit; <0.03 = credit stress ahead of equity** ([Convex](https://convextrade.com/compare/vix-vs-hy-spreads)) **[HARD/DIR]**.

**OAS vs Treasury level & curve shape.** Two opposing forces. In *growth-driven* selloffs, yields and spreads move *opposite* (flight-to-quality: Treasuries rally, spreads widen — negative correlation; "buy Treasuries as safe haven, sell risky bonds," documented for EM sovereigns where credit spreads load *negatively* on UST yields, statistically significant at 1% — [HKMA RM03/2016](https://www.hkma.gov.hk/media/eng/publication-and-research/research/research-memorandums/2016/RM03_2016.pdf)) **[HARD]**. In *inflation/rate-shock* selloffs (2022), yields *and* spreads can rise together. Empirically, **changes in rates affect HY spreads only minimally** regardless of the fed-funds level — HY is a *credit/growth* instrument, not a rates instrument ([JBES](https://jbes.scholasticahq.com/article/146576-investigating-the-vix-index-relationship-with-high-yield-investment-grade-bond-spreads-exploring-structural-breaks-threshold-effects.pdf)) **[HARD]**. *Curve:* a deeply inverted curve historically precedes spread widening (recession signal) but is *not* contemporaneously correlated.

**OAS vs the broad dollar.** A *stronger dollar tightens dollar funding* and tends to *widen* credit spreads, most acutely for **EM sovereign/corporate** dollar debt. Currency appreciation vs USD "is associated with a compression of EME sovereign spreads and inflows into EM bond funds" — i.e., USD strength ⇒ spread widening + outflows (the risk-taking channel of the dollar) ([Hofmann, Shim & Shin, NBER/BIS](https://conference.nber.org/confer/2016/SI2016/IFM/Hofmann_Shim_Shin.pdf)) **[HARD]**. The correlation is **heterogeneous within EM**: higher-rated and oil-*exporting* sovereigns show little/negative dollar-beta; lower-rated importers show strong positive dollar-beta ([ING EM Sovereign Perspectives](https://research.ing.com/docs/B7A42C7E-9A97-4FF4-BDD9-99C8831D88D1.pdf)) **[HARD]**. For US IG/HY the dollar link is weaker and indirect (via global risk appetite).

**Stock–bond correlation regime & the post-2022 sign flip.** The single most important cross-asset regime fact for a credit/multi-asset desk. Stock-bond correlation was **positive from the late 1960s–late 1990s, turned negative through the 2000s–2010s, and flipped *positive again in 2022*** ([ECB FSR Box, Nov 2022](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/financial-stability-publications/fsr/focus/2022/html/ecb.fsrbox202211_02~7abb48e333.en.html)) **[HARD]**. Driver: **inflation and real-rate volatility** — the correlation "is lowest when inflation is close to 2%" and "always positive in high-inflation environments"; a +1% rise in *both* inflation and real rates raises the correlation by **~+0.17** ([Brixton et al. / Financial Analysts Journal 2024](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0015198X.2024.2317333); [ECB](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/financial-stability-publications/fsr/focus/2022/html/ecb.fsrbox202211_02~7abb48e333.en.html)) **[HARD]**. Episode magnitudes: a representative 60/40 saw the flip to **~+0.17 in 2022 (S&P −19%, AGG −13%)** and the correlation stayed positive (~+0.27) into 2023–24 ([Beyond Equity Hedging, 2026](https://www.open-funds.ch/sites/default/files/2026-05/20260324_BEYOND_EQUITY_HEDGING_FinalFinal.pdf)) **[HARD/DIR]**. **Second-order consequence:** the flip *destroyed risk-parity's diversification* — risk parity "is vulnerable to shifts in correlations, as 2022 demonstrated" ([LongTail Alpha](https://www.longtailalpha.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/LongTail-Alpha-Risk-Parity-With-Trend-Following-1.pdf)) — and it removes the Treasury "hedge" for credit: in an inflation shock, *both* legs of a credit-plus-duration book lose, "there is no offset at all" ([Beyond Equity Hedging](https://www.open-funds.ch/sites/default/files/2026-05/20260324_BEYOND_EQUITY_HEDGING_FinalFinal.pdf)).

## 3.3 When NOT to trust each correlation (regime-break guide)

> **DECODER CARD — correlation kill-switches**
> - **HY OAS ↔ VIX (~0.8):** breaks when (a) **vol is structurally suppressed** (short-vol/0DTE flows) → VIX understates credit risk; (b) an **idiosyncratic default wave** widens HY with calm equities. *Don't* use VIX as a HY proxy when the VIX/HY ratio is >0.08 or <0.03 ([Convex](https://convextrade.com/compare/vix-vs-hy-spreads); [JBES](https://jbes.scholasticahq.com/article/146576-investigating-the-vix-index-relationship-with-high-yield-investment-grade-bond-spreads-exploring-structural-breaks-threshold-effects.pdf)).
> - **OAS ↔ UST (negative/flight-to-quality):** breaks in **inflation/rate-shock regimes** (2022) — Treasuries stop hedging credit; the duration "offset" in IG vanishes ([ECB](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/financial-stability-publications/fsr/focus/2022/html/ecb.fsrbox202211_02~7abb48e333.en.html)).
> - **Stock–bond (negative):** the *whole regime can flip positive* when inflation/real-rate vol is high (post-2022). **Never assume the 2000–2020 negative correlation is structural** — it was a low-inflation artifact ([FAJ 2024](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0015198X.2024.2317333)).
> - **EM sovereign ↔ dollar:** **not uniform** — collapses/inverts for oil-exporters and high-grade EM; only reliable for lower-rated importers ([ING](https://research.ing.com/docs/B7A42C7E-9A97-4FF4-BDD9-99C8831D88D1.pdf)).
> - **Risk-parity / cross-asset diversification generally:** in a *dash-for-cash*, **all correlations go to ~1** as everything is sold for cash — diversification fails exactly when needed (March 2020, [ECB vol-targeting box](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/financial-stability-publications/fsr/focus/2020/html/ecb.fsrbox202005_02~f6616db9be.en.html)).
> **[HARD anchors / DIR rules]**

## Epistemic-humility ledger — "no free public figure exists"

A deliberate inventory of where this desk should *decline to quote a number* rather than fabricate one:

1. **Live credit-factor returns** (Value/Momentum/Quality/Carry long-short): **proprietary** (Robeco/AQR/Bloomberg). Free: only index/rating OAS levels.

6. **Crowding index for credit:** no clean free gauge; assemble from flows + factor-valuation + CDS-cash basis, and disclose the imprecision.
