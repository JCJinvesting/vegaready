# P-06 — Fetchable Sources for the Four Pending Credit Tiles

**Author:** Claude Opus 4.8 (model-council research) · **Analysis depth:** Deep Dive
**Scope:** For each of the four pending credit tiles, every realistic fetch path — direct/delayed feeds, regulatory public dissemination, ETF-constituent proxies, and scheduled publications — with exact URL, fetch format, cadence/lag, precise definition/units, fragility, and a PRIMARY/FALLBACK recommendation plus the honest label the tile must carry.

A note on method: where possible I fetched the actual files rather than relying on descriptions. I downloaded and parsed the DTCC public CREDITS swap file, the SPDR JNK holdings spreadsheet, and confirmed the Morningstar Indexes screener and iShares EMB schema first-hand. Findings that **contradict the priors in the brief** are flagged explicitly — there are several material ones (notably that CDX.NA.HY is disseminated as *spread*, not price, in the U.S. SDR feed; and that JNK's holdings file carries *no* per-bond yield, so the distress-ratio proxy must be built from HYG, not JNK).

## Metric 1 — US High-Yield Distress Ratio (share of HY universe trading ≥ 1000 bp)

### What the canonical figure is
The distress ratio has two near-identical canonical definitions used by the two main publishers. J.P. Morgan (the version reproduced in fund literature) defines it as **the percent of high-yield bonds trading with a spread-to-worst greater than 1000 bps**, stated explicitly on the source note of the Eaton Vance / Morgan Stanley *High Yield Market Monitor* ([Eaton Vance High Yield Market Monitor Q1 2026](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q12026.pdf?1778544000225)). FridsonVision defines it as **the percentage of issues in the ICE BofA US High Yield Index quoted at distressed levels — an OAS of +1,000 bps or more** ([S&P Global Market Intelligence, Fridson monthly recap](https://www.spglobal.com/marketintelligence/en/news-insights/latest-news-headlines/fridson-monthly-high-yield-recap-reveals-conflicting-market-signals-67402398)). The only definitional fork is *spread-to-worst* (J.P. Morgan) vs *OAS* (Fridson/ICE); the two run within tens of bp of each other and either can be honestly labeled.

### (a) Direct / delayed feeds
There is **no free programmatic feed of the distress ratio itself**, confirming the brief. FRED carries only the aggregate ICE BofA US High Yield OAS series `BAMLH0A0HYM2` ([FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)) and rating-bucket spreads such as Single-B OAS `BAMLH0A2HYB` ([FRED BAMLH0A2HYB](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A2HYB)) — these are aggregate OAS levels, not the constituent-level share-above-1000bp. The ICE BofA **US Distressed High Yield Index** that Fridson references (its constituents are exactly the ≥1000bp OAS names) is a real ICE subindex, but it is **not republished on FRED** as either a level or a count, so no distress-*ratio* can be derived from FRED. **Dead end at the feed level.**

### (b) Regulatory public-dissemination channels
Not applicable. The distress ratio is a derived statistic over cash bonds; there is no SDR/SEF/FINRA channel that disseminates "share of HY trading ≥1000bp." FINRA TRACE disseminates individual corporate-bond prints but not yields/spreads in a form that yields a ready distress ratio without a full HY universe and a curve engine. Not a realistic path for a tile.

### (c) Computable proxy from public ETF constituent files — **this is the live path**
The honest, buildable proxy is **"share of a broad HY ETF's holdings trading at a distressed yield/spread."** The decisive question is which ETF's holdings file actually carries a *per-bond* yield or spread field. I downloaded both candidate files and verified:

- **iShares HYG — VIABLE.** The iShares US holdings file exposes a full per-bond schema including `Price`, `YTM (%)`, `Yield to Call (%)`, `Yield to Worst (%)`, `Coupon (%)`, `Par Value`, `Duration`, `Maturity`, `Weight (%)`, `CUSIP/ISIN/SEDOL` — the same header set confirmed on the iShares HYG/EMB product pages ([iShares HYG holdings header](https://www.ishares.com/ch/professionals/en/products/239565/ishares-iboxx-high-yield-corporate-bond-etf)). With per-bond `Yield to Worst (%)` and `Weight (%)`, you can compute a weighted "share of portfolio with YTW − Treasury benchmark ≥ 1000bp" or, more simply and honestly, "share of holdings with YTW above an absolute distressed threshold." The legacy programmatic URL template is `{product_url}/1467271812596.ajax?fileType=csv&fileName=HYG_holdings&dataType=fund` (HYG product id `239565`), the same template used by the open-source `etf_scraper` library ([etf-scraper on PyPI](https://pypi.org/project/etf-scraper/); [ETF-Scraper source, iShares scraper](https://github.com/nikulpatel3141/ETF-Scraper)).
  - **Fragility flag (important):** when I fetched that endpoint live it returned `Content-Type: text/csv` but a ~2.7 MB **HTML consent-wrapped body** rather than raw CSV — iShares has begun gating the legacy ajax endpoint behind a JavaScript/consent layer. The holdings data is still present (embedded as JSON within the page; the `Yield to Worst` string is locatable in the payload), but a plain `requests.get` no longer reliably yields a clean CSV. This makes HYG a **medium-fragility** source that may require a headless browser or parsing the embedded JSON. Treat the clean-CSV behavior as "works until it doesn't."
- **SPDR JNK — NOT VIABLE for distress.** I downloaded the SSGA daily holdings spreadsheet (`https://www.ssga.com/us/en/intermediary/library-content/products/fund-data/etfs/us/holdings-daily-us-en-jnk.xlsx`, a clean 127 KB `.xlsx`, HTTP 200). Its columns are only **`Name, Identifier, SEDOL, Weight, Coupon, Par Value, Market Value, Local Currency, Maturity`** — **no yield, no YTW, no spread, no price**. JNK therefore **cannot** produce a distress ratio. This corrects the brief's implication that "SPDR JNK" is a candidate proxy for this metric; it is a clean programmatic file but lacks the necessary field.
- **Net:** the only ETF-derived distress proxy that is honestly computable from a public daily file is **HYG-based**, and only because iShares ships per-bond `Yield to Worst (%)`.

Definitional caveat to label precisely: a HYG-derived proxy is *not* the J.P. Morgan/ICE distress ratio. HYG tracks the iBoxx $ Liquid High Yield Index (a liquid subset, ~1,300 bonds vs the full ICE/JPM universe of ~1,800+), it uses YTW vs a benchmark rather than ICE's OAS engine, and "distressed" must be defined by you (e.g., YTW spread ≥ 1000bp computed against an interpolated Treasury, or a price/absolute-yield cutoff). It will systematically *understate* the true distress ratio because the liquid index excludes the smallest, most distressed names.

### (d) Scheduled publications quotable on a cadence — **best PRIMARY**
- **Eaton Vance / Morgan Stanley IM — High Yield Market Monitor (quarterly PDF).** Carries the J.P. Morgan distress ratio with the exact ≥1000bp spread-to-worst definition printed on the source line ([Eaton Vance HY Market Monitor Q1 2026 PDF](https://www.eatonvance.com/content/dam/im/assets/publication/thought-leadership/article/article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q12026.pdf?1778544000225)). URL pattern: `.../article_highyieldmarketmonitor_q{Q}{YYYY}.pdf`. Fetch format: PDF (text-extractable). Cadence: quarterly, ~4–6 weeks after quarter-end. **Fragility note:** the host returns HTTP 403 to non-browser user-agents — fetchable but requires browser-like headers; the quarterly filename token also has to be incremented.
- **S&P Global Market Intelligence — Fridson monthly recap (HTML article).** Republishes FridsonVision's monthly distress ratio in prose, e.g. "On Oct. 31, the U.S. distress ratio was 1.68%, down from 2.00% one month earlier… defined as the percentage of issues in the ICE BofAML U.S. High Yield Index quoted at distressed levels (OAS of +1,000 bps or more)" ([S&P Global, Fridson recap](https://www.spglobal.com/marketintelligence/en/news-insights/latest-news-headlines/fridson-monthly-high-yield-recap-reveals-conflicting-market-signals-67402398)). Fetch format: HTML + selector / regex on the article body. Cadence: monthly (mid-month, for prior month-end). **Fragility:** medium — article URLs are unique per month (not a stable single poll URL), so you must discover the latest article via the S&P LCD/Fridson tag page rather than poll one address.
- **FridsonVision primary letter (paid PDF).** The number originates here ([FridsonVision High Yield Strategy sample PDF](https://fridson.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Fridson-High-Yield-Strategy.pdf)); gated/paid, so not a free poll target, but it confirms provenance and the ICE Distressed HY Index linkage.
- **Adjacent, fully-FRED-able context tile:** if you want a *daily, machine-readable* companion that needs no proxy gymnastics, the aggregate ICE BofA US HY OAS `BAMLH0A0HYM2` updates daily on FRED ([FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)) and FRED has a clean JSON/CSV API. It is *not* the distress ratio but is the standard "stress thermometer" and is honest, fresh, and unbreakable.

### Recommendation — Metric 1
- **PRIMARY:** Scheduled-publication scrape of the **S&P Global Market Intelligence Fridson monthly recap** (HTML), backed by the **Eaton Vance/Morgan Stanley HY Market Monitor** quarterly PDF for the J.P. Morgan variant. These carry the real, named figure.
- **FALLBACK:** **HYG-derived proxy** computed from iShares per-bond `Yield to Worst (%)` + `Weight (%)`, recomputed daily — used only when the latest publication is stale, and clearly marked as an approximation.
- **Honest tile label:** *"US HY distress ratio — % of issues with OAS ≥ 1000 bp, as published by FridsonVision / ICE BofA, [month-end date]"* for the primary; for the fallback, *"ETF-derived distress proxy: share of iShares HYG holdings with yield-to-worst spread ≥ 1000 bp; computed [date], not the J.P. Morgan/ICE distress ratio."*

## Metric 2 — CDX.NA.IG and CDX.NA.HY On-the-Run Index Levels

This is the metric where the **regulatory public-dissemination channel is not just viable but is a genuine, free, programmatic, near-real-time feed.** I verified it end-to-end by downloading and parsing the actual file.

### (a) Direct / delayed feeds
The official Markit/S&P index fixings sit behind registered-user walls, confirming the brief. Public aggregators do **not** carry the real index: the TradingView/exchange ticker "CDX" resolves to an unrelated equity fund (e.g. an AMEX-listed fund trading ~$22, and an ASX micro-cap), not the credit index ([TradingView AMEX:CDX](https://www.tradingview.com/symbols/AMEX-CDX/)). Investing.com's "CDX" page is likewise a site-specific construct. **No clean third-party feed of the true on-the-run level exists for free.**

### (b) Regulatory public-dissemination channel — **the working PRIMARY**
U.S. swap rules (CEA §2(a)(13), CFTC Part 43) require SDRs to **publicly disseminate index-CDS trade prints in real time, in a machine-readable, downloadable format** ([CFTC final rule 2020-21568](https://www.cftc.gov/LawRegulation/FederalRegister/finalrules/2020-21568.html)). DTCC's Public Price Dissemination platform (DDR) is the venue ([DTCC Public Reporting](https://www.dtcc.com/public-reporting)).

**Exact fetch path (verified live):**
- **Cumulative daily file (recommended):** `https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api/report/cumulative/cftc/CFTC_CUMULATIVE_CREDITS_{YYYY}_{MM}_{DD}.zip` — I fetched several dates successfully (HTTP 200, ~70–85 KB zipped). The zip contains a single CSV `CFTC_CUMULATIVE_CREDITS_{date}.csv`.
- **Intraday slice files (near-real-time):** the dashboard at `https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/` lists `CFTC_SLICE_CREDITS_{date}_{n}.zip` slices published every few seconds-to-minutes; same API base, `.../api/report/slices/cftc/...` ([Actrix: Analysing CFTC SDR Public Dissemination Data](https://actrixft.com/analysing-cftc-sdr-public-dissemination-data/); historical SEC/Superstonk documentation of the identical `pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api/report/...` URL family ([Reddit r/Superstonk SDR download method](https://www.reddit.com/r/Superstonk/comments/1e5jqjz/export_of_the_gme_swap_data_up_until_yesterday/))).
- **Fetch format:** zipped CSV, ~150 columns. **Cadence/lag:** cumulative file builds through the trading day and finalizes EOD; intraday slices appear within seconds. Individual prints carry a **15-minute** public-dissemination delay for the relevant swap categories under 17 CFR §43.5 ([Cornell LII 17 CFR §43.5](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/17/43.5)). So this is **T-0 intraday with a 15-minute trade-level lag** — far fresher than any publication.

**How to isolate CDX.NA.IG / CDX.NA.HY on-the-run (verified against the 2026-06-12 file, 1,621 credit records):**
- Filter `Asset Class = "CR"` and `UPI Underlier Name = "CDX.NA.IG"` or `"CDX.NA.HY"`. (The plain `Product name` / `Underlier ID-Leg 1` columns are *empty* in the current schema — the index is identified by the **`UPI Underlier Name`** and `UPI FISN = "NA/CDS Corp Idx"` fields. This corrects any assumption that you'd key off `Product name`.)
- **On-the-run = the dominant `Expiration Date` cluster + single dominant UPI.** In the June 2026 file, the 5-year on-the-run is `Expiration Date = 2031-06-20`: it accounted for 289 of 305 CDX.NA.IG prints (UPI `QZ3WVVQG5T8K`) and 236 of 247 CDX.NA.HY prints (UPI `QZHH8NT767RX`). Off-the-run series and 10Y tenors fall in other expiration buckets. Because series roll each March/September (the brief is correct), the on-the-run expiration date rolls to the next 20-Jun/20-Dec quarterly date.
- **Pricing fields and a critical divergence from the brief:**
  - **CDX.NA.IG** disseminates as **spread** in `Spread-Leg 1` (decimal; `Spread notation-Leg 1 = 3`). On 2026-06-12 the on-the-run IG cluster had a **median spread of 50.7 bp** (0.00507), tightly clustered 50.4–51.1 bp across 268 quotes — a clean, usable level. `Fixed rate-Leg 1 = 0.01` confirms the 100bp coupon convention.
  - **CDX.NA.HY** — **contrary to the brief's "HY quotes in price" assumption, the U.S. SDR feed carries HY as SPREAD, not price.** Across all 247 HY rows the `Price` column was **empty (0 non-null)**; the level lives in `Spread-Leg 1`, with `Fixed rate-Leg 1 = 0.05` confirming the 500bp coupon. On 2026-06-12 the on-the-run HY 5Y had a **median spread of 108.2 bp**. The market-convention **dollar price** (e.g. ~$107) is **not** in this feed; if your tile needs the price quote you must convert spread→price yourself (upfront/price is a function of spread, coupon, and the index factor) or source it elsewhere. This is the single most important "gotcha" for this metric.
  - `Index factor` (`0.98–0.99` for HY, `1` for IG) reflects names removed by default — you should display the factor or note it, because a sub-1 factor signals the series has taken defaults.
- **Block-trade caps (the key data-quality gotcha):** large trades are flagged `Block trade election indicator = TRUE` and their notional is masked. I observed notional reported as **`"250,000,000+"`** and `"230,000,000"` block prints. Under the CFTC's post-2024 Part 43 schedule the relevant 5Y caps are: **CDX.IG block $200M / cap $250M; CDX.HY block $50M / cap $76M** (tenor bucket 1,477D–2,207D) ([CFTC Post-Initial Block & Cap Sizes, May 23 2024](https://www.cftc.gov/media/10716/Part43BlockThresholdsCapSizes052324/download); [Katten analysis of 2024 block/cap update](https://quickreads.ext.katten.com/post/102jan7/redefining-limits-the-cftcs-updated-block-and-cap-sizes-for-2024)). Capped notional doesn't distort the *price/spread* (which is still published in full), only the size — so for a level tile this is benign, but you must not sum notionals naively.
- **Cleared vs uncleared:** `Cleared = "I"` (intent-to-clear/cleared) vs `"N"` (uncleared). Both are disseminated; uncleared bilateral prints can be off-market, so use the cleared cluster and take a robust statistic.
- **Robustification:** I found one HY outlier of `1082450 bp` (a malformed/erroneous `Spread-Leg 1`), so **use the median, not the mean**, and clip to a sane band. With that, the daily on-the-run IG and HY 5Y levels are cleanly recoverable.

**UPI → series resolution:** each UPI maps to a specific index/series/tenor; the human-readable mapping (e.g., which series number `QZHH8NT767RX` is) is resolvable via the ANNA-DSB UPI service ([ANNA-DSB UPI](https://www.anna-dsb.com/upi-); [ANNA-DSB "How to search for a UPI" guide](https://www.anna-dsb.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/The-UPI-How-to-search-for-and-create-a-UPI.pdf)). For a level tile you don't strictly need the series number — the on-the-run is identifiable purely from the dominant expiration/UPI cluster — but the DSB lookup lets you label the exact series (subscription/registration may apply for full attribute search).

**Alternative regulatory venue:** ICE Trade Vault also runs an SDR with a public real-time ticker at `www.icetradevault.com` for credit/IRS/FX/commodity swaps ([ICE SDR Rulebook, CFTC filing](https://www.cftc.gov/sites/default/files/filings/orgrules/16/07/rule072616icesdrsdr005.pdf)) — a secondary mirror if DTCC's format changes, though DTCC carries the large majority of index-CDS volume.

### (c) ETF-constituent proxy
Not meaningfully applicable — there is no single ETF that *is* CDX.NA.IG/HY. (Some funds hold CDX positions, but you cannot back out the index level from an ETF NAV.) Skip.

### (d) Scheduled publications
Sell-side credit strategy notes quote CDX levels but are gated and not on a pollable cadence. Octus/LevFin weeklies occasionally cite CDX in prose but unreliably. Not a primary path; the SDR feed dominates.

### Recommendation — Metric 2
- **PRIMARY:** **DTCC PPD CFTC CREDITS feed**, filtering `UPI Underlier Name ∈ {CDX.NA.IG, CDX.NA.HY}`, on-the-run = dominant expiration/UPI cluster, taking the **median `Spread-Leg 1`** of cleared prints. URL: `https://pddata.dtcc.com/ppd/api/report/cumulative/cftc/CFTC_CUMULATIVE_CREDITS_{YYYY}_{MM}_{DD}.zip`. Display **spread (bp)** for both IG and HY (do not promise a HY dollar price unless you convert it yourself).
- **FALLBACK:** ICE Trade Vault public SDR ticker if DTCC's format/URL breaks.
- **Honest tile label:** *"CDX.NA.IG / CDX.NA.HY 5Y on-the-run — median SDR trade print (spread, bp), cleared, [date/time], 15-min delayed; source: DTCC public price dissemination (CFTC Part 43). Not the official Markit/S&P fixing."* If you publish HY in price, add *"price converted from disseminated spread."*

## Metric 3 — EMBI Global Diversified Spread (J.P. Morgan EM Sovereign)

### What the canonical figure is
The J.P. Morgan **EMBI Global Diversified (EMBIGD)** sovereign blended spread, quoted in bp. Fund fact sheets that benchmark to it report it monthly, e.g. an Option-Adjusted Spread of **285 bp** ([Fort Washington EM Debt 1Q2026 commentary](https://www.westernsouthern.com/-/media/files/fortwashington/fund-literature/fw-1052-em-commentary.pdf)) and TCW's "EMBI Global Diversified Sovereign Spreads" chart ([TCW EM Income fund characteristics](https://www.tcw.com/-/media/Downloads/com/Products/US-Funds/TCW-Funds/Monthly-Characteristics/CHAR-EIF.pdf/1000)).

### (a) Direct / delayed feeds
**No free programmatic feed of EMBIGD spread**, confirming the brief. FRED's `BAMLEMCBPIOAS` is EM *corporate*, not sovereign, and there is no FRED sovereign-EMBI series. **Dead end at the feed level.**

### (b) Regulatory public dissemination
Not applicable — EMBIGD is a cash-bond sovereign index, not a swap; no SDR/SEF channel disseminates it. (CDX.EM index-CDS prints *do* appear in the same DTCC CREDITS feed — `UPI Underlier Name` for emerging-markets CDS — but CDX.EM is a CDS basket of a handful of sovereigns and is **not** the EMBIGD cash spread; do not substitute one for the other.)

### (c) Computable proxy from public ETF constituent files — the live path, with a definitional asterisk
- **iShares EMB — best proxy.** EMB's daily holdings file carries the richest per-bond schema of any of these ETFs: `Price, YTM (%), Yield to Worst (%), Yield to Call (%), Real YTM (%), Coupon (%), Par Value, Market Value, Duration, Mod. Duration, Maturity, Weight (%), CUSIP/ISIN/SEDOL, Location` ([iShares EMB holdings header](https://www.blackrock.com/us/individual/products/239572/ishares-jp-morgan-usd-emerging-markets-bond-etf)). With per-bond `Yield to Worst (%)` and `Weight (%)` you can compute a **weighted portfolio yield**, and (subtracting a duration-matched Treasury) a **portfolio-level EM-sovereign spread proxy**. URL template: `{product_url}/1467271812596.ajax?fileType=csv&fileName=EMB_holdings&dataType=fund` (EMB product id `239572`).
  - **Same fragility as HYG:** my live fetch returned `text/csv` content-type but a consent-wrapped HTML/JSON body, not clean CSV. Medium fragility; the YTW data is present but may need JSON extraction or a headless browser.
  - **Definitional divergence (must be labeled):** EMB now tracks the **J.P. Morgan EMBI Global *Core* Index**, *not* EMBI Global *Diversified* ([iShares EMB benchmark = "J.P. Morgan EMBI Global Core Index"](https://www.ishares.com/ch/professionals/en/products/239572/ishares-jp-morgan-usd-emerging-markets-bond-etf)). Core and Diversified differ in their issuer-capping rules, so an EMB-derived spread is a *cousin* of EMBIGD, not EMBIGD itself.
- **A cleaner, near-canonical public number:** iShares' own EMB product page publishes a portfolio **Option Adjusted Spread** directly — I observed **170.24 bp as of 04/Jun/2026** on the EMB professional page ([iShares EMB, "Option Adjusted Spread 170.24"](https://www.ishares.com/ch/professionals/en/products/239572/ishares-jp-morgan-usd-emerging-markets-bond-etf)). This is a published, fund-level OAS you can scrape via HTML+selector, no per-bond math required — though it is the *Core*-tracking fund's OAS, and the US blackrock.com page does not always surface the OAS tile (the value appears on the CH/professional and characteristics views). Lower fragility than the holdings file for a single number; the divergence-from-Diversified caveat still applies.

### (d) Scheduled publications — most accurate for the *named* index
A broad set of EM-debt manager fact sheets quote the **EMBIGD** spread monthly with ~2–4 week lag and a stable landing URL:
- **Fort Washington EM Debt commentary (quarterly PDF):** "J.P. Morgan EMBI Global Diversified… Option Adjusted Spread 285" ([Fort Washington 1Q2026](https://www.westernsouthern.com/-/media/files/fortwashington/fund-literature/fw-1052-em-commentary.pdf)).
- **TCW Emerging Markets Income (monthly PDF):** plots and states "EMBI Global Diversified Sovereign Spreads (bps)," e.g. "Spreads on the EM sovereign index… ended the month at 381 bps" ([TCW CHAR-EIF](https://www.tcw.com/-/media/Downloads/com/Products/US-Funds/TCW-Funds/Monthly-Characteristics/CHAR-EIF.pdf/1000)).
- **Vontobel / GSAM EM debt fact sheets** quote the same OAS monthly ([Vontobel EM Debt fact sheet](https://am.vontobel.com/en/document/345fad7b-042c-4dfb-91e4-6c391f5a832e/Emerging-Markets-Debt-Strategy-Fact-Sheet_20240930_EN.pdf); [GSAM EM Debt positioning](https://www.gsam.com/content/dam/gsam/pdfs/us/en/fund-resources/portfolio-positioning/EMDD_PP.pdf?sa=n&rd=n)).
- **World Bank** publishes EMBIG composite spread charts/data in its Global Economic Prospects and prospects-group notes, including spreadsheets ([World Bank GEP June-2023 EMBIG spread XLSX](https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/5443e6bba11cd7fa7c0c678a20edd4dd-0350012023/related/GEP-June-2023-Chapter3-Fig3-6.xlsx); [World Bank prospects note referencing EMBIG spreads](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/360851468336713164/txt/493800NEWS0Ext101OFFICIAL0USE0ONLY1.txt)) — but these are EMBIG *Global* composite, periodic, and lagged, useful only as a sanity cross-check.

**Fragility of the publications:** each is a unique per-period filename and several manager hosts (and the Eaton Vance host) return 403 to non-browser agents, so you must poll a known fact-sheet landing page and parse the latest PDF with browser-like headers. No single manager is "the" source, so build a small panel and take the most recent.

### Recommendation — Metric 3
- **PRIMARY:** Scheduled-publication scrape of a small panel of EM-debt fact sheets (**TCW / Fort Washington**) for the explicitly-named **EMBI Global Diversified** OAS, monthly.
- **FALLBACK:** **EMB-derived proxy** — either the scraped fund-level **Option Adjusted Spread** off the iShares page (low effort) or a weighted **Yield-to-Worst-minus-Treasury** spread computed from EMB's per-bond holdings (more work, daily). Used when the monthly publication is stale.
- **Honest tile label:** *"EMBI Global Diversified sovereign spread — as published [manager], [month-end]"* for primary; for fallback, *"ETF-derived EM-sovereign spread proxy from iShares EMB (tracks J.P. Morgan EMBI Global Core, not Diversified); fund OAS / holdings-weighted YTW spread, [date]."*

## Metric 4 — Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan Index (level / spread)

### What the canonical figure is
The **Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan Index** (rebranded from S&P/LSTA in Aug 2022), a market-value-weighted index of institutional leveraged loans, **priced daily at 3:00 PM ET** using PitchBook LCD constituent prices ([Morningstar Leveraged Loan Indexes Methodology PDF](https://indexes.morningstar.com/api/docs/69bd3e9250ca695336d4045f); [Morningstar LSTA index documentation](https://indexes.morningstar.com/api/docs/68f772ac36fedc63abff8f4a)).

### (a) Direct / delayed feeds — **a genuinely good public path exists**
- **Morningstar Indexes public screener (HTML) — PRIMARY candidate.** The Morningstar Indexes screener displays the **index level, daily return, and trailing returns with an as-of date, with no login**. I confirmed it live: the screener returned **Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan = 4,351.35, daily +0.06%, as of 06/13/26**, and **Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan 100 = 3,189.04, as of 06/13/26** ([Morningstar Indexes screener](https://indexes.morningstar.com/indexes/screener?filterPattern=Leveraged+Loan)). Fetch format: HTML (the screener is a client-side React app that renders the values; a fetch-and-extract or headless render both surface the number — the level was extractable on fetch). Cadence: **daily**, refreshed after the 3 PM ET pricing. Units: index level (price-return / total-return level depending on the row). **This is the freshest free source for the level.**
  - **Fragility:** medium. The screener is JS-rendered; the underlying JSON XHR endpoint is not stably documented publicly (I probed several `indexes.morningstar.com/api/...` paths and got 404s — the data API requires the app's own session/token discovery), so the robust approach is HTML extraction of the rendered table or a headless browser, both of which are sensitive to front-end redesigns. Per-index landing pages also exist under `indexes.morningstar.com` for a more targeted poll.
- **Aggregator confirmations of the level** (useful as cross-checks, not primary): Octus reported "the LSTA Leveraged Loan Index was last indicated at 99.02" (the *average bid price*, a different unit than the index level) ([Octus Americas LevFin Weekly](https://octus.com/resources/articles/americas-leveraged-finance-weekly-lbo-transactions-come-to-market-amid-open-primary-full-of-refinancings/)); Investing.com cited daily index moves ([Investing.com on Morningstar LSTA index](https://www.investing.com/news/economy-news/us-junk-debt-investors-cautious-of-leveraged-loans-as-economy-slows-3570934)).

### (b) Regulatory public dissemination
Not applicable — leveraged loans are not swaps; no SDR/SEF channel. (Loan-CDS/LCDX is largely defunct.) Skip.

### (c) ETF-constituent proxy
- **Invesco BKLN — partial.** BKLN (Invesco Senior Loan ETF) tracks the **Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan 100 Index** and its product page even prints the index's own trailing returns ([Invesco BKLN product detail, "Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan 100 Index-TR"](https://www.invesco.com/us/financial-products/etfs/product-detail?audienceType=Advisor&productId=ETF-BKLN)). However, BKLN's holdings download is **not** cleanly programmatic the way SSGA's is: my attempts at the documented Invesco holdings/download endpoints returned the SPA HTML shell (HTTP 200, `text/html`), not a CSV. BKLN can give you a *price-level* proxy (NAV/loan prices) but loans lack a clean per-instrument "spread" field in a public file, so a discount-margin proxy is not honestly computable from BKLN alone. Treat BKLN as a directional cross-check, not a spread source.
- The "100" index (BKLN's benchmark) ≠ the broad index; if you proxy via BKLN you'd be tracking the 100-largest-loans subindex, which the Morningstar screener also publishes directly — so just take the screener value rather than the ETF proxy.

### (d) Scheduled publications — strong FALLBACK and the source of the *spread/DM*
- **LSTA monthly index analysis (HTML, stable URL pattern) — best for prose level + context.** LSTA posts a monthly "Morningstar LSTA Leveraged Loan Index Analysis," e.g. the February 2026 post (returns, YTD, sector color) at a stable, pollable slug ([LSTA Morningstar LSTA Leveraged Loan Index Analysis, February 2026](https://www.lsta.org/content/morningstar-lsta-leveraged-loan-index-analysis-february-2026/)) — I confirmed HTTP 200. URL pattern: `.../content/morningstar-lsta-leveraged-loan-index-analysis-{month}-{year}/`. Cadence: monthly, early in the following month.
- **S&P Global Market Intelligence / LCD daily news (HTML).** Publishes the daily index move and the "3-year discount margin" — the spread measure the brief asks about — e.g. "Loans gained 0.28% today… Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan 100… gained 0.32%" ([S&P Global, daily Morningstar LSTA loan index recap](https://www.spglobal.com/marketintelligence/en/news-insights/latest-news-headlines/morningstar-lsta-us-leveraged-loan-index-loans-gain-0-28-ytd-return-is-0-94-12495495)). These daily recaps are the practical home of the **discount-margin/spread** quote; URLs are unique per day, so discover via the LCD news tag rather than poll one address.
- **Octus / LevFin weeklies** quote the average bid price (~99 handle) weekly ([Octus LevFin Weekly](https://octus.com/resources/articles/americas-leveraged-finance-weekly-lbo-transactions-come-to-market-amid-open-primary-full-of-refinancings/)) — note this is *average bid price*, not the index level (~4,351) nor the DM; label the unit carefully.

### Recommendation — Metric 4
- **PRIMARY (level):** **Morningstar Indexes public screener** HTML extraction of the **Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan** index level, daily ([Morningstar Indexes screener](https://indexes.morningstar.com/indexes/screener?filterPattern=Leveraged+Loan)).
- **FALLBACK (level + spread):** **LSTA monthly index-analysis post** (HTML, stable slug) for the monthly level/return, and the **S&P Global/LCD daily recap** for the level move and the **3-yr discount margin** when you want the spread rather than the level.
- **Honest tile label:** *"Morningstar LSTA US Leveraged Loan Index — level [4,351.35], as of [date], source Morningstar Indexes (PitchBook LCD pricing, 3 PM ET)."* If you show spread, *"3-yr discount margin as reported by S&P Global Market Intelligence / LCD, [date]."* Never relabel the ~99 average bid price as the index level.

### The three findings that most change the build
1. **CDX is the easy one, not the hard one.** A free, programmatic, ~real-time on-the-run level for **both** CDX.NA.IG and CDX.NA.HY is sitting in the DTCC public CFTC dissemination file — I extracted IG ≈ 50.7 bp and HY ≈ 108 bp medians for 12-Jun-2026 directly. The catch is that **HY is disseminated as spread, not price** (the brief's "HY in price" prior is wrong for this feed), block notionals are capped, and you must median-filter erroneous prints.
2. **For the distress ratio, HYG works but JNK does not** — JNK's public holdings file has no yield/spread column at all, while HYG/EMB ship per-bond `Yield to Worst (%)`. And the truly *named* figure only lives in scheduled publications (Fridson/S&P, Eaton Vance), not any feed.
3. **The Morningstar LSTA level is publicly visible** on the Morningstar Indexes screener with a daily as-of date — the gated "performance pages" are not the only door. The **spread (3-yr discount margin)**, however, only appears in S&P/LCD daily prose, not as a free number.

Nothing among the four is a true dead end at the "does a fetchable number exist" standard. The aggregate FRED HY OAS is the only piece that is itself a clean API but is *not* the requested metric (it's the nearest honest substitute for the distress ratio's level context, not its share-above-1000bp).
