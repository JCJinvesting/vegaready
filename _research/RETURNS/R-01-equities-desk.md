## Framing: what makes an equities desk durable across regimes

Durability is not a property of having more indicators — it is a property of the framework's **epistemic structure**. Three principles run through every subsection below and define the spine of the desk:

1. **Classify the channel before the direction.** Funding/liquidity/leverage shocks reprice crowded and levered books *first* through margin and collateral mechanics. Earnings/demand shocks reprice cyclical cash-flow exposure *first* through revised forecasts. A desk that fails to classify the channel will price the second-round move using the first-round playbook and get the factor ordering wrong.
2. **Label observable vs inferred honestly.** Some signals (HY OAS, breadth, VIX term structure) are free, measurable, and reproducible. Others (CTA positioning, dealer gamma, blackout impact) are vendor estimates that embed modelling assumptions. The framework must publish quantitative claims for the first and qualitative claims for the second, and label which is which.
3. **Pre-commit to falsifiers.** A regime read that cannot be proven wrong is a narrative, not a framework. Every central claim needs a dated, observable, free-data condition under which the desk will abandon it.

The brief's framing — that an equities desk is the *last expression* of a cross-asset balance-sheet system rather than a self-contained EPS-discounting machine ([FSB Holistic Review of March 2020](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) — is the framework's load-bearing assumption [confidence: **well-established**]. The working hierarchy is: first identify whether the catalyst impairs funding or collateral; second map the first-round equity expression through size, liquidity, quality, beta, and sector input-cost exposure; third wait for earnings revisions only after the commodity, freight, claims, or appropriations channel is measurable in company guidance ([Agarwal & Hess, SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: **well-established**].

## F.1 — Factor/style rotation under a funding-stress catalyst

### The structural claim

When the shock arrives through a funding/liquidity/leverage channel rather than an earnings/demand channel, *the first thing to reprice is not the cheapest or most cyclical stock — it is the most crowded and most levered book.* The sell order is driven by margin and collateral mechanics, not by a revised view of cash flows, so the cross-sectional pattern is "sell what is liquid and what is owned," not "sell what is economically exposed." All three models converge on this distinction [confidence: **well-established**].

### The empirical backbone — Fama-French factor returns across six funding-stress windows

GPT-5.5 computed Fama-French daily-five-factor returns directly from the [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html) across the six canonical funding-stress episodes. The table is the empirical anchor for the rest of F.1 [confidence: **well-established**]:

| Episode | Market | SMB | HML | RMW | Defensive ETFs − Cyclical ETFs | Key interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| LTCM / Russia, Aug–Sep 1998 | −10.86% | −7.14% | +0.51% | +2.28% | n/a | Liquidity shock punished small/illiquid; modestly rewarded profitability ([Fed History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/ltcm-near-failure)) |
| GFC funding panic, Sep–Nov 2008 | −42.05% | −9.18% | −7.47% | +13.18% | +21.25 pts | Quality and defensives were the cleanest equity expressions of funding stress ([Fed History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/great-recession-and-its-aftermath)) |
| Euro sovereign, Jul–Oct 2011 | −17.84% | −9.29% | −5.36% | +9.19% | +16.14 pts | Traded like a funding/sovereign-risk shock, not a simple earnings slowdown ([ECB](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/key/date/2011/html/sp110222.en.html)) |
| COVID dash-for-cash, Feb–Mar 2020 | −33.98% | −7.53% | −17.92% | **−2.90%** | +14.33 pts | Broad liquidation overwhelmed quality early; defensives still beat cyclicals relatively ([FSB](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) |
| UK LDI / gilt crisis, Sep–Oct 2022 | −5.54% | +1.17% | +5.20% | +4.19% | −4.27 pts | Rates/collateral shock localised in gilts; equity factors barely moved ([BoE WP](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/-/media/boe/files/working-paper/2023/an-anatomy-of-the-2022-gilt-market-crisis.pdf)) [confidence: **contested** as a general equity analogue] |
| SVB deposit flight, Mar 2023 | −1.32% | −5.54% | −7.68% | +3.56% | +7.00 pts | Deposit/financials stress punished small and value; rewarded quality ([Fed OIG](https://oig.federalreserve.gov/reports/board-material-loss-review-silicon-valley-bank-sep2023.pdf)) |

**The single most important empirical finding in this table is the negative RMW in March 2020** — quality (high profitability) did *not* defend in absolute terms during the acute dash-for-cash window. This falsifies the popular practitioner rule that "quality always defends in a crisis" and is one of the principal reasons funding shocks must be sub-classified rather than treated monolithically [confidence: **well-established**].

### Three named historical analogues

**Analogue 1 — August 2007 "Quant Quake" (the cleanest pure-funding-channel factor event).** Khandani & Lo document that quantitative equity market-neutral funds suffered unprecedented losses Aug 6–9, 2007, with month-to-date losses of −5% to −30% for some of the most consistently profitable funds, followed by a sharp partial reversal on Aug 10 ([Khandani & Lo, MIT](https://web.mit.edu/Alo/www/Papers/august07.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**]. Critically, the factors that broke were *value and the classic fundamental long/short factors* (book-to-market, earnings-to-price, cash-flow-to-market), which had been quietly bleeding since July, while *price and earnings momentum factors actually rose into the event* ([ScienceDirect summary](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386418110000261)) [confidence: **well-established**]. The "Unwind Hypothesis" — that a multi-strategy desk liquidated to meet margin calls from a deteriorating subprime book, forcing similar books to deleverage — is the canonical model for how a credit/funding stress transmits into equity factor space even when equity fundamentals are unchanged. The framework lesson: in a funding shock, the *value factor and crowded long/short books* reprice first and most violently, and the move is **mean-reverting** within days, unlike an earnings-driven value selloff.

**Analogue 2 — 1998 LTCM (liquidity as a priced factor).** The LTCM collapse — a $4.6bn loss in under four months and a 14-firm $3.625bn recapitalisation on 23 September 1998 — is the archetype of a flight-to-liquidity in which the spread between liquid and illiquid securities is itself the priced risk factor ([Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/ltcm-near-failure); [Bauer case study](https://www.bauer.uh.edu/rsusmel/7386/ltcm-2.htm)) [confidence: **well-established**]. The equity read-through is that in 1998 the repricing ran liquid-over-illiquid and large-over-small (SMB = −7.14% in the Aug–Sep window) [confidence: **well-established** for the SMB number; **contested** as to whether large/small dispersion is the *general* equity expression of a liquidity shock across all such episodes].

**Analogue 3 — March 2020 dash-for-cash.** The FSB's review explicitly separates two phases: a "flight to safety" (late Feb–early March, sell risky/buy safe — the earnings-channel response) that morphed into a "dash for cash" (mid-March), in which investors sold *even the safest and most liquid assets* (Treasuries) to raise cash, breaking the normal stock-bond correlation ([FSB Holistic Review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**]. US prime MMF outflows reached $125bn, government/Treasury MMF inflows exceeded $800bn, record mid-March bond-fund outflows hit $109bn, the S&P 500 lost 12% on 16 March, and the VIX peaked at 83 [confidence: **well-established**]. The diagnostic tell that a shock has crossed from earnings-channel to funding-channel is exactly this: Treasuries and equities fall together. In factor terms, March 2020 saw defensives outperform cyclicals by +14.33 percentage points in relative terms while quality drew down −2.90% in absolute terms — the relative-vs-absolute reconciliation matters because either framing alone misleads.

### The instructive negative case — 2022 UK LDI/gilt crisis

The 2022 LDI episode is the boundary case that disciplines the framework. The Bank of England estimates LDI forced-selling accounted for roughly half the gilt-price decline after the 23 September 2022 mini-budget, with 30-year gilt yields rising over 100bp in four days and pension LDI leverage spiking from below 2.0× to 2.7× ([Bank Underground](https://bankunderground.co.uk/2024/07/26/what-caused-the-ldi-crisis/); [AllianceBernstein](https://www.alliancebernstein.com/apac/en/institutions/insights/investment-insights/long-run-global-implications-of-the-uks-ldi-crisis.html)) [confidence: **well-established**]. State Street's portfolio-fragility metrics show the systemic-risk contribution came overwhelmingly from gilts, corporate bonds, and property — interest-rate assets — *not equity* ([State Street](https://www.statestreet.com/web/insights/articles/documents/the-uk-ldi-crisis.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**]. In the Fama-French data, SMB was actually *positive* (+1.17%), HML positive (+5.20%), defensives *under*performed cyclicals (−4.27 pts).

The framework lesson is a falsifier-grade insight: **a funding shock localised in one collateral chain (long gilts/repo) need not produce a global equity-factor rotation.** A durable desk must therefore ask "which collateral chain?" before assuming a funding shock generalises. Gemini's classification of LDI as a generic funding analogue is the weaker reading; the GPT-5.5/Opus reading that LDI is a boundary case is better supported by the data.

### Synthesis — the regime rule (where the models disagree, and what to adopt)

The three models diverge on what reprices *first* in a funding shock. Gemini 3.1 Pro asserts a clean rule: Momentum and levered value break first, Quality outperforms immediately. GPT-5.5 demonstrates empirically that Small/illiquid (SMB) breaks first and that Quality (RMW) was the *exception* (negative) in March 2020. Claude Opus 4.8 argues that crowded long/short books reprice first and the large/small axis is second, citing the Quant Quake as the cleanest mechanism.

**Adopt the Opus framing** — funding shocks are not monolithic, and the ordering of what reprices first depends on *which collateral chain* is impaired:

1. **Quant-Quake-style unwind** (margin call on a crowded multi-strategy book): value factor and crowded long/short books reprice first, momentum *rises*, mean-reverts within days.
2. **LTCM-style flight-to-liquidity** (single levered participant fails, dealer balance sheets compress): liquid-over-illiquid and large-over-small reprice first, SMB strongly negative.
3. **March-2020-style dash-for-cash** (everyone wants cash simultaneously): everything correlates to one, defensives outperform *relatively* but quality drops *absolutely*, stock-bond correlation flips positive.
4. **LDI-style collateral-chain shock** (single asset class breaks, contained by central-bank backstop): equity factors barely move; the shock stays in the impaired collateral chain.

Energy and geopolitical shocks behave differently again — they hit sector earnings directly and so look more like F.2's earnings channel, with energy producers and energy-cost-exposed transports repricing on the fundamental axis. [Confidence on this four-way classification: **contested** — the sequencing is well supported case-by-case, but no single study ranks all factor pairs across all episodes on a common scale.]

## F.2 — Earnings-revision transmission

### The lag asymmetry

Market *prices* react to a funding/geopolitical/oil catalyst within hours, but consensus *EPS revisions* lag by weeks to a full quarter because they are gated by the earnings calendar and by analysts' documented sluggishness. A desk that treats consensus EPS as a real-time signal will systematically mis-time the regime [confidence: **well-established**].

### The academic bedrock

Chan, Jegadeesh & Lakonishok find that "security analysts' earnings forecasts respond sluggishly to past news, especially in the case of stocks with the worst past performance," and that past earnings surprises predict large drifts in future returns — i.e., the market itself underreacts and only gradually incorporates information ([Quantpedia summary](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/post-earnings-announcement-effect)) [confidence: **well-established**]. Post-earnings-announcement drift (PEAD), first documented by Ball & Brown (1968), establishes that abnormal returns continue to drift for *weeks to months* after the news ([ScienceDirect PEAD review](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214635020303750); [UCLA Anderson](https://www.anderson.ucla.edu/documents/areas/fac/accounting/drift503.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**]. The Federal Reserve's study of S&P 500 analyst forecast errors finds bottom-up analyst forecasts are largely uncorrelated with macro-based growth forecasts and that the macro-analyst gap predicts about 50% of current-quarter forecast errors and about 40% of quarter-ahead forecast errors — direct evidence that macro information is not fully embedded in consensus immediately ([Federal Reserve FEDS](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/files/2024049pap.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**].

Agarwal & Hess find that analysts' forecasts respond to macro announcements after controlling for firm and industry earnings surprises, with stronger reliance on macro news for cyclical industries and stronger effects in medium-term than current-year forecasts ([Agarwal & Hess, SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: **well-established**]. Chen, Narayanamoorthy, Sougiannis & Zhou show analyst forecast revisions display momentum and underreaction, with post-forecast-revision drift linked to serial correlation in individual analysts' revisions ([Chen et al., SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2578757)) [confidence: **well-established**].

### Quantified sector lags — what the data actually says

Lombard Odier's econometric decomposition estimates that a supply-driven oil shock produces a rapid drop of about −1.9% in profits after three months, persisting to around −1.6% a year after the shock, and that a 50% oil-price increase translates to roughly a 15% earnings decline over a year, with the *direct cost/margin channel* (≈ −2.1% of −2.2%) dominating the *interest-rate channel* (≈ −0.2%) ([Lombard Odier](https://am.lombardodier.com/insights/2026/april/how-vulnerable-are-us-corporate-earnings-to-an-oil-supply-shock.html)) [confidence: **contested** — single-house model but internally precise and mechanism-consistent]. The ECB's real-time model finds a 1% oil-spot rise maps near 1:1 into oil futures and is passed on (one-to-one) to gasoline prices within a week, with airline stocks responding −0.45% on impact, "substantially stronger than that of aggregate indices" ([ECB Working Paper 2472](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp2472~611f104931.en.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**].

### Sector ordering of revision speed

| Sector | Realistic EPS-revision lag from an oil/shipping/defense catalyst | Why | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Energy producers** | 1–4 weeks | Catalyst *is* the revenue line; refiner/E&P decks update on spot/futures within days, with consensus following after company hedging/capex commentary ([ECB WP 2472](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp2472~611f104931.en.pdf)) | **well-established** |
| **Transports / airlines** | 4–12 weeks | Fuel surcharges often based on prior-week prices; near-term tickets sold before shock cannot be retroactively repriced ([Direct Drive Logistics](https://www.directdrivelogistics.com/logisticsNews/LogisticsFuelCosts); [CNBC airline fuel coverage](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/airfare-prices-jet-fuel-iran-war.html)) | **anecdotal** for the exact window; **well-established** for the mechanism |
| **Insurers (P&C)** | Two-stage: 4–12 weeks (short-tail catastrophe/auto/property) → 1–3 years (long-tail) | Long-tail claims settle years later; inflation assumptions require reserve/pricing model changes ([BDO insurance inflation](https://www.bdo.com/insights/industries/insurance/anticipating-future-claims-costs-the-impact-of-inflation-on-property-casualty-insurance)) | **well-established** |
| **Defense primes** | Quarters to multi-year | Revenue flows through multi-year procurement/budget cycles; news first reduces uncertainty around future cash flows and forecast dispersion before any revenue is recognised | **anecdotal** — mechanism-plausible, no clean isolating study found |
| **Consumer discretionary** | 3–6 months | Real-income/gasoline-price pass-through is fast (within a week) but consensus EPS waits for the next print; analysts smooth revisions, preferring to "wait for the earnings call" | **contested** |

### Framework rule for the desk

Consensus EPS is a *lagging confirmation*, not a *leading signal*. The desk should lead on price, positioning, and credit, and use revisions to *confirm* regime — expecting the first real EPS evidence ~3 months after a cost-channel catalyst. By the time EPS revisions capitulate, the market has usually already priced the damage into multiples, and the trough in prices often precedes the trough in earnings revisions by several months [confidence: **well-established**].

## F.3 — Index plumbing: what is FREE vs vendor-only

This subsection is where epistemic honesty matters most, because the plumbing signals are the most heavily *marketed* and the least *verifiable*. The discipline: state which inputs a reader can reconstruct from free public data (so the desk can be quantitative) versus which are vendor estimates (so the desk stays qualitative and avoids false precision).

### Buyback blackout calendars — FREE / reconstructable, but the effect is contested

The blackout window is a derivable calendar object, not a proprietary dataset. NASPP survey data shows 75% of companies close their trading window ≥11 days before fiscal quarter-end (32% at 11–15 days, 23% at >25 days) ([NASPP](https://www.naspp.com/blog/4-Trends-in-Trading-Blackout-Periods)), and European MAR mandates a 30-day pre-results closed period ([PwC](https://www.pwc.ch/en/insights/fs/practical-guide-blackout-periods.html)) [confidence: **well-established**]. Skadden notes companies customarily institute blackout periods late in each quarter, and Harvard Law School Forum notes Rule 10b5-1 plans can permit repurchases during a closed window if pre-established ([Skadden](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2022/03/revisiting-share-repurchases-in-volatile-times); [Harvard Law](https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2025/08/14/structuring-share-repurchases-under-rules-10b-18-and-10b5-1/)) [confidence: **well-established**].

But the *directional market impact* is **contested**: State Street's study finds "no significant difference in returns in any of the periods measured" and "no strong linkage between buybacks and any performance degradation during blackout periods," even reporting *more* positive excess-return months *before* earnings ([State Street](https://www.ssga.com/library-content/pdfs/etf/us/b27-buyback-blackout-periods-do-not-negatively-impact-performance.pdf)).

**Free implementation:** build a qualitative blackout heat map from public earnings calendars ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/calendar/earnings/), [Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/earnings)) overlaid with the standard "5 weeks before through 48 hours after" window ([Goldman Sachs commentary](https://www.goldmansachs.com/pdfs/insights/the-markets/can-us-equities-rally-again/transcript.pdf)).

**Vendor-only:** a daily estimate of authorised, active, blackout-adjusted corporate demand by index constituent and broker execution channel.

**Rule:** publish the calendar quantitatively, treat the directional effect as a contested qualitative overlay.

### CTA / vol-control positioning — partly free aggregates, vendor-only point estimates

The *mechanism* is well-documented and public: CTAs build positions via moving-average signals and scale by volatility targeting, mechanically selling into downtrends and reducing size in choppy markets ([BNP Paribas](https://wealthmanagement.bnpparibas/en/insights/market-strategy/trend-following-2024.html); [Return Stacked](https://www.returnstacked.com/managed-futures-trend-following/); [Graham Capital primer](https://www.grahamcapital.com/blog/trend-following-primer/)) [confidence: **well-established**].

**Free aggregates exist but are weekly proxies:**
- **CFTC COT / TFF** — Dealer/Intermediary, Asset Manager/Institutional, Leveraged Funds, and Other Reportables categories, released Friday at 3:30 p.m. ET for the prior Tuesday's positions ([CFTC](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm)) [confidence: **well-established**].
- **OFR Hedge Fund Monitor** — publishes leveraged-fund net notional exposure to major equity-index futures; identifies leveraged funds as hedge funds, CTAs, CPOs, and certain unregistered funds ([OFR](https://www.financialresearch.gov/hedge-fund-monitor/categories/size/chart-90/)) [confidence: **well-established**].

**Vendor-only / desk-estimated:** real-time CTA rebalancing thresholds, vol-control AUM, target-vol allocations, dealer-client flow decomposition, and specific de-grossing magnitudes ("$30bn of S&P selling if SPX breaks X") — these are proprietary sell-side models (Nomura, Goldman QIS, Deutsche Bank, UBS, J.P. Morgan) with no free equivalent ([J.P. Morgan market data intelligence](https://www.jpmorgan.com/markets/market-data-intelligence)) [confidence: **well-established**].

**Rule:** positioning de-grossing magnitudes are vendor-only and must stay qualitative — the desk can describe the trigger logic (vol-target de-grossing on a vol spike) but should not publish point estimates it cannot source.

### 0DTE & gamma exposure — activity free, the level vendor-dependent

**The growth is documented from free sources.** 0DTE options reached over 40% of SPX options volume by mid-2023 and a record ~56% share by Feb 2025, up from <17% in 2020 ([Technical Indicators That Sort Of Work](https://technicalindicatorsthatsortofwork.com/blog/indications/gamma-exposure-gex-and-its-application-to-spx-0dte-options-trading)) [confidence: **well-established**]. Cboe publishes SPX/XSP 0DTE education and market resources, and OCC provides volume, open interest, series, new listings, and put/call ratio resources ([Cboe 0DTE](https://www.cboe.com/en/tradable-products/0dte/); [Cboe market statistics](https://www.cboe.com/en/markets/us/options/market-statistics/); [OCC options data](https://www.optionseducation.org/referencelibrary/market-data)) [confidence: **well-established**].

**The dealer-hedging mechanism is public and methodologically transparent:** positive GEX → counter-trend, vol-dampening, "pinning"; negative GEX → pro-trend, vol-amplifying ([SpotGamma](https://spotgamma.com/gamma-exposure-gex/)) [confidence: **well-established**].

**But the computed GEX level and "gamma flip" require a dealer-positioning assumption** (typically "dealers short puts, long calls") that is a simplification, and the resulting flip level is "an estimated level based on models," dynamic intraday, and provider-dependent ([Barchart SPX gamma](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$SPX/gamma-exposure); [SpotGamma SPX gamma](https://spotgamma.com/free-tools/spx-gamma-exposure/)) [confidence: **contested**]. Gemini 3.1 Pro treats free public GEX feeds as solving the problem; GPT-5.5 and Claude Opus 4.8 flag that "free GEX" still embeds a modelling prior. **Adopt the more conservative reading** — free inputs (Cboe open interest, strikes) let the desk *approximate* GEX, but the headline number is not free or canonical.

**Rule:** cite GEX qualitatively, flag the dealer-positioning assumption, never treat the flip level as a hard line.

### Summary table

| Signal | Free / reconstructable? | Vendor-only component | Desk treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buyback blackout *calendar* | Yes — from earnings dates + 10b5-1 rules | None (calendar is rule-based) | Quantitative calendar |
| Buyback blackout *return effect* | No — academic evidence is null/contested ([State Street](https://www.ssga.com/library-content/pdfs/etf/us/b27-buyback-blackout-periods-do-not-negatively-impact-performance.pdf)) | Authorised vs active vs blackout-adjusted corporate demand | Qualitative overlay only |
| CTA mechanism (trend signal, vol target) | Yes — documented in primers | None | Quantitative trigger logic |
| CTA / vol-control positioning magnitudes | Weekly proxies via CFTC TFF + OFR | Real-time AUM, rebalancing thresholds, de-grossing levels | Qualitative; cite proxies |
| 0DTE activity & share of volume | Yes — Cboe / OCC | None | Quantitative |
| Dealer GEX *level* and flip | Approximate only | Computed GEX with dealer-positioning model | Qualitative; flag assumptions |
| VIX term structure | Yes — Cboe daily | None | Quantitative |

## F.4 — Breadth and internals: the three most diagnostic FREE metrics

All three models converge on the same three metrics — they are deliberately orthogonal, measuring **participation**, **concentration**, and **thrust** respectively (Claude Opus 4.8's taxonomy). All are free, daily-updating end-of-day, and reproducible by any reader, which is itself a durability property.

### 1. Percent of S&P 500 above 200-day moving average — *participation*

- **Free sources:** [StockCharts $SPXA200R](https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=%24SPXA200R); [Barchart $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH).
- **Cadence:** daily, end-of-day (Barchart quotes may be delayed 15 minutes).
- **Diagnostic use:** distinguishes a durable advance from a narrow mega-cap rally. A tape rising on a falling %>200dma is narrowing leadership — a classic late-regime tell. An index at higher highs while this percentage makes lower highs is a textbook sell signal.
- **Confidence:** **well-established** for divergence detection; **contested** for precise timing.

### 2. Equal-weight vs cap-weight S&P 500 — *concentration*

- **Free sources:** [RSP/SPY ratio on Yahoo Finance](https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=RSP%3ASPY); methodology at [S&P 500 Equal Weight Index](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500-equal-weight-index/) and [S&P 500](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500/).
- **Cadence:** daily (ETF prices) / real-time intraday via RSP:SPY.
- **Diagnostic use:** measures whether average constituents are confirming index leadership. Invesco documents that the top-10 names contributed over 70% of index return in H1 2024 and that the cap-weight P/E (28.0) carried a 28% premium over equal-weight ([Invesco](https://www.invesco.com/apac/en/institutional/insights/etf/three-compelling-reasons-to-consider-s-and-p-500-equal-weight.html)) — extreme concentration that historically mean-reverts.
- **Confidence:** **well-established** as a concentration gauge; **contested** as a timing signal.

### 3. Net new 52-week highs minus lows — *thrust and deterioration*

- **Free sources:** [WSJ new 52-week highs/lows](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/stocks/newfiftytwoweekhighsandlows); [Barchart 52-week highs/lows](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/highs-lows); [StockCharts $NYHL](https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=%24NYHL).
- **Cadence:** daily, end-of-day.
- **Diagnostic use:** cuts through index weighting to show if the median stock is participating. An index at new highs while new lows expand is the single most reliable free divergence warning; a surge in net new highs off a low is a breadth-thrust confirmation.
- **Confidence:** **well-established**.

### Framework rule

Use all three together — a single breadth metric is noise, all three pointing the same direction is signal. Because they measure orthogonal properties, conflict between them is itself informative: e.g., a rising %>200dma with falling RSP/SPY indicates broad participation but narrowing leadership — a transitional regime.

## F.5 — Cross-desk hooks: the three strongest credit→equities lead/lag relationships

The point of this subsection is **reference without duplication**: the equities page should cite the credit desk's signals rather than rebuild them, and should be honest that credit's lead over equities is *real but regime-dependent and often coincident rather than cleanly leading.*

### 1. HY OAS level → forward equity/risk returns (valuation channel)

The strongest single hook. LSEG/FTSE Russell finds tight HY credit spreads are "strongly associated with lower future bond returns," with spread-to-forward-return correlations of 69% at 1 year, 87% at 2 years, 88% at 3 years, and 86% at 4 years, while HY and Russell 1000 monthly returns had an 84% correlation over the five years to February 2023 and 92% over the final year of that sample ([LSEG/FTSE Russell](https://www.lseg.com/content/dam/ftse-russell/en_us/documents/research/valuation-matters-us-high-yield-and-equities.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established** as a valuation/return relationship; **contested** as a short-horizon timing lead — the relationship is strongest at 2–4 year horizons, not days/weeks].

**Free input:** [FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2 (ICE BofA US HY OAS)](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2), daily at close. The series measures spreads between a below-investment-grade bond index and a spot Treasury curve.

**Where the models disagree.** Gemini 3.1 Pro asserts a sharper practitioner rule: "HY OAS leads equity drawdowns by days to weeks" when breaking above 400–500bp. GPT-5.5 and Opus cite the OFR finding that the lead/lag direction is contested in the literature — Acharya & Johnson find CDS-to-stock information flow, while Norden & Weber and Hilscher et al. find information runs from equities to CDS ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: **contested**].

**Adopt the conservative reading.** Publish HY OAS as a *state variable and divergence detector*, not a point-forecast input. The equity use is not "HY OAS predicts next week's SPX," but "HY OAS widening without equity breadth damage is an early-warning divergence, and HY OAS tightening after a shock is a necessary condition for durable equity beta re-risking."

### 2. CDX / Credit VIX vs equity VIX — the "two barometers must agree" hook

The strongest *practical* cross-desk rule requires credit and equity-vol to confirm each other: a VIX spike *without* a widening of HY OAS or CDX is more likely a positioning/gamma event than a fundamental risk repricing — directly mapping onto F.1's funding-vs-earnings split.

Figuerola-Ferretti & Paraskevopoulos find cointegration between VIX and CDS/iTraxx, predominant VIX price leadership, and both VIX and iTraxx values rising by about 400% over 2008 ([EFMA paper](https://www.efmaefm.org/0efmameetings/EFMA%20ANNUAL%20MEETINGS/2011-Braga/papers/0229.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**]. Cboe and S&P DJI launched **Credit VIX indices (VIXHY, VIXIG) in October 2023** explicitly to give "a comparable benchmark index for the credit markets" alongside equity VIX ([Cboe](https://ir.cboe.com/news/news-details/2023/Cboe-Global-Markets-and-SP-Dow-Jones-Indices-to-Launch-New-Credit-VIX-Indices-on-October-13/default.aspx); [S&P DJI methodology](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/fixed-income/cdx-cboe-na-high-yield-1-month-volatility-index-bp-volatility/)) [confidence: **well-established** that the instruments exist; **contested** on the lead/lag between credit-vol and equity-vol].

**Free input:** VIX is free/daily on Cboe; granular Credit VIX history is partly vendor-gated.

**Desk rule:** use VIX–CDX/Credit-VIX divergence as a *cross-check*, not a single trigger. VIX often leads credit-vol adjustment in broad risk shocks; CDS can lead in firm-specific credit-information events. Claude Opus 4.8's identification of the Credit VIX as the missing instrument to operationalise this hook is the most actionable insight in this subsection — the equities desk should monitor it going forward even though most academic studies predate its launch.

### 3. Investment-Grade OAS and credit-index price discovery — the systemic validator

While HY OAS moves early, IG OAS moves when stress becomes systemic. If HY OAS widens but IG OAS remains tight (<150bp), the stress is contained to lower-quality companies. If IG OAS begins widening rapidly alongside HY OAS, it validates a macro/systemic equity selloff [confidence: **well-established** as a stylised rule; **contested** on the exact threshold].

The OFR's CDS price-discovery work finds single-name CDS spreads led corporate bond spreads before UMR — a 1% CDS-spread increase was associated with a 0.20% next-day bond-spread increase — while post-UMR the relationship weakened to 0.11%. CDX index changes led corporate bond index changes across the sample and remained stable after UMR ([OFR](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: **well-established**].

**Free input:** [FRED BAMLC0A0CM (ICE BofA US Corporate IG OAS)](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLC0A0CM), daily.

**Desk rule:** the equity page should reference the credit desk when CDX or HY OAS moves ahead of equity breadth, because the credit desk owns the spread-quality, default, and cash-bond liquidity diagnosis while equities should translate the signal into beta, sector, and factor exposure.

### Framework rule for cross-desk hooks

Treat credit (HY OAS, IG OAS) as the *fundamental* confirmer and vol term-structure / Credit VIX as the *stress-state* confirmer. Only call a "fundamental risk-off regime" when both move with equities — otherwise label it a positioning event. This single rule is what prevents the desk from confusing a Quant-Quake-style positioning event for a GFC-style fundamental repricing.

## F.6 — Five concrete falsifiers for the central regime read

A framework that cannot be falsified is not durable — it is unaccountable. Assume the desk's central regime read is some version of: *"the current shock is a funding/liquidity event (mechanical, mean-reverting) rather than an earnings/recession event, so dislocations in crowded factors and breadth should reverse and credit should confirm benign fundamentals."* The five falsifiers below are dated, observable, free-data conditions under which the desk will abandon that read. They combine Claude Opus 4.8's "sustained vs spike" framing (the most epistemically aligned with the brief), GPT-5.5's staged checklist structure, and Gemini's tactical tripwires.

### Falsifier 1 — Sustained HY OAS regime break

**Trigger:** HY OAS breaks and *stays* above ~600–700bp for 20+ consecutive trading days (vs the benign ~275bp regime in mid-2026). A spike-and-revert is consistent with a mechanical funding shock; a sustained widening is the credit desk telling equities the shock is fundamental.
**Free data:** [FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2), daily.
**What it falsifies:** the "credit confirms benign fundamentals" clause of the central regime read.
**Confidence:** **well-established** signal logic.

### Falsifier 2 — Quant-Quake reversal pattern fails to appear

**Trigger:** within ~5 trading days of a crowded-factor dislocation, the Aug-2007-style partial reversal does not appear; value/momentum or large/small dislocations *keep extending* past a week instead of mean-reverting.
**Free data:** Fama-French factor series ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)); IWM/SPY, RSP/SPY price ratios.
**What it falsifies:** the Unwind Hypothesis — i.e., the desk's read that the shock is a mechanical forced-liquidation event rather than a fundamental repricing.
**Confidence:** **well-established** as a mechanism.

### Falsifier 3 — Fast, broad, cost-insulated EPS cuts

**Trigger:** consensus sector EPS is revised down by more than 5% within the first earnings season (~3 months) after the catalyst, including in *cost-insulated* sectors (sectors with no direct exposure to the catalyst's input-cost channel). The Lombard Odier oil-shock model predicts ~−1.9% aggregate profit at 3 months for a real oil shock; revisions arriving *faster and broader* than the cost-channel mechanics justify signal demand destruction, not just margin compression.
**Free data:** consensus EPS via [Yahoo Finance earnings calendar](https://finance.yahoo.com/calendar/earnings/) and revision breadth via free aggregators; corroborated by Lombard Odier's published decomposition ([Lombard Odier](https://am.lombardodier.com/insights/2026/april/how-vulnerable-are-us-corporate-earnings-to-an-oil-supply-shock.html)).
**What it falsifies:** the "contained funding event" read.
**Confidence:** **contested** — single-house calibration; mechanism-consistent but threshold is judgemental.

### Falsifier 4 — Breadth fails to recover

**Trigger:** %>200dma ($S5TH) stays below ~30% for 20+ trading days while the cap-weight S&P 500 recovers, *or* the RSP/SPY ratio breaks a 40-week low and stays there. A genuine funding shock that mean-reverts should see participation broaden on the rebound; a recovery carried only by a few mega-caps while breadth stays broken signals a deteriorating-fundamentals regime.
**Free data:** [Barchart $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH); [StockCharts $SPXA200R](https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=%24SPXA200R); RSP/SPY price ratio.
**What it falsifies:** the durability and mean-reversion read.
**Confidence:** **well-established** signal logic; **contested** on the exact thresholds.

### Falsifier 5 — Persistent positive stock-bond correlation

**Trigger:** the SPX/UST return correlation flips positive and *stays* positive for a sustained window (≥20 trading days). Per the FSB, this is the precise signature that a shock has crossed from ordinary risk-off into a *systemic dash-for-cash*; if it persists rather than resolving after a liquidity backstop, the "contained, mean-reverting funding event" thesis is falsified and the desk must price a genuine liquidity-spiral/recession regime.
**Free data:** daily SPX returns vs daily UST returns (FRED or Yahoo Finance); 20-day rolling correlation.
**Source:** [FSB Holistic Review of March 2020](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf).
**Confidence:** **well-established**.

### Bonus tripwire (Gemini-only) — interbank funding stress

Gemini 3.1 Pro's unique contribution is the **FRA-OIS / SOFR spread blowing past ~40bp** as an instant falsifier for any "smooth liquidity" regime — interbank funding stress that bypasses the equity-specific falsifiers above and goes straight to the money-market plumbing. This is the only model-surfaced bridge between the equities page and money-market diagnostics; carry it as a sixth tripwire even though it falls outside the brief's "five concrete falsifiers" requirement [confidence: **well-established** mechanism].

## Closing: the throughline

The throughline across all six subsections is a single durability principle: **the framework must classify the *channel* before it classifies the *direction*.** Funding-channel shocks reprice crowded/levered factors first and mean-revert (F.1), arrive in prices long before EPS revisions confirm them (F.2), are dominated by mechanical flows that are mostly *vendor-estimated, not measurable* (F.3), and must be cross-checked against breadth (F.4) and credit (F.5) to avoid mistaking a positioning event for a fundamental one. The five falsifiers (F.6) are the discipline that keeps the desk honest — each is a dated, free-data observable that converts the regime read from a narrative into a testable claim. A desk that pre-commits to these falsifiers is durable precisely because it has told the market, in advance, what would make it change its mind.

The three models converged on the structural claim, the data-honesty hierarchy, the three breadth metrics, the HY OAS hook, and the stock-bond-correlation falsifier. They diverged on whether quality always defends (GPT-5.5's negative RMW in March 2020 settles this against Gemini's clean rule), on whether the 2022 LDI crisis generalises (Opus's negative-case reading is better-supported by the data), and on how strong the HY OAS short-horizon lead really is (the academic evidence sides with the conservative reading). The synthesis above adopts the better-supported position in each case and carries every model's unique contribution forward.

# Appendix A — Gemini 3.1 Pro: Full Report


## 1. Factor/Style Rotation Under a Funding-Stress Catalyst

When an equities selloff is catalyzed by funding liquidity stress, geopolitical shock, or energy spikes rather than a traditional earnings recession, the factor rotation playbook differs significantly from a standard late-cycle downturn. Academic and empirical research demonstrates that under funding stress, the most immediate casualty is **Momentum and Levered Yield/Value**, while **Quality and Profitability** provide the most robust defense ([Russell Investments](https://russellinvestments.com/us/blog/factor-investing-us-recessions)). *Confidence: Well-established.*

Unlike an earnings recession where cyclicals gradually underperform defensives, a funding shock triggers a "dash for cash," heavily punishing factors reliant on high leverage, refinancing, or crowded positioning. 
- **1998 LTCM Crisis (Aug-Oct 1998):** Driven by a Russian debt default and the collapse of Long-Term Capital Management, this was a pure liquidity/funding shock. The Size (Small Cap) factor was crushed as liquidity dried up, while large-cap Quality outperformed rapidly as investors sought balance sheet safety ([NBER](https://ideas.repec.org/r/nbr/nberwo/13385.html)). *Confidence: Well-established.*
- **2020 March COVID "Dash for Cash":** Initially a severe funding shock before becoming an earnings shock, Treasury market plumbing broke down, leading hedge funds to radically deleverage basis trades ([AUT Research](https://acfr.aut.ac.nz/__data/assets/pdf_file/0004/577156/Sumudu-Watugala-HF_Treasury_Shock.pdf)). Factor-wise, Value (often holding highly levered industrials/financials) collapsed instantly relative to Growth (long duration, strong cash flows), and Minimum Volatility failed its defensive mandate because highly levered utilities and REITs were sold indiscriminately for cash ([Bridgeway](https://bridgeway.com/perspectives/stress-test-how-factors-perform-before-during-and-after-recessions/)). *Confidence: Well-established.*
- **2022 LDI / Gilt Crisis (Sept-Oct 2022):** A localized but severe duration and collateral shock. High-dividend/Value underperformed Quality in the UK as pension funds liquidated their most liquid assets to meet margin calls, disregarding traditional valuation metrics. *Confidence: Anecdotal/Contested as a pure equities driver globally.*

**Key takeaway:** In a funding shock, sell Value/Leverage and buy Quality/Profitability immediately. The traditional "Defensive" sectors (Utilities, Real Estate) may actually *underperform* if they are highly levered and sensitive to refinancing costs, whereas "Quality" (strong balance sheets, free cash flow) acts as the true safe haven.

## 2. Earnings-Revision Transmission

The transmission mechanism from a macroeconomic shock (e.g., oil price spike, shipping disruption) to consensus EPS revisions is notoriously lagged. Sell-side analysts tend to wait for company management to update guidance before adjusting their models, creating a structural delay between observable macro catalysts and EPS estimate cuts. *Confidence: Well-established.*

Historical data and sell-side modeling show that an exogenous shock like a $20/bbl oil spike or a sustained shipping freight squeeze takes approximately **3 to 6 months** to fully reflect in forward consensus EPS estimates for affected sectors (Consumer Discretionary, Transports). Analysts generally smooth revisions, preferring to \"wait for the earnings call\" rather than proactively cutting estimates based on commodity futures.
- **Energy and Defense:** Revisions here are faster, usually lagging spot prices by only 1–2 months because revenue modeling is directly tied to the commodity/contract. *Confidence: Well-established.*
- **Consumer and Transports:** The lag extends to 3-6 months. Analysts wait to see if the cost spike is transitory or if the company can pass costs to consumers before adjusting margins downward. 

Because of this lag, the equities desk cannot rely on consensus EPS revisions as a real-time signal during a macro shock. By the time EPS revisions capitulate, the market has usually already priced the damage into multiples, and the trough in prices often precedes the trough in earnings revisions by several months.

## 3. Index Plumbing

Tracking systematic flows and corporate buybacks is critical for understanding market microstructure, but data availability varies heavily.

- **Buyback Blackout Calendars:** These are largely observable using public data. Blackout periods typically begin 5 weeks (or two weeks before the quarter-end) before the scheduled earnings release and end 48 hours after the report ([Goldman Sachs](https://www.goldmansachs.com/pdfs/insights/the-markets/can-us-equities-rally-again/transcript.pdf)). You can track this freely by overlaying corporate earnings calendars (e.g., Yahoo Finance) with standard blackout window rules. *Confidence: Well-established.*
- **0DTE and Gamma Exposure (GEX):** This was historically a vendor-only metric, but free public sources now exist. Sites like [FlashAlpha](https://flashalpha.com/articles/free-gex-data-gamma-exposure-levels-any-ticker) provide free, real-time Gamma Exposure (GEX) levels, call/put walls, and gamma flips for indices (SPX, QQQ) and single stocks, including 0DTE strikes. *Confidence: Well-established.*
- **Systematic / CTA Positioning:** This remains largely qualitative or vendor-only for precise numbers. While you can find estimates scattered on Twitter/X (often leaked from prime brokerage desks like Goldman Sachs or Nomura), there is no official, free real-time feed of CTA or Vol-Control positioning. Desks must rely on delayed proxy ETFs or expensive institutional data (e.g., Goldman Sachs QIS). *Confidence: Well-established.*

## 4. Breadth and Internals

To diagnose the health of an equities trend without relying on vendor data, the desk should monitor three highly diagnostic, free breadth metrics available on platforms like StockCharts. *Confidence: Well-established.*

1. **Equal-Weight vs. Cap-Weight Ratio (RSP/SPY):** A declining ratio indicates that mega-cap stocks are masking weakness in the broader market, often a late-cycle warning sign (e.g., leading into the 2022 bear market) ([StockCharts](https://articles.stockcharts.com/article/articles-arthurhill-2025-03-spy-reverses-longterm-uptrend-437/)). **URL:** `https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=RSP%3ASPY` (Daily update).
2. **Percent of Stocks Above 200-Day Moving Average ($SPXA200R):** This measures the long-term internal health of the S&P 500. A divergence where the SPX makes higher highs but this percentage makes lower highs is a classic sell signal. **URL:** `https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=%24SPXA200R` (Daily update).
3. **Net New 52-Week Highs - Lows ($NYHL):** Tracks the raw number of stocks making new highs minus new lows on the NYSE. It cuts through index weighting to show if the median stock is participating in the rally ([StockCharts](https://chartschool.stockcharts.com/table-of-contents/market-indicators/net-new-52-week-highs)). **URL:** `https://stockcharts.com/h-sc/ui?s=%24NYHL` (Daily update).

## 5. Cross-Desk Hooks

Credit markets trade on the math of survival, making them highly sensitive to downside risk, while equities trade on the dream of upside. Consequently, credit often leads equities. *Confidence: Well-established.*

1. **High Yield Option-Adjusted Spread (HY OAS) vs. S&P 500:** HY OAS tracks the risk premium on junk bonds. When HY OAS widens while equities remain flat or climb, it signals the bond market is pricing in structural stress or recession risk that equities are ignoring ([Acid Capitalist](https://acidcapitalist.com/learn/hy-oas-ig-oas-credit-spreads)). Historically, HY OAS breaking above 400-500 bps leads equity drawdowns by days to weeks. 
2. **Investment Grade OAS (IG OAS) as a Systemic Validator:** While HY OAS moves early, IG OAS moves when stress becomes systemic. If HY OAS widens but IG OAS remains tight (<150 bps), the stress is contained to lower-quality companies. If IG OAS begins widening rapidly alongside HY OAS, it validates a macro/systemic equity selloff. 
3. **Credit Stress vs. Volatility (CDX vs VIX):** The CDX High Yield index tracks credit default swaps. A rising CDX (credit insurance getting more expensive) combined with a muted VIX is a leading indicator that equity volatility is severely underpriced and a volatility spike is imminent. 

## 6. What Would Falsify the Equities Framework

For any regime framework to be robust, it must have clear falsification triggers. If the desk is operating under a specific "Regime Read" (e.g., 'Goldilocks Growth' or 'Impending Recession'), the following observable metrics would invalidate that thesis: *Confidence: Well-established.*

1. **Credit Divergence (Falsifier for Bullish Regime):** The SPX makes a new all-time high, but HY OAS widens by >50 bps over a two-week period. This invalidates the soft-landing narrative.
2. **Breadth Capitulation (Falsifier for Bullish Regime):** The S&P 500 cap-weighted index holds its 50-day moving average, but the RSP/SPY ratio breaks a 40-week low and the % of stocks above the 200-DMA drops below 50%.
3. **Defensive Factor Rotation (Falsifier for Cyclical Expansion):** Quality and Low Volatility factors outperform Value and Small Caps for 4 consecutive weeks during a period of rising indices, signaling the market is rotating to safety under the hood.
4. **Earnings Revision Breadth (Falsifier for Bearish Regime):** Despite macro fears, the 3-month moving average of EPS estimate *upgrades* outnumbers *downgrades* across cyclical sectors (Transports, Industrials). 
5. **Funding Stress Indicator (Falsifier for Liquidity Flush Regime):** The 3-month FRA-OIS spread (or SOFR equivalent) blows out past 40 bps, indicating interbank funding stress, instantly falsifying any framework reliant on smooth liquidity provision and leveraging.

# Appendix B — GPT-5.5: Full Report


## Framing: durability across regimes

An equities desk is durable across regimes when it treats equity index price as the last expression of a cross-asset balance-sheet system, not as a self-contained earnings-discounting machine; this framing is especially useful in funding, energy, and geopolitical shocks because liquidity, margin, dealer capacity, and factor crowding can reprice before bottom-up EPS models are changed ([FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The working hierarchy I would use is: first, identify whether the catalyst impairs funding or collateral; second, map the first-round equity expression through size, liquidity, quality, beta, and sector input-cost exposure; third, wait for earnings revisions only after the commodity, freight, claims, or appropriations channel is measurable in company guidance or consensus systems ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: well-established]. The practical edge is to separate “shock-to-price” from “shock-to-EPS,” because the March 2020 episode had forced selling, margin calls, MMF outflows, Treasury-market depth collapse, and S&P 500 volatility extremes before the earnings path was knowable with precision ([FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

## 1. Factor/style rotation under a funding-stress catalyst

The most repeatable first repricing in funding-stress episodes is small/illiquid underperformance versus large/liquid stocks, because funding stress raises the shadow price of liquidity and balance-sheet capacity before it proves an earnings recession; the Fama-French daily SMB factor was -7.14% in Aug.–Sep. 1998, -9.18% in Sep.–Nov. 2008, -9.29% in Jul.–Oct. 2011, -7.53% in Feb.–Mar. 2020, and -5.54% in the Mar. 2023 SVB window in my calculations from the public Fama-French daily five-factor file ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: well-established]. Quality/profitability generally reprices early when the shock is funding-led, because balance-sheet durability and operating profitability become scarce; Fama-French RMW was +13.18% in Sep.–Nov. 2008, +9.19% in Jul.–Oct. 2011, +4.19% during the UK LDI/gilt stress window, and +3.56% during the SVB stress window, while the COVID dash-for-cash was the exception at -2.90% because the initial liquidation was broad and indiscriminate ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html), [FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. Defensives versus cyclicals usually acts faster than EPS revisions in macro/funding shocks; using ETF proxies, defensive sectors XLP/XLU/XLV outperformed cyclical XLY/XLI/XLF/XLE by +21.25 percentage points in Sep.–Nov. 2008, +16.14 points in Jul.–Oct. 2011, +14.33 points in Feb.–Mar. 2020, and +7.00 points during the SVB window, based on Yahoo Finance adjusted prices for sector ETFs ([Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history)) [confidence: well-established]. Value versus growth is less stable as a first-round stress signal: Fama-French HML was +0.51% in Aug.–Sep. 1998, -7.47% in Sep.–Nov. 2008, -5.36% in Jul.–Oct. 2011, -17.92% in Feb.–Mar. 2020, +5.20% in the LDI/gilt window, and -7.68% in Mar. 2023, so the sign depends on whether financials/energy are the epicenter or the beneficiary ([Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: contested]. Low-volatility usually helps in classic equity drawdowns but can fail when rate duration is the shock; SPLV beat SPY by +10.07 points in Jul.–Oct. 2011, lagged SPY by -2.11 points in Feb.–Mar. 2020, lagged by -1.84 points during the LDI/gilt window, and was roughly flat versus SPY in the SVB window using ETF price histories ([Yahoo Finance SPLV history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPLV/history)) [confidence: contested].

Three analogues are most useful for desk playbooks. In the 1998 Russia/LTCM shock, Russia’s devaluation/default triggered a flight to safer and more liquid assets, LTCM lost 44% in August, and a 14-firm consortium injected $3.6 billion on 23 September 1998 to prevent disorderly liquidation; in that same Aug.–Sep. window, market beta was -10.86%, SMB was -7.14%, RMW was +2.28%, and CMA was +3.82% in the Fama-French daily data ([Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/ltcm-near-failure), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: well-established]. In the 2008 funding panic, Lehman filed on 15 September 2008, AIG received support the next day, and the Fed created liquidity programs for money-market funds and commercial paper; in Sep.–Nov. 2008, SPY fell -40.71%, IWM underperformed SPY by -7.01 points, defensives beat cyclicals by +21.25 points, and Fama-French RMW rose +13.18% ([Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/great-recession-and-its-aftermath), [Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: well-established]. In March 2020, the dash-for-cash included US prime MMF outflows of $125 billion, government/Treasury MMF inflows above $800 billion, record mid-March bond-fund outflows of $109 billion, a 12% S&P 500 one-day loss on 16 March, and a VIX peak of 83; in the 19 Feb.–23 Mar. window, Fama-French market beta fell -33.98%, SMB was -7.53%, HML was -17.92%, and defensive sectors beat cyclicals by +14.33 points ([FSB March 2020 review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html), [Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history)) [confidence: well-established].

## 2. Earnings-revision transmission

Consensus EPS revisions usually lag market-price factor rotation because sell-side estimates respond most strongly when public macro releases, commodity decks, company guidance, or industry-specific earnings surprises can be mapped into model line items; Agarwal and Hess find that analysts’ forecasts respond to macro announcements after controlling for firm and industry earnings surprises, with stronger reliance on macro news for cyclical industries and stronger effects in medium-term than current-year forecasts ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: well-established]. A realistic desk convention is “prices within hours/days, consensus within one to six weeks, annual EPS after guidance,” because analyst forecast revisions display momentum and underreaction, and post-forecast-revision drift is linked to serial correlation in individual analysts’ revisions ([Chen, Narayanamoorthy, Sougiannis, and Zhou SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2578757)) [confidence: well-established]. The Federal Reserve’s study of S&P 500 analyst forecast errors finds bottom-up analyst forecasts are largely uncorrelated with macro-based growth forecasts and that the macro-analyst gap predicts about 50% of current-quarter forecast errors and about 40% of quarter-ahead forecast errors, which is direct evidence that macro information is not fully embedded in consensus immediately ([Federal Reserve FEDS paper](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/files/2024049pap.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

By sector, energy EPS revisions have the shortest mechanical lag after an oil shock because spot and futures curves flow quickly into realized price decks, but consensus often waits for company hedging, production, and capex commentary before changing full-year EPS; this is why the desk should monitor the oil curve first and weekly/monthly earnings-revision breadth second ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: well-established]. Transports have a faster price reaction than EPS revision because fuel surcharges can be based on prior-week fuel prices and a rapid diesel/jet-fuel move creates a temporary mismatch before carriers can reprice contracts or fares; one logistics explainer describes weekly fuel-surcharge adjustment and a lag when fuel prices rise rapidly ([Direct Drive Logistics](https://www.directdrivelogistics.com/logisticsNews/LogisticsFuelCosts)) [confidence: anecdotal]. Airlines have a 30–90 day earnings-risk window after a jet-fuel spike because near-term tickets may have been sold before the fuel shock and fares cannot be retroactively repriced, according to public reporting of airline-analyst commentary ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/airfare-prices-jet-fuel-iran-war.html)) [confidence: anecdotal]. Insurers revise in two stages: short-tail catastrophe and auto/property claim costs can affect current-quarter EPS quickly, while long-tail lines take longer because claims may settle years later and inflation assumptions require reserve/pricing model changes ([BDO insurance inflation analysis](https://www.bdo.com/insights/industries/insurance/anticipating-future-claims-costs-the-impact-of-inflation-on-property-casualty-insurance)) [confidence: well-established]. Defense primes are slowest on EPS despite fast stock moves, because military-spending news first reduces uncertainty around future procurement cash flows and forecast dispersion, but recognized revenue depends on contracts, funding, backlog conversion, and ASC 606 performance obligations ([NBER war puzzle paper](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w29837/w29837.pdf), [Deloitte DART aerospace and defense revenue recognition](https://dart.deloitte.com/USDART/home/publications/deloitte/industry/aerospace-defense/revenue-recognition-asc-606-step-1)) [confidence: well-established]. Consumer EPS revisions after oil/food/shipping shocks are usually the most model-dependent, because analysts need evidence on pass-through, volumes, mix, and margin absorption, and the academic literature says analysts put more weight on macro information when the firms are more cyclical ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161)) [confidence: contested].

## 3. Index plumbing: what is free versus vendor-only

Buyback blackout calendars are partially observable from free data because earnings dates are public and customary blackout periods are tied to the weeks before quarterly results, but the exact percentage of market capitalization in blackout and the live discretionary buyback bid are generally prime-broker or issuer-desk datasets; Skadden notes companies customarily institute blackout periods late in each quarter, and Harvard Law School Forum notes Rule 10b5-1 plans can permit repurchases during a closed window if pre-established ([Skadden](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2022/03/revisiting-share-repurchases-in-volatile-times), [Harvard Law School Forum](https://corpgov.law.harvard.edu/2025/08/14/structuring-share-repurchases-under-rules-10b-18-and-10b5-1/)) [confidence: well-established]. The free implementation is a qualitative blackout heat map built from public earnings calendars and SEC repurchase disclosures, while the vendor-only implementation is a daily estimate of authorized, active, blackout-adjusted corporate demand by index constituent and broker execution channel ([Yahoo Finance earnings calendar](https://finance.yahoo.com/calendar/earnings/), [Nasdaq earnings calendar](https://www.nasdaq.com/market-activity/earnings)) [confidence: contested].

CTA and systematic positioning are partially observable from free public data through CFTC COT/TFF and OFR aggregations, because the TFF report includes Dealer/Intermediary, Asset Manager/Institutional, Leveraged Funds, and Other Reportables categories and is generally released Friday at 3:30 p.m. ET for the prior Tuesday’s positions ([CFTC COT](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm)) [confidence: well-established]. The OFR Hedge Fund Monitor publishes leveraged-fund net notional exposure to major equity-index futures and identifies leveraged funds as including hedge funds, CTAs, CPOs, and certain unregistered funds, but it is still a weekly futures proxy rather than a complete real-time CTA model ([OFR Hedge Fund Monitor](https://www.financialresearch.gov/hedge-fund-monitor/categories/size/chart-90/)) [confidence: well-established]. Real-time CTA rebalancing thresholds, vol-control AUM, target-vol allocations, and dealer-client flow decomposition are vendor-only or desk-estimated because they require fund universes, AUM, leverage assumptions, intraday volatility inputs, and proprietary model calibration ([J.P. Morgan market data intelligence page](https://www.jpmorgan.com/markets/market-data-intelligence)) [confidence: well-established].

0DTE activity is partly observable from free sources because Cboe publishes SPX/XSP 0DTE education and market resources, Cboe has public market-statistics pages, and OCC provides volume, open interest, series, new listings, and put/call ratio resources ([Cboe 0DTE resources](https://www.cboe.com/en/tradable-products/0dte/), [OCC options data](https://www.optionseducation.org/referencelibrary/market-data), [Cboe options statistics](https://www.cboe.com/en/markets/us/options/market-statistics/)) [confidence: well-established]. Aggregate gamma exposure is less cleanly public: Barchart and SpotGamma offer public gamma-exposure pages or snapshots, but robust dealer gamma by strike, 0DTE customer type, and intraday hedging pressure require OPRA-level options data, trade classification, implied-vol surfaces, and dealer-position assumptions ([Barchart SPX gamma exposure](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$SPX/gamma-exposure), [SpotGamma SPX gamma exposure](https://spotgamma.com/free-tools/spx-gamma-exposure/)) [confidence: contested].

## 4. Breadth and internals: three diagnostic free metrics

The first diagnostic breadth metric is the percent of S&P 500 stocks above the 200-day moving average, because it distinguishes a durable index advance from a narrow mega-cap rally; Barchart publishes “S&P 500 Stocks Above 200-Day Average ($S5TH),” and StockCharts lists $SPXA200 and $SPXA200R breadth symbols for S&P 500 stocks above the 200-day average ([Barchart $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH), [StockCharts symbol catalog](https://stockcharts.com/freecharts/catalog/?sl=sa)) [confidence: well-established]. The update cadence is effectively daily/end-of-session for the signal even when quote display may be delayed, because the 200-day moving-average condition is based on daily closing prices, and Barchart states stocks data can be delayed by 15 minutes in its data-delay help page ([Barchart real-time vs delayed data](https://www.barchart.com/trader/help/layout/realtime_delayed.php)) [confidence: well-established].

The second diagnostic breadth metric is the equal-weight versus cap-weight S&P 500 ratio, because it measures whether average constituents are confirming index leadership; S&P describes the S&P 500 Equal Weight Index as the equal-weight version of the widely used S&P 500, while S&P describes the S&P 500 as 500 leading companies covering about 80% of available U.S. market capitalization ([S&P 500 Equal Weight Index](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500-equal-weight-index/), [S&P 500](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500/)) [confidence: well-established]. The free implementation is to chart RSP/SPY or SPXEW/SPX daily, because RSP is an ETF linked to the equal-weight index and SPY is an ETF linked to the cap-weighted S&P 500 ([Yahoo Finance RSP history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/RSP/history), [Yahoo Finance SPY history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/SPY/history)) [confidence: well-established].

The third diagnostic breadth metric is 52-week new highs minus new lows, because it detects whether stress is producing broad downside expansion or merely rotating leadership; Barchart publishes 52-week new-high and new-low pages for U.S. exchanges, and MarketInOut defines the New Highs-New Lows indicator as the daily difference between stocks reaching new 52-week highs and stocks reaching new 52-week lows ([Barchart highs-lows](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/highs-lows), [MarketInOut new highs-new lows](https://www.marketinout.com/technical_analysis.php?t=New_Highs-New_Lows&id=70)) [confidence: well-established]. The update cadence should be treated as intraday-to-daily for observation and end-of-day for signal confirmation, because new highs/lows are session extrema and delayed public quote feeds can make intraday readings less reliable ([Barchart real-time vs delayed data](https://www.barchart.com/trader/help/layout/realtime_delayed.php)) [confidence: well-established].

## 5. Cross-desk hooks: credit-to-equities lead/lag relationships

The strongest credit-to-equity hook is HY OAS as a risk-appetite and forward-return state variable, because FRED publishes the ICE BofA US High Yield OAS daily at close, and the series measures spreads between a below-investment-grade bond index and a spot Treasury curve ([FRED HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)) [confidence: well-established]. LSEG/FTSE Russell finds U.S. high-yield spreads have forecasted future HY returns well over the last two decades, with spread/future-return correlations of 0.69 at one year, 0.87 at two years, 0.88 at three years, and 0.86 at four years, while HY and Russell 1000 monthly returns had an 84% correlation over the five years to February 2023 and 92% over the final year of that sample ([LSEG/FTSE Russell](https://www.lseg.com/content/dam/ftse-russell/en_us/documents/research/valuation-matters-us-high-yield-and-equities.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The equity use is not “HY OAS predicts next week’s SPX point target,” but “HY OAS widening without equity breadth damage is an early-warning divergence, and HY OAS tightening after a shock is a necessary condition for durable equity beta re-risking” ([FRED HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2), [LSEG/FTSE Russell](https://www.lseg.com/content/dam/ftse-russell/en_us/documents/research/valuation-matters-us-high-yield-and-equities.pdf)) [confidence: contested].

The second hook is CDX/iTraxx versus VIX, because the VIX-credit relationship captures whether equity-option stress is being confirmed by traded credit risk; Figuerola-Ferretti and Paraskevopoulos find cointegration between VIX and CDS/iTraxx, predominant VIX price leadership, and both VIX and iTraxx values rising by about 400% over 2008 ([EFMA paper](https://www.efmaefm.org/0efmameetings/EFMA%20ANNUAL%20MEETINGS/2011-Braga/papers/0229.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The direction of price discovery is contested for credit versus equities, because OFR’s 2024 CDS paper notes that Acharya and Johnson find CDS-to-stock information flow, while Norden and Weber and Hilscher et al. find information runs from equities to CDS ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: contested]. The practical desk rule is to use VIX-CDX divergence as a cross-check rather than a single trigger, because VIX often leads credit-vol adjustment in broad risk shocks while CDS can lead in firm-specific credit-information events ([EFMA paper](https://www.efmaefm.org/0efmameetings/EFMA%20ANNUAL%20MEETINGS/2011-Braga/papers/0229.pdf), [OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: contested].

The third hook is credit-index price discovery versus cash bonds as a funding-stress confirmation signal, because OFR finds single-name CDS spreads led corporate bond spreads before UMR with a 1% CDS-spread increase associated with a 0.20% next-day bond-spread increase, while post-UMR the same relationship weakened to 0.11%; OFR also finds CDX index changes led corporate bond index changes across the sample and remained stable after UMR ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: well-established]. The equity page should therefore reference the credit desk when CDX or HY OAS moves ahead of equity breadth, because the credit desk owns the spread-quality, default, and cash-bond liquidity diagnosis while equities should translate the signal into beta, sector, and factor exposure ([OFR CDS price-discovery paper](https://www.financialresearch.gov/working-papers/files/OFRwp-24-04_do-credit-default-swaps-still-lead-the-effects-of-regulation-on-price-discovery.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

## 6. What would falsify the equities framework

Falsifier 1: If a funding-stress catalyst is identified at date T but by T+5 trading days HY OAS has not widened by at least 50 bps, VIX has not risen above 25 or by at least 8 points, and the S&P 500 percent above 200dma has not fallen by at least 5 percentage points, then the desk’s “funding shock first” regime read should be downgraded to an earnings or idiosyncratic shock; the observables are FRED HY OAS, FRED/Cboe VIX, and Barchart/StockCharts breadth ([FRED HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2), [FRED VIX](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/VIXCLS), [Barchart $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH)) [confidence: contested]. Falsifier 2: If by T+10 trading days small caps are outperforming large caps, equal weight is outperforming cap weight, and Fama-French SMB is positive while HY OAS remains wider, then the assumed liquidity preference for large/liquid equities is wrong for that episode; the observables are IWM/SPY, RSP/SPY, and Fama-French SMB ([Yahoo Finance IWM history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/IWM/history), [Yahoo Finance RSP history](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/RSP/history), [Kenneth French Data Library](https://mba.tuck.dartmouth.edu/pages/faculty/ken.french/Data_Library/f-f_5_factors_2x3.html)) [confidence: contested]. Falsifier 3: If by T+15 trading days defensives have not outperformed cyclicals and new lows are not expanding, then the shock is not being transmitted through macro risk aversion or funding stress as expected; the observables are XLP/XLU/XLV versus XLY/XLI/XLF/XLE and 52-week new highs-lows ([Yahoo Finance sector ETF histories](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/XLP/history), [Barchart highs-lows](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/highs-lows)) [confidence: contested]. Falsifier 4: If by the next weekly CFTC release after T+7 leveraged-fund equity-index futures exposure has not fallen and OFR/CFTC proxies do not show de-risking, then the CTA/vol-control deleveraging leg should be removed from the narrative; the observables are CFTC TFF and OFR leveraged-fund equity-index futures exposure ([CFTC COT](https://www.cftc.gov/MarketReports/CommitmentsofTraders/index.htm), [OFR Hedge Fund Monitor](https://www.financialresearch.gov/hedge-fund-monitor/categories/size/chart-90/)) [confidence: well-established]. Falsifier 5: If by the first full earnings-revision cycle after T, defined as the next four weekly consensus snapshots or the next reporting-season update, sector EPS revisions do not move in the predicted direction for the directly exposed sectors, then the catalyst is price-only rather than earnings-transmissive; this falsifier is most important for energy, transports, insurers, defense primes, and consumer sectors because analyst revisions can lag and underreact to macro information ([Agarwal and Hess SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2024161), [Federal Reserve FEDS paper](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/feds/files/2024049pap.pdf)) [confidence: well-established].

# Appendix C — Claude Opus 4.8: Full Report

### Claude Opus 4.8 — Equities Desk Deep Dive

**Analytical angle.** The brief asks what makes an equities desk *durable across regimes*. My argument is that durability is not a property of having more indicators — it is a property of the framework's **epistemic structure**: how it distinguishes funding-channel shocks (where price action leads fundamentals and mechanical flows dominate) from earnings-channel shocks (where fundamentals lead and revisions dominate), how honestly it labels which signals are observable versus inferred, and whether it pre-commits to falsifiers. A desk that cannot state what would prove it *wrong* is not running a framework; it is running a narrative with a chart attached. Below, every claim carries an inline source and a confidence tag: **well-established** (multiple primary/academic sources agree), **contested** (credible sources disagree or evidence is mixed), or **anecdotal** (single-source, practitioner lore, or mechanism-plausible but under-measured).

---

## F.1 — Factor/style rotation under a funding-stress catalyst

The central regime distinction is this: when the shock arrives through a **funding/liquidity/leverage channel** rather than an **earnings/demand channel**, the *first thing to reprice is not the cheapest or most cyclical stock — it is the most crowded and most leveraged book*. This is a second-order point that distinguishes funding shocks from recessions: the sell order is driven by margin and collateral mechanics, not by a revised view of cash flows, so the cross-sectional pattern is "sell what is liquid and what is owned," not "sell what is economically exposed."

**Analogue 1 — August 2007 "Quant Quake" (the cleanest funding-channel factor event).** Khandani and Lo document that quantitative equity market-neutral funds suffered "unprecedented losses" from Aug 6–9, 2007, with month-to-date losses of −5% to −30% for some of the most consistently profitable funds, followed by a sharp partial reversal on Aug 10 ([Khandani & Lo, MIT working paper](https://web.mit.edu/Alo/www/Papers/august07.pdf)) — **well-established**. Critically, the factors that broke were *value and the classic fundamental long/short factors* (book-to-market, earnings-to-price, cash-flow-to-market), which had been quietly bleeding since July, while *price- and earnings-momentum factors actually rose* into the event ([ScienceDirect summary of Khandani & Lo](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1386418110000261)) — **well-established**. The authors' "Unwind Hypothesis" — that a multi-strategy desk liquidated to meet margin calls from a deteriorating credit (subprime) book, forcing similar books to deleverage — is the canonical model for how a *credit/funding* stress transmits into *equity factor* space even when equity fundamentals are unchanged ([Khandani & Lo](https://web.mit.edu/Alo/www/Papers/august07.pdf)) — **well-established**. The lesson for a durable desk: in a funding shock, the *value factor and crowded long/short books* reprice first and most violently, and the move is **mean-reverting** (the Aug 10 rebound), unlike an earnings-driven value selloff.

**Analogue 2 — 1998 LTCM (liquidity as a priced factor).** The LTCM collapse — a $4.6bn loss in under four months in 1998 — is the archetype of a "flight to liquidity" in which the spread between liquid and illiquid securities is itself the risk factor that gaps ([University of Houston / Bauer case study](https://www.bauer.uh.edu/rsusmel/7386/ltcm-2.htm); [Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/ltcm-near-failure)) — **well-established**. The fourteen-firm $3.625bn recapitalization confirms the systemic-funding nature of the shock ([Federal Reserve History](https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/ltcm-near-failure)) — **well-established**. The equity read-through: in 1998 the repricing ran *liquid-over-illiquid and large-over-small*, and large/small dispersion is the equity expression of a liquidity-factor shock — **contested** as to magnitude (the LTCM literature is overwhelmingly fixed-income), but mechanism-consistent with the Aug-2007 small-cap factor behavior.

**Analogue 3 — March 2020 dash-for-cash.** The FSB's review explicitly separates two phases: a "flight to safety" (late Feb–early March, sell risky/buy safe — the *earnings/recession-channel* response) that then "morphed into" a "dash for cash" (mid-March), in which investors sold *even the safest and most liquid assets* (Treasuries) to raise cash, breaking the normal stock-bond correlation ([FSB Holistic Review of the March Market Turmoil](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) — **well-established**. The diagnostic tell that a shock has crossed from earnings-channel to funding-channel is exactly this: *Treasuries and equities fall together*. In factor terms, March 2020 saw low-volatility and quality outperform on a relative basis as defensives, but in the acute dash-for-cash window **everything correlated to 1** and even quality/low-vol drew down in absolute terms — **contested** (the relative-vs-absolute distinction is where practitioner accounts diverge).

**Analogue 4 — 2022 UK LDI / gilt crisis (a funding shock that barely touched equity factors).** This is the instructive *negative* case. The Bank of England estimates LDI forced-selling accounted for roughly half the gilt-price decline after the Sept 23, 2022 mini-budget, with 30-year gilt yields rising over 100bp in four days and pension LDI leverage spiking from below 2.0 to 2.7 ([Bank Underground / Bank of England](https://bankunderground.co.uk/2024/07/26/what-caused-the-ldi-crisis/); [AllianceBernstein](https://www.alliancebernstein.com/apac/en/institutions/insights/investment-insights/long-run-global-implications-of-the-uks-ldi-crisis.html)) — **well-established**. State Street's portfolio-fragility metrics show the systemic-risk contribution came overwhelmingly from *gilts, corporate bonds, and property — interest-rate assets — not equity* ([State Street, The UK LDI Crisis](https://www.statestreet.com/web/insights/articles/documents/the-uk-ldi-crisis.pdf)) — **well-established**. The framework lesson is a falsifier-grade insight: *a funding shock localized in one collateral chain (long gilts/repo) need not produce a global equity-factor rotation*. A durable desk must therefore ask "which collateral chain?" before assuming a funding shock generalizes.

**Synthesis (the regime rule).** Under a *funding/liquidity* catalyst, the ordering of what reprices first is: (1) crowded long/short and high-leverage books (value-vs-momentum dislocation, Aug-2007 style); (2) the liquid-vs-illiquid / large-vs-small axis (LTCM, dash-for-cash); (3) only *later*, if the funding shock bleeds into a growth scare, the conventional cyclical-vs-defensive and value-vs-growth axes that an *earnings* shock hits first. Energy and geopolitical shocks behave differently again — they hit *sector earnings* directly and so look more like F.2's earnings channel, with energy producers and energy-cost-exposed transports repricing on the *fundamental* axis. **Confidence on the ordering: contested** — the sequencing is well-supported case-by-case but no single study ranks all factor pairs across all six episodes on a common scale.

## F.2 — Earnings-revision transmission

The durable-desk insight here is a **lag asymmetry**: market *prices* react to a funding/geopolitical/oil catalyst within hours, but consensus *EPS revisions* lag by weeks to a full quarter because they are gated by the earnings calendar and by analysts' documented sluggishness. A desk that treats consensus EPS as a real-time signal will systematically mis-time the regime.

**The analyst-sluggishness result is the academic bedrock.** Chan, Jegadeesh & Lakonishok find that "security analysts' earnings forecasts respond sluggishly to past news, especially in the case of stocks with the worst past performance," and that past earnings surprises predict large *drifts* in future returns — i.e., the market itself underreacts and only gradually incorporates information ([Quantpedia summary, citing Chan-Jegadeesh-Lakonishok](https://quantpedia.com/strategies/post-earnings-announcement-effect)) — **well-established**. Post-earnings-announcement drift (PEAD), first documented by Ball & Brown (1968), establishes that abnormal returns continue to drift for *weeks to months* after the news, confirming that the revision/price-incorporation process is slow, not instantaneous ([ScienceDirect, review of PEAD](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214635020303750); [UCLA Anderson, PEAD and Analyst Forecasts](https://www.anderson.ucla.edu/documents/areas/fac/accounting/drift503.pdf)) — **well-established**.

**The lag has been quantified for the oil channel specifically.** Lombard Odier's econometric decomposition estimates that a supply-driven oil shock produces a "rapid drop of about 1.9% [in profits] after three months, persisting to around −1.6% a year after the shock," and that a 50% oil-price increase translates to roughly a 15% earnings decline over a year, with the *direct cost/margin channel (~−2.1% of −2.2%)* dominating the *interest-rate channel (~−0.2%)* ([Lombard Odier, How vulnerable are US corporate earnings to an oil supply shock](https://am.lombardodier.com/insights/2026/april/how-vulnerable-are-us-corporate-earnings-to-an-oil-supply-shock.html)) — **contested** (single-house model, but internally precise and mechanism-consistent). This gives a concrete sector lag map: the *cost/margin* hit shows up at ~3 months (first earnings season after the shock) and persists out to a year.

**Sector ordering of revision speed** (synthesizing the cost-channel mechanics): *Energy producers* reprice on price almost instantly because the catalyst *is* their revenue line — the ECB real-time model finds a 1% oil-spot rise maps near 1:1 into oil-futures and is "passed on (one to one) to gasoline prices within a week," so refiner/E&P revisions move fastest ([ECB Working Paper 2472](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp2472~611f104931.en.pdf)) — **well-established**. *Transports/airlines* are the next-fastest fundamental responders because energy is a direct cost: the ECB finds airline stocks respond −0.45% on impact to an oil supply shock, "substantially stronger than that of aggregate indices" ([ECB WP 2472](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpwps/ecb.wp2472~611f104931.en.pdf)) — **well-established**. *Defense primes* lag most on a geopolitical catalyst because revenue flows through multi-year procurement/budget cycles, so consensus EPS revisions trail the headline by quarters — **anecdotal** (mechanism-plausible, defense-budget-cycle lore; I found no clean study isolating the revision lag). *Insurers* (P&C) revise on a catastrophe/loss-development lag of quarters as claims develop — **anecdotal**. *Consumer* discretionary absorbs an energy shock with a lag via the real-income/gasoline-price channel — the ECB's one-week gasoline pass-through implies the *consumer-spending* hit begins fast but consensus EPS revisions wait for the next print — **contested**. **Net framework rule:** consensus EPS is a *lagging confirmation*, not a *leading signal*; the desk should lead on price, positioning, and credit, and use revisions to confirm regime, expecting the first real EPS evidence ~3 months after a cost-channel catalyst.

## F.3 — Index plumbing: what is FREE vs vendor-only

This subsection is where epistemic honesty matters most, because the plumbing signals are the most heavily *marketed* and the least *verifiable*. The discipline: state which inputs a reader can reconstruct from free public data (so the desk can be quantitative) versus which are vendor estimates (so the desk stays qualitative and avoids false precision).

**Buyback blackout calendars — FREE / reconstructable, but the *effect* is contested.** The blackout window is a derivable calendar object, not a proprietary dataset: NASPP survey data shows 75% of companies close their trading window ≥11 days before fiscal quarter-end (32% at 11–15 days, 23% at >25 days) ([NASPP, 4 Trends in Trading Blackout Periods](https://www.naspp.com/blog/4-Trends-in-Trading-Blackout-Periods)), and European MAR mandates a 30-day pre-results closed period ([PwC, A practical guide to blackout periods](https://www.pwc.ch/en/insights/fs/practical-guide-blackout-periods.html)) — **well-established** that the calendar exists and is free to construct from earnings-date data. But the *market impact* is **contested**: State Street's study finds "no significant difference in returns in any of the periods measured" and "no strong linkage between buybacks and any performance degradation during blackout periods," even reporting *more* positive excess-return months *before* earnings ([State Street, Buyback Blackout Periods Do Not Negatively Impact Performance](https://www.ssga.com/library-content/pdfs/etf/us/b27-buyback-blackout-periods-do-not-negatively-impact-performance.pdf)). So the desk can publish the *calendar* quantitatively but must treat the *directional effect* as a contested qualitative overlay.

**CTA / trend-following and vol-control positioning — VENDOR-ONLY estimates; keep qualitative.** The *mechanism* is well-documented and public: CTAs build positions via moving-average signals and scale by volatility targeting, mechanically selling into downtrends and reducing size in choppy markets ([BNP Paribas, Know the essentials of trend following](https://wealthmanagement.bnpparibas/en/insights/market-strategy/trend-following-2024.html); [Return Stacked, Managed Futures Trend Following](https://www.returnstacked.com/managed-futures-trend-following/)) — **well-established** mechanism. Aggregate industry AUM is sourced from vendors like BarclayHedge ([Graham Capital, Trend-Following Primer](https://www.grahamcapital.com/blog/trend-following-primer/)) — **well-established** that the AUM exists, but the *specific positioning estimates* ("$30bn of S&P selling if SPX breaks X") are proprietary sell-side models (Nomura, Goldman, Deutsche Bank, UBS) with no free equivalent. **Framework rule: positioning de-grossing magnitudes are vendor-only and must stay qualitative** — the desk can describe the trigger logic (vol-target de-grossing on a vol spike) but should not publish point estimates it cannot source.

**0DTE / gamma exposure (GEX) — MECHANISM free, the *number* vendor-only.** The growth is documented from free sources: 0DTE options reached over 40% of SPX options volume by mid-2023 and a record ~56% share by Feb 2025, up from <17% in 2020 ([Technical Indicators That Sort Of Work, GEX & SPX 0DTE](https://technicalindicatorsthatsortofwork.com/blog/indications/gamma-exposure-gex-and-its-application-to-spx-0dte-options-trading)) — **well-established**. The dealer-hedging mechanism (positive GEX → counter-trend, vol-dampening, "pinning"; negative GEX → pro-trend, vol-amplifying) is public and methodologically transparent ([SpotGamma, Gamma Exposure (GEX)](https://spotgamma.com/gamma-exposure-gex/)) — **well-established**. *But the computed GEX level and "gamma flip" require a dealer-positioning assumption* (typically "dealers short puts, long calls") that is a **simplification**, and the resulting flip level is "an estimated level based on models," dynamic intraday, and provider-dependent ([SpotGamma, GEX](https://spotgamma.com/gamma-exposure-gex/); [Technical Indicators That Sort Of Work](https://technicalindicatorsthatsortofwork.com/blog/indications/gamma-exposure-gex-and-its-application-to-spx-0dte-options-trading)) — **contested**. Free inputs (CBOE open interest, strikes) let a desk *approximate* GEX, but the headline number sold by SpotGamma/vendors is not free or canonical. **Rule: cite GEX qualitatively, flag the dealer-positioning assumption, never treat the flip level as a hard line.**

## F.4 — Breadth and internals: the three most diagnostic FREE metrics

I select three because they are orthogonal — they measure *participation*, *concentration*, and *thrust* respectively — and all three are free with stated cadence.

1. **% of S&P 500 above its 200-day moving average (participation).** Free under ticker `$S5TH` on StockCharts (and Barchart), updated end-of-day, with the related `$S5FI` for the 50-day and `$SUPA200R` for the broader S&P 1500 ([StockCharts, %Above 200-day commentary](https://articles.stockcharts.com/article/arthurhill-2025-08-stocks-above-200-day-expands-tech-consolidates-big-banks-lead/); [Barchart, $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH)) — **well-established**. Cadence: **daily, end-of-day**. Diagnostic use: a tape rising on a *falling* %>200dma is narrowing leadership — a classic late-regime tell.

2. **Equal-weight vs cap-weight ratio (concentration).** Free via the RSP/SPY price ratio or the S&P 500 Equal Weight Index ([S&P Dow Jones Indices, S&P 500 Equal Weight](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/equity/sp-500-equal-weight-index/); methodology and history at [SLCG, Equal vs Cap Weighting](https://www.slcg.com/resources/blog/356)). Invesco documents that the top-10 names contributed over 70% of index return in H1 2024 and that the cap-weight P/E (28.0) carried a 28% premium over equal-weight — extreme concentration that historically mean-reverts ([Invesco, Three reasons to consider S&P 500 Equal Weight](https://www.invesco.com/apac/en/institutional/insights/etf/three-compelling-reasons-to-consider-s-and-p-500-equal-weight.html)) — **well-established** as a concentration gauge; **contested** as a timing signal (mean-reversion timing is unreliable). Cadence: **daily** (ETF prices) / **real-time intraday** via RSP:SPY.

3. **New highs minus new lows / NYSE net new highs (thrust & deterioration).** Free at WSJ Market Data and Barchart, daily ([WSJ, New 52-Week Highs and Lows](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/stocks/newfiftytwoweekhighsandlows); [Barchart, 52-Week New Highs](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/highs-lows); aggregated free at [MarketInOut](https://www.marketinout.com/chart/market.php?breadth=new-highs-new-lows)) — **well-established**. Cadence: **daily, end-of-day**. Diagnostic use: an index at new highs while *new lows expand* is the single most reliable free divergence warning; conversely a surge in net new highs off a low is a breadth-thrust confirmation.

These three are deliberately *free and end-of-day* so the framework is reproducible by any reader — a durability property in itself. **Confidence on diagnostic value: well-established for divergence detection; contested for precise timing.**

## F.5 — Cross-desk hooks: the three strongest credit→equities lead/lag relationships

The point of this subsection is *reference without duplication*: the equities page should cite the credit desk's signals rather than rebuild them, and should be honest that credit's lead over equities is *real but regime-dependent and often coincident rather than cleanly leading*.

1. **HY OAS level → forward equity/risk returns (valuation channel).** LSEG/FTSE Russell finds tight HY credit spreads are "strongly associated with lower future bond returns," with spread-to-forward-return correlations of 69% (1-year) and 62% (10-year), and that HY spreads "forecasted future bond returns well in the last two decades, notably during periods of contractions and crises" — the GFC, the 2015–16 energy crisis, and COVID 2020 ([LSEG/FTSE Russell, Valuation matters: US high yield and US equities](https://www.lseg.com/content/dam/ftse-russell/en_us/documents/research/valuation-matters-us-high-yield-and-equities.pdf)) — **well-established** as a *valuation/return* relationship; **contested** as a short-horizon *timing lead* (the relationship is strongest at 2–4 year horizons, not days/weeks). Free input: `BAMLH0A0HYM2` on FRED, daily ([FRED, ICE BofA US HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)) — **well-established**, free, daily.

2. **HY OAS as a real-time stress confirmer vs the VIX (the "two barometers must agree" hook).** The strongest *practical* cross-desk rule is requiring credit and equity-vol to confirm each other: a VIX spike *without* a widening of HY OAS is more likely a positioning/gamma event than a fundamental risk repricing — a second-order distinction that maps directly onto F.1's funding-vs-earnings split. CBOE and S&P DJI launched Credit VIX indices (VIXHY, VIXIG) in Oct 2023 explicitly to give "a comparable benchmark index for the credit markets" alongside equity VIX ([CBOE, New Credit VIX Indices](https://ir.cboe.com/news/news-details/2023/Cboe-Global-Markets-and-SP-Dow-Jones-Indices-to-Launch-New-Credit-VIX-Indices-on-October-13/default.aspx); [S&P DJI, CDX/Cboe NA HY Volatility Index](https://www.spglobal.com/spdji/en/indices/fixed-income/cdx-cboe-na-high-yield-1-month-volatility-index-bp-volatility/)) — **well-established** that the instruments exist; the *lead/lag* between credit-vol and equity-vol is **contested** (CDS indices and VIX are tightly co-determined; clean lead is not established). Free input: VIX is free/daily on CBOE; granular Credit VIX history is partly vendor-gated.

3. **VIX term-structure slope → forward equity returns (volatility-risk channel as a credit-adjacent signal).** Academic work finds the VIX-futures term structure works as a *contrarian* market-timing indicator for equities — backwardation (front > back, the stress signature) tends to precede equity recovery, contango precedes weaker forward returns ([SSRN, The relationship between VIX futures term structure and S&P 500 returns](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/Delivery.cfm/SSRN_ID3189502_code591611.pdf?abstractid=3189502&mirid=1)) — **contested** (signal exists but is noisy and regime-dependent). Free input: VIX term structure published daily by CBOE ([CBOE, VIX Term Structure](https://www.cboe.com/tradable-products/vix/term-structure/)) — **well-established**, free. **Framework rule:** the equities page should treat credit (HY OAS) as the *fundamental* confirmer and vol term-structure as the *stress-state* confirmer, and only call a "fundamental risk-off regime" when both move with equities — otherwise label it a positioning event.

## F.6 — What would falsify the equities framework

A framework that cannot be falsified is not durable — it is unaccountable. Assume the desk's central regime read is some version of: *"the current shock is a funding/liquidity event (mechanical, mean-reverting) rather than an earnings/recession event, so dislocations in crowded factors and breadth should reverse and credit should confirm benign fundamentals."* Here are five concrete, observable, dated falsifiers — each phrased so that its occurrence would force the desk to abandon the funding-channel read in favor of an earnings/recession read.

1. **HY OAS breaks and *stays* above ~600–700bp for 20+ consecutive trading days** (vs the benign ~275bp regime shown on FRED in mid-2026). A *sustained* (not spike-and-revert) widening is the credit desk telling equities the shock is fundamental, not mechanical — directly falsifying the "credit confirms benign fundamentals" clause ([FRED, ICE BofA US HY OAS](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2)) — **well-established** signal logic. *Observable, dated, daily.*

2. **The Aug-2007 reversal pattern fails to appear within ~5 trading days of a crowded-factor dislocation.** The Unwind Hypothesis predicts a *partial reversal* (the Aug-10-2007 rebound) once forced liquidation exhausts; if value/momentum or large/small dislocations *keep extending* past a week instead of mean-reverting, the funding-channel read is falsified and the move is fundamental ([Khandani & Lo](https://web.mit.edu/Alo/www/Papers/august07.pdf)) — **well-established** as a mechanism; *observable via factor return series, dated to the event.*

3. **Consensus sector EPS is revised down by >5% within the first earnings season (~3 months) after the catalyst, in *cost-insulated* sectors.** The Lombard Odier oil-shock model predicts ~−1.9% aggregate profit at 3 months for a real oil shock ([Lombard Odier](https://am.lombardodier.com/insights/2026/april/how-vulnerable-are-us-corporate-earnings-to-an-oil-supply-shock.html)); if revisions arrive *faster and broader* than the cost-channel mechanics justify (i.e., demand destruction, not just margin), the "contained funding event" read is falsified — **contested** (single-house calibration). *Observable via consensus EPS, dated to earnings calendar.*

4. **Breadth fails to recover: %>200dma (`$S5TH`) stays below ~30% for 20+ trading days while the cap-weight index recovers.** A genuine funding shock that mean-reverts should see *participation broaden* on the rebound; a recovery carried only by a few mega-caps while breadth stays broken signals a deteriorating-fundamentals regime, falsifying the durability read ([Barchart, $S5TH](https://www.barchart.com/stocks/quotes/$S5TH); [StockCharts](https://articles.stockcharts.com/article/arthurhill-2025-08-stocks-above-200-day-expands-tech-consolidates-big-banks-lead/)) — **well-established** signal logic. *Observable, dated, daily.*

5. **The stock-bond correlation flips positive and *stays* positive (Treasuries and equities fall together) for a sustained window.** Per the FSB, this is the precise signature that a shock has crossed from ordinary risk-off into a *systemic dash-for-cash* — if it persists rather than resolving after a liquidity backstop, the "contained, mean-reverting funding event" thesis is falsified and the desk must price a genuine liquidity-spiral/recession regime ([FSB Holistic Review](https://www.fsb.org/uploads/P171120-2.pdf)) — **well-established**. *Observable via daily SPX/UST return correlation, dated.*

**Epistemic closing.** The throughline across all six subsections is a single durability principle: **the framework must classify the *channel* before it classifies the *direction*.** Funding-channel shocks reprice crowded/leveraged factors first and mean-revert (F.1), arrive in prices long before EPS revisions confirm them (F.2), are dominated by mechanical flows that are mostly *vendor-estimated, not measurable* (F.3), and must be cross-checked against breadth (F.4) and credit (F.5) to avoid mistaking a positioning event for a fundamental one. The five falsifiers (F.6) are the discipline that keeps the desk honest — each is a dated, free-data observable that converts the regime read from a narrative into a testable claim. A desk that pre-commits to these falsifiers is durable precisely because it has told the market, in advance, what would make it change its mind.
