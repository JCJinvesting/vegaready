### e) FIMA repo and swap lines

[HARD-H] Eligible transactions are overnight or seven-day term repos, with overnight pricing tied to the Standing Repo Facility minimum bid rate and seven-day pricing at weekly OIS plus 25bp unless changed by the Fed ([Federal Reserve FIMA FAQ](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fima-repo-facility-faqs.htm)).

[HARD-H] Current use is tiny: H.4.1 on 2026-06-11 reports central-bank liquidity swaps at **$28mn** and repurchase agreements with foreign official counterparties at **$1mn** on Wednesday 2026-06-10 ([Federal Reserve H.4.1](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h41/current/)). [HARD-H] Historical swap use was huge: central-bank liquidity swaps peaked near **$450bn** in late May 2020, including **$223bn** by the Bank of Japan and **$145bn** by the ECB ([Fed balance-sheet developments](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/bsd-recent-developments-202008.htm)). [DIR-H] FIMA’s option value is much larger than its observed balance: a $50bn FIMA draw would replace a $50bn market sale with a Fed collateralized repo asset and would remove roughly **$40.5mn/bp** of immediate DV01 from private market absorption. [DIR-H] That is why the lead indicator is not current FIMA outstanding, which is near zero, but the spread between offshore dollar funding stress and the FIMA/SRF administered rate.

### Country mechanisms and net UST effect

[DIR-H] **Japan** is the largest marginal duration risk because it combines a $1.19tn TIC stock, credible intervention history, and a current MOF monthly total that may represent renewed defense pressure. [HARD-H] A $50bn Japan sale is **$40.5mn/bp** of 10-year-equivalent DV01, and the March TIC drop of **$47.7bn** is already one such unit in stock terms ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html), [Federal Reserve H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/)).

[DIR-H] **China** is a structural seller rather than a panic seller, and its loop-back is lower-duration because the market expects gradual diversification rather than emergency execution. [HARD-H] China’s March 2026 holdings of **$652.3bn** were down **$113.1bn y/y**, the largest y/y decline among the named top holders in my scorecard ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

[DIR-H] **India** is the clean substitution story: without FCNR(B), RBI can sell reserves and reduce USTs; with FCNR(B), private NRI dollars enter banks and are swapped to RBI. [HARD-M] The 2026 FCNR(B) reporting points to 3-5-year deposits, RBI hedge-cost absorption, and bank rates moving into the **6-7%** zone, which makes a **$25-50bn** inflow plausible but not yet official ([Mint](https://www.livemint.com/money/personal-finance/rbi-revives-fcnr-b-swap-window-for-nris-can-it-replicate-the-2013-inflow-boom-explained-11781094613743.html), [ET BFSI](https://bfsi.economictimes.indiatimes.com/amp/articles/rbi-forex-swap-windows-expected-to-attract-40-50-billion-in-fy27/131681807)).

[DIR-M] **Gulf oil exporters** are two-sided: high oil receipts normally recycle into dollar assets, but Hormuz closure can disrupt settlement, raise precautionary liquidity, and push shorter-tenor dollar deposits over duration. [HARD-H] Saudi and UAE TIC holdings together were **$263.7bn** in Mar-2026 and fell **$16.6bn m/m**, even though both were up y/y by **$27.8bn** combined ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

[DIR-M] **Korea** is an FX-smoothing and institutional-hedging channel rather than a pure reserve story. [HARD-H] Korea’s TIC holdings were **$136.8bn**, down **$4.1bn m/m** and up **$11.0bn y/y** in Mar-2026 ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

[DIR-M] **Brazil** is more likely to defend through real rates and carry than UST sales. [HARD-H] BCB took Selic to **13.75%** in Aug-2022, while March 2026 TIC shows Brazil at **$168.0bn**, down **$40.4bn y/y** ([Banco Central do Brasil](https://www.bcb.gov.br/en/publications/copomminutes/03082022), [Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

[HARD-H] TCMB’s policy-rate move from **8.5% to 50%** by Mar-2024 shows the “raise own rates, avoid reserve liquidation” mechanism at full scale ([TCMB](https://www.tcmb.gov.tr/wps/wcm/connect/EN/TCMB+EN/Main+Menu/Announcements/Remarks+by+Governor/2024/SpeechG08_02_2024), [TCMB Mar-2024 rate decision](https://www.tcmb.gov.tr/wps/wcm/connect/en/tcmb+en/main+menu/announcements/press+releases/2024/ano2024-14)).

### Net UST demand effect, next six months

[DIR-M] My central six-month net is **+$60bn foreign UST demand**, decomposed as official reserve/FX-defense flows **-$120bn**, private/custodial/basis and pension/insurance flows **+$110bn**, oil-exporter recycling **+$50bn**, and FIMA/swap substitution **+$20bn avoided selling**. [DIR-M] The range is **-$75bn to +$175bn**, because the sign turns on whether Japan/China/Gulf official selling repeats March or whether private UK/Cayman and petrodollar flows absorb it.

[DIR-H] Rate impact under the central estimate is small-to-neutral because +$60bn net buying is about **$49mn/bp** of demand, but the composition is bearish for term premium if official sellers of liquid duration are replaced by leveraged private holders. [DIR-M] Under a stress-sale scenario of **-$200bn** official net selling over six months, 10-year-equivalent DV01 supplied is about **$162mn/bp**, which is large enough to add **15-25bp** to the long end if it coincides with weak auctions, repo haircuts, and basis-trade deleveraging.

[HARD-H] Current backstop usage does **not** show a crisis: FIMA-like foreign-official repo assets are **$1mn**, central-bank swaps are **$28mn**, and foreign official/international account Treasury custody is **$2.633tn** in H.4.1 on 2026-06-10 ([Federal Reserve H.4.1](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h41/current/)). [DIR-H] If FIMA rises from $1mn to $25-50bn while TIC official selling slows, the UST bearish impulse is much lower than if TIC official holdings fall by $25-50bn without FIMA use.

## 6. Layman Anchor Bank + Kit

### Anchor analogy

[DIR-H] Think of a country as a household with a dollar emergency and three wallets: a savings account in U.S. Treasuries, a credit line using those Treasuries as collateral, and relatives abroad who can send dollars. [DIR-H] Selling Treasuries is like selling your house investments to get cash, FIMA is like borrowing against them temporarily, and diaspora deposits are like asking relatives to place money in your bank so you do not have to sell investments.

### Top-5 hardest concepts

1. [DIR-H] **Carry trade**: borrow cheap yen or francs and buy higher-yielding pesos, reais, rupees, or bonds; the danger is that the funding currency jumps and everyone runs for the exit. [HARD-H] BIS estimated the yen carry at about **¥40tn / $250bn** before the August 2024 event ([BIS Bulletin 90](https://www.bis.org/publ/bisbull90.pdf)).
2. [DIR-H] **FX intervention**: the central bank sells dollars and buys its own currency; if the dollars come from Treasuries, U.S. yields can rise because more Treasuries hit the market. [HARD-H] Japan sold dollars and bought yen in verified ¥9.7885tn Apr-May 2024 operations ([Japan MOF](https://www.mof.go.jp/english/policy/international_policy/reference/feio/quarter/2024_2Qe.html)).
3. [DIR-H] **Diaspora deposits**: overseas citizens place dollar deposits at home-country banks; the central bank receives dollar liquidity without dumping reserves. [HARD-H] India’s RIB and IMD raised **$4.2bn** and **$5.5bn** respectively ([IMF](https://www.elibrary.imf.org/view/journals/001/2004/048/article-A001-en.xml)).
4. [DIR-H] **Swap lines / FIMA**: the Fed lends dollars to foreign central banks directly or against Treasuries, reducing forced selling. [HARD-H] Swap lines peaked near **$450bn** in late May 2020 ([Fed balance-sheet developments](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/bsd-recent-developments-202008.htm)).
5. [DIR-H] **Selling USTs raises mortgage rates**: mortgage rates price off Treasury yields plus mortgage spreads, so forced foreign selling can lift the Treasury base rate even if the Fed does not hike. [HARD-H] A $50bn 10-year-equivalent sale is roughly **$40.5mn/bp** of DV01 that must be absorbed by someone else, using the H.15 10-year yield and an 8.1 duration assumption ([Federal Reserve H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/)).

### Top-3 misconceptions

[DIR-H] Misconception 1: “China or Japan selling Treasuries automatically crashes the market”; reality is that size, maturity, speed, custody, Fed backstops, and private buying determine the rate effect. [HARD-H] March 2026 had a **$138.4bn** drop in total foreign holdings but the y/y total was still up **$294.5bn** ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

[DIR-H] Misconception 2: “FIMA is QE for foreigners”; reality is that FIMA is collateralized temporary dollar liquidity against Treasuries and is priced as a backstop. [HARD-H] The Fed says FIMA transactions are fully collateralized by Treasuries and usually priced above private repo rates in normal markets ([Federal Reserve FIMA FAQ](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fima-repo-facility-faqs.htm)).

[DIR-H] Misconception 3: “Gold buying means immediate UST dumping”; reality is that gold accumulation is usually gradual reserve allocation and only becomes an acute UST-sale issue when combined with FX intervention or reserve drawdown. [HARD-H] WGC reports a **29t/month** 36-month official-sector gold purchase average, which is steady-flow not a one-day liquidation ([World Gold Council](https://www.gold.org/goldhub/gold-focus/2026/06/central-bank-gold-statistics-central-banks-resume-net-buying-april)).

## 7. Council & Honest Gaps

### Disagreement camps

[DIR-H] **Bearish foreign-flow camp** argues that March TIC official selling, Japan intervention risk, China’s y/y decline, and gold diversification make foreign official demand a negative term-premium impulse. [HARD-H] The strongest hard evidence is the **$108.7bn m/m** decline in foreign-official Treasury holdings in March 2026 and China’s **$113.1bn y/y** decline ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

[DIR-H] **Absorption/backstop camp** argues that FIMA, swap lines, diaspora deposits, oil recycling, and private basis/custody demand prevent official selling from translating one-for-one into higher U.S. yields. [HARD-H] The strongest hard evidence is that swap lines once absorbed nearly **$450bn** of dollar demand in 2020 and FIMA is explicitly an alternative to UST sales ([Fed balance-sheet developments](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/bsd-recent-developments-202008.htm), [Federal Reserve FIMA FAQ](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fima-repo-facility-faqs.htm)).

[DIR-M] **Private-fragility camp** splits the difference: private UK/Cayman demand supports USTs until repo/basis leverage unwinds, at which point the same channel becomes a seller. [HARD-H] UK and Cayman together added **$46.0bn m/m** in March 2026, while Japan and China together fell **$88.7bn m/m** ([Treasury TIC](https://www.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/tic/Documents/slt_table5.html)).

### No-free-feed / blocked metrics

- [DIR-H] Exact latest COFER currency amounts/shares were not programmatically accessible from the IMF page in this run; the page gave methodology and reporter count but not the numeric table ([IMF COFER dataset page](https://data.imf.org/en?sk=e6a5f467-c14b-4aa8-9f6d-5a09ec4e62a4)).

- [DIR-M] Hedge-fund basis-trade gross/net exposure, repo haircuts by counterparty, and real-time foreign official selling are no-free-feed or supervisory-market data, not a reliable public feed.
- [DIR-M] Saudi/UAE/Kuwait actual SWF UST ownership is materially obscured by custodial booking, external managers, and TIC attribution limits.
- [DIR-M] India 2026 FCNR(B) realized inflow and official RBI swap take-up were not yet observed in a free official time series during this run.

[DIR-H] I would **not** present “17% for 3 years” as a verified FCNR(B) deposit rate. [HARD-M] The reported 2026 bank card rates I found are around **6-7.1%** for USD FCNR(B) deposits, and a separate market estimate says leveraged NRI investors could earn **15-26%** on deployed capital, which makes **17%** plausible only as a leveraged return-on-capital figure, not an unlevered deposit yield ([ET BFSI bank-rate story](https://bfsi.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/financial-services/sbi-icici-other-banks-hike-fcnr-deposit-rates/131656885), [ET BFSI inflow estimate](https://bfsi.economictimes.indiatimes.com/amp/articles/rbi-forex-swap-windows-expected-to-attract-40-50-billion-in-fy27/131681807)).
