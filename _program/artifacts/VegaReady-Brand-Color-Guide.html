02 · The operating system

Tiers, layers, ratios, transitions

Tiers

T1 core anchors — first choice, always. T2 structural — only when T1 doesn't fit, document why. T3 behavioural — one purpose, never full backgrounds, never stacked. Utility — the neutral connective tissue.

The 5 layers + coverage

Canvas 70–100% · Overlay/Surface 20–40% · Structural 15–30% · Text — · CTA/Accent 1–5%. Coverage discipline is the anti-distraction mechanism.

Transitions

Tones enter/exit through neutrals. Never fear ↔ hope adjacency. No same-emotion stacking ("optimism → optimism = saturation"). Risk quarantined.

Contrast floor

4.5:1 body text (WCAG 2.1, JCJ standard). White on darks, charcoal on lights, never light-on-light. Every demo below is live-scored.

11 · Adjacency & sequencing

How sections may follow each other

Neutrals are the connective tissue

Coloured bands are entered and exited through canvas/surface neutrals — the "Modal Gray primer" pattern. Canvas = the universal reset.

Never fear ↔ hope adjacency

Risk never sits against optimism in either direction ("optimism whiplash" · "hope → legal alarm collapses tone"). Route through neutral explanation.

No same-emotion stacking

"Optimism → optimism = saturation" · "double risk = allocator exit" · "interactive → interactive = overload." One accent moment per view; one hero figure per view.

Risk is quarantined

Alert red: live alerts and realized losses only, neutral-framed, ≤10%, never animated, never decorative — your Crimson laws verbatim. Latent risk is grey so the hypothetical never wears the alarm.

Muted at scale, saturated at point

Large areas: muted/deepened tones. Saturated colour: small attention surfaces, matched to the tone of the content. The web form of "Tier 3 never full background."

Gradients are behavioural, rare

2 stops · L→R · fade to neutral · one per view · meaning a tone transition (structure → action), never decoration. See §07 for the sanctioned set.
