Part I: High Net Worth Individuals (H1-H7)
H1: Traditional Non-Crypto HNWI
Persona Profile Reminder: Successful in traditional assets, crypto-skeptical, needs translation not education, fears looking foolish, responds to safety signals.
Friction 1: "I Don't Understand This Space"
Surface Signal: "Crypto is too complicated for me" / "I stick to what I know"
Hidden Monologue: "I've been successful without this. Admitting I don't understand it threatens my identity as a sophisticated investor. But I also fear missing something important."
Tone Calibration: Peer validation, zero condescension, bridge-building from their expertise
Strategic Reframe: "You know, that's exactly what the smartest traditional investors say initially. Here's the thing - you don't need to understand blockchain any more than you need to understand SWIFT to wire money. What you already understand - risk management, diversification, momentum - that's what actually matters here. We're just applying institutional discipline to a new asset class. Think of it like when commodities futures became accessible in the 70s - same principles, new underlying."
Tactical Truth Surfacing: "What's interesting is you've probably navigated bigger complexity before - when you first invested in [reference their industry], what helped you get comfortable despite not knowing every detail?"
Expected Reaction Pattern: Relief at not needing technical knowledge → Curiosity about familiar frameworks → Questions about risk management
Progressive Path:
Connect to their existing expertise
Show institutional infrastructure
Provide "translator" materials
Focus on risk metrics they understand
Never mention DeFi, Web3, or complex crypto terms
Friction 2: "My Advisor Doesn't Recommend Crypto"
Surface Signal: "I need to check with my wealth manager" / "My team handles alternatives"
Hidden Monologue: "I trust my advisor more than you. They've protected my wealth for years. If they're not recommending this, there must be a reason. But what if they're behind the curve?"
Tone Calibration: Respectful of existing relationships, collaborative not competitive
Strategic Reframe: "Your advisor sounds like they've done a great job protecting your wealth - that relationship is invaluable. What we're finding is that many traditional advisors are just now getting comfortable with institutional-grade digital asset exposure. We actually created a one-page framework specifically for advisors that shows how this fits into traditional portfolio construction. Would it be helpful if I sent that for your next review with them? We're happy to have a three-way call if that would help."
Tactical Truth Surfacing: "Out of curiosity, how did your advisor initially react to alternatives like private equity or hedge funds? Sometimes the best advisors are cautious with new categories until the infrastructure catches up."
Expected Reaction Pattern: Defensiveness about advisor relationship → Curiosity about advisor materials → Request for "safe to forward" content
Progressive Path:
Validate advisor relationship
Position as advisor-friendly
Provide advisor education materials
Offer to educate advisor directly
Never compete with advisor influence
Friction 3: "The Volatility Is Crazy"
Surface Signal: "Bitcoin swings 20% in a day" / "Too much risk for my age"
Hidden Monologue: "I've worked too hard to gamble now. One bad investment could undo decades of discipline. But the returns are compelling if true."
Tone Calibration: Steady, mathematical, risk-focused before return-focused
Strategic Reframe: "You're absolutely right about the volatility - Bitcoin alone would be irresponsible for most portfolios. That's why we don't buy and hold. Our system actually uses that volatility as a signal to reduce exposure. When Bitcoin fell 77%, we were only down 22% because the system stepped aside. We're not trying to ride the volatility - we're trying to navigate around it. Think of it like having a pilot who knows when not to fly."
Tactical Truth Surfacing: "What's your framework for volatility in your current portfolio? I'm curious because many successful investors actually have more volatility than they realize - it's just hidden in different ways."
Expected Reaction Pattern: Surprise at drawdown comparison → Interest in risk management → Questions about capital preservation
Progressive Path:
Acknowledge volatility reality
Show systematic risk controls
Compare to their existing portfolio volatility
Focus on drawdown protection
Provide volatility-adjusted return metrics
Friction 4: "What If It Goes to Zero?"
Surface Signal: "Tulips went to zero" / "It's not backed by anything"
Hidden Monologue: "I remember the dot-com crash. I've seen 'new paradigms' destroy wealth. This feels like another bubble that smart money avoids."
Tone Calibration: Historical perspective, patient, evidence-based
Strategic Reframe: "That's the right question to ask - it's the same one institutions asked before Bitcoin ETFs got approved. Here's what's changed: we now have BlackRock, Fidelity, and traditional custody infrastructure involved. Could Bitcoin go to zero? Theoretically, yes. But so could any asset - remember when people thought GE was invincible? What we do is different - we're not making a directional bet on crypto. We're trading momentum patterns. If the trend breaks, we exit. We're not believers - we're systematic traders."
Tactical Truth Surfacing: "When you think about your portfolio, how do you typically protect against catastrophic loss in any single position? Because that framework probably applies here too."
Expected Reaction Pattern: Appreciation for honest risk acknowledgment → Interest in systematic approach → Questions about exit strategies
Progressive Path:
Validate the concern as intelligent
Show institutional adoption as risk mitigation
Emphasize systematic vs. buy-and-hold
Focus on risk management process
Never argue about intrinsic value

Surface Signal: "This is for younger investors" / "I'm focused on preservation now"

They typically allocate 2-3% as a portfolio diversifier, not a core position. It's actually the younger investors who tend to over-allocate. The wisdom of preservation with small strategic bets - that's exactly the right approach at any age."
Tactical Truth Surfacing: "How do you think about the portfolio you'll eventually transfer? Are there conversations with the next generation about their perspective on digital assets?"
Expected Reaction Pattern: Surprise at peer age involvement → Interest in small allocation → Questions about intergenerational wealth
Progressive Path:
Normalize their age cohort participating
Focus on small, strategic allocation
Connect to legacy planning
Emphasize preservation within innovation
Reference other mature investors
