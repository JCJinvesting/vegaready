# VegaReady — Perplexity research prompt (Credit Desk)

ROLE & CONTEXT
You are a senior fixed-income and information-architecture consultant. I'm designing the public CREDIT DESK page of an institutional, dual-audience cross-asset market-intelligence website (a research-grade public Koyfin/Bloomberg-style markets page). The site reads markets as a "regime" and overlays live event-catalysts (a geopolitical conflict, an OPEC+ decision, a Fed pivot) on top of a DURABLE, catalyst-neutral framework — so the page must make sense whether or not any catalyst is active. Audience is dual: (1) institutional credit analysts/PMs who want density, and (2) sophisticated non-specialists who need plain language. Governing principle: honesty over false precision — every claim is source-tiered and scenario-aware, correlation ≠ causation, and competing drivers plus a named residual are always shown.

Answer ALL parts. Use tables for the per-topic and per-cohort parts. Cite a reputable/primary source for every factual claim and every data-source recommendation. End with a numbered source list.

PART A — PAGE ARCHITECTURE
A1. The ideal top-to-bottom section hierarchy for an institutional credit desk serving both audiences — and why each section earns its place.
A2. What the best products (Bloomberg, Koyfin, sell-side credit-strategy notes, FT/Economist) put on a credit overview — what to borrow and what to avoid.
A3. Progressive disclosure (30-second → 3-minute → deep analyst layer) and a plain-language ⇄ analyst toggle over the SAME content.

PART B — PER-TOPIC CONTENT (the durable, catalyst-neutral toolkit)
For EACH topic give: what to include, the specific indicators an analyst tracks, how best to present it, must-have vs nice-to-have, its leading-indicator value, and the best FREE/PUBLIC data sources with update cadence:
- Spread regime (IG/HY OAS levels vs history, direction, vol, tight/cheap)
- Internal structure (IG vs HY, ratings migration, fallen angels/rising stars, distress ratio, CCC share)
- Valuation & fair value (OAS vs default-implied spread, spread-per-turn-of-leverage, breakevens)
- Fundamental driver (default rate & recovery, net leverage, interest coverage, the maturity wall, earnings link)
- Cohort rotation (by ratings bucket and by sector)
- Credit factors/styles (carry, quality, spread-duration)
- Positioning & flows (fund flows, CDS positioning, primary issuance, dealer inventories)
- Cross-asset linkages (credit ↔ equities, credit ↔ rates/curve, credit ↔ the dollar)

PART C — PER-COHORT CONTENT
For EACH ratings bucket (IG / BBB / BB / B / CCC) AND the most credit-sensitive sectors (banks/financials, energy, real estate, leveraged tech/telecom, retail) give a compact desk profile: what structurally drives spreads, sensitivity to rates/cycle/commodities/USD, the 3–5 KPIs to track, leading indicators, common ETF/index proxies (e.g. LQD, HYG, JNK, EMB, CDX IG/HY), classic risks, and when each leads vs lags. Present as a table.

PART D — CATALYST OVERLAYS
How to present a live event/catalyst's effect on credit WITHOUT it dominating the durable framework: event-overlay best practices, scenario-conditional winners/losers (sovereign vs corporate, IG vs HY), supporting multiple simultaneous catalysts, and avoiding recency bias. Include the sovereign/funding channel (EM sovereign spreads/EMBI, dollar-funding/cross-currency basis, MMF flows, sukuk, IMF, swap lines).

PART E — EPISTEMIC & DATA DISCIPLINE
E1. Confidence labeling (source tiers; data vs attribution confidence; observed vs interpretation; value types baseline/current/nowcast/forecast/scenario).
E2. Reliable free/public sources for every indicator (FRED OAS series, ICI flows, Moody's/S&P default & ratings, CFTC, MOVE, Damodaran) with cadence and redistribution caveats.

PART F — EXTENSIBILITY
Briefly: how this credit-desk template generalizes to rates, FX, commodities and crypto desks.

OUTPUT: clear headers; tables for Parts B and C; a numbered, reputable source list at the end.
